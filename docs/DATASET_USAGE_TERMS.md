## PROFESSIONAL CONSULTATION REQUIRED

### ⚠️ IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTICE

**This data should NOT be used as the sole basis for operational decisions.**

You MUST:
- ✅ Consult with qualified laser safety professionals before implementing any procedures
- ✅ Follow all applicable safety regulations and standards, including:
  - ANSI Z136 series (American National Standards for Safe Use of Lasers)
  - IEC 60825 series (Safety of Laser Products)
  - OSHA regulations (Occupational Safety and Health Administration)
  - Local and regional safety requirements
- ✅ Conduct proper risk assessments for your specific application
- ✅ Implement appropriate engineering controls and safety procedures
- ✅ Provide adequate training to all personnel
- ✅ Use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
- ✅ Verify all parameters with qualified testing before production use

### Laser Safety Hazards

Laser cleaning equipment can present serious hazards:
- **Eye Damage** - Permanent vision loss from direct or reflected laser exposure
- **Skin Burns** - Thermal injury from laser contact
- **Fire Hazards** - Ignition of flammable materials or fumes
- **Toxic Fumes** - Release of harmful substances during cleaning
- **Electrical Hazards** - High-voltage equipment risks

**Never operate laser equipment without proper training and safety measures.**
