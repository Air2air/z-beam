# Technical Guide to Laser Cleaning Alumina Surfaces in Industrial Applications  

## 1. Introduction to Alumina  
Alumina (Al₂O₃), a high-purity ceramic with 99.5% aluminum oxide content, is widely used in industries requiring thermal shock resistance (melting point: 2072°C) and contaminant-free surfaces. Its density (3.95 g/cm³) and thermal conductivity (30 W/m·K) make it ideal for laser cleaning, a non-abrasive method that preserves structural integrity.  

## 2. Technical Specifications and Capabilities  
Optimal laser cleaning of Alumina employs a **1064nm ±2nm fiber laser** with adjustable parameters:  
- **Fluence Range**: 0.5–10 J/cm²  
- **Power Range**: 100–1000W (modular)  
- **Pulse Duration**: 10–200ns (prevents microcracking)  
- **Spot Size**: 0.2–3mm diameter  
- **Repetition Rate**: 10–100kHz  

These settings achieve **99.9% purity** (SEM-verified) and **0.1µm surface roughness**, critical for precision applications.  

## 3. Applications and Use Cases  
- **Semiconductor Prep**: Removal of oxide layers at 0.8–1.2 J/cm² fluence (1064nm) for substrate preparation.  
- **Medical Devices**: 20–50ns pulses clean implantable components without compromising biocompatibility (ISO 18562-1 compliant).  
- **Aerospace**: 5–8 J/cm² fluence treats thermal barriers in turbines, maintaining porosity (ASTM F2888).
