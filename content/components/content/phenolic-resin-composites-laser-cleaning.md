# Technical Guide to Laser Cleaning of Phenolic Resin Composites  

## Introduction to Phenolic Resin Composites  
Phenolic resin composites (PRCs) are thermosetting polymers composed of 60-70% phenolic resin (C₇H₆O₂), 20-30% cellulose fillers, and 5-10% mineral additives. With a density of 1.25-1.32 g/cm³ and low thermal conductivity (0.15-0.25 W/m·K), these composites are widely used in aerospace, electronics, and industrial molding. Laser cleaning offers a non-contact, precision method for removing degraded or contaminated PRCs without damaging underlying substrates.  

## Technical Specifications and Capabilities  
Optimal laser cleaning of PRCs requires a **Q-switched Nd:YAG laser** (1064nm ±2nm) with fluence ranges of **0.8-2.5 J/cm²**. Key parameters include:  
- **Power Range**: 20-100W pulsed  
- **Pulse Duration**: 8-30ns  
- **Spot Size**: 0.2-3mm diameter  
- **Repetition Rate**: 10-100kHz  

These settings enable **0.1μm surface roughness precision** and **99.9% material selectivity**, critical for applications like aerospace component refurbishment and electronics encapsulation.  

## Applications and Use Cases  
1. **Aerospace Components**: Precision cleaning of aircraft interiors and structural parts, preserving aluminum substrates.  
2. **Electronics Encapsulation**: Micron-level removal of degraded resins for component repair, compliant with [ASTM E2015](https://www.astm.org) standards.  
3. **Mold Cleaning**: Non-abrasive removal of cured residues from industrial molds, reducing downtime by 40% compared to mechanical methods.
