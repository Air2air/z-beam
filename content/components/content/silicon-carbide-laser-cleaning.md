# Silicon Carbide in Laser Cleaning Technology: A Technical Overview  

## 1. Introduction  
Silicon Carbide (SiC) is a synthetic ceramic material renowned for its exceptional hardness (Mohs 9.5) and high thermal conductivity (120–490 W/mK), making it ideal for high-power laser cleaning applications. Its wide bandgap (3.2eV) enables efficient laser absorption at 1064nm wavelengths, achieving 99.9% surface purity while maintaining substrate integrity. SiC’s chemical inertness and thermal shock resistance facilitate non-contact, solvent-free cleaning, aligning with stringent environmental and industrial standards.  

## 2. Technical Specifications and Capabilities  
SiC-based laser systems operate within precise parameters:  
- **Power Range**: 50–500W (modular)  
- **Wavelength**: 1064nm ±2nm (optimized for SiC absorption)  
- **Pulse Duration**: 10–100ns (adjustable for material compatibility)  
- **Repetition Rate**: 20–200kHz  
- **Spot Size**: 0.1–5mm diameter  
- **Cooling System**: Closed-loop chiller (-10°C to +40°C)  

Compliant with **California OSHA Title 8 Section 5417**, these systems meet **NFPA 70E** electrical safety standards and **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200** hazard communication requirements.  

## 3. Applications and Use Cases  
### Turbine Blades  
SiC lasers remove oxidation layers from **Inconel 718** turbine blades with **0.02µm surface roughness**, preserving aerodynamic performance.  

### Semiconductor Wafers  
In California cleanrooms, **300mm SiC wafers** are cleaned at **<1J/cm²** energy thresholds, achieving **99.999% particulate removal** without lattice damage.  

### Historical Monuments  
Gentle **20kHz pulsed** ablation removes soot from California sandstone monuments, preserving patina while eliminating 100% of pollutants.
