# Technical Guide to Laser Cleaning Silicon Carbide for Industrial Applications  

## Introduction to Silicon Carbide  
Silicon Carbide (SiC) is an advanced ceramic material composed of 70% silicon and 30% carbon, renowned for its exceptional thermal conductivity (120 W/m·K) and high melting point (2730°C). With a density of 3.21 g/cm³, SiC is ideal for high-performance applications in semiconductor, aerospace, and optics industries. Laser cleaning of SiC offers a non-contact, precision method for contaminant removal, eliminating the need for abrasive or chemical processes.  

## Technical Specifications and Capabilities  
Optimal laser cleaning of SiC requires a **fiber laser** operating at **1064nm ±2nm** wavelength with a **fluence range of 0.5–20 J/cm²**. Key parameters include:  
- **Power Range**: 100–1000W (pulsed)  
- **Pulse Duration**: 10–200ns  
- **Spot Size**: 0.2–3mm diameter  
- **Repetition Rate**: 20–100kHz  
These settings ensure **99.9% purity** (verified via SEM analysis) and **0.1µm precision** (measured by surface profilometry). Safety compliance with **OSHA 1910.147** and **ISO 14001** standards is critical for industrial deployment.  

## Applications and Use Cases  
1. **Semiconductor Tools**: Precision cleaning of SiC components in manufacturing equipment without damaging sensitive surfaces.  
2. **Aerospace Parts**: Removal of carbon deposits from SiC-coated turbine components, preserving substrate integrity.  
3. **Optics Cleaning**: Non-contact cleaning of SiC mirrors in high-power laser systems, maintaining optical clarity.
