## Laser Cleaning Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer (CFRP) - Technical Guide  

## Introduction  
Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer (CFRP) is a high-performance composite material widely used in aerospace, automotive, and renewable energy industries due to its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio and durability. Laser cleaning has emerged as a precise, non-contact method for surface preparation, contaminant removal, and refurbishment of CFRP components without damaging the underlying fibers.  

## Material Composition & Properties  
CFRP consists of:  
- **60-70% carbon fiber** (structural reinforcement, `C`)  
- **30-40% epoxy resin** (polymer matrix, `(C₁₁H₁₂O₃)ₙ`)  
- **Optional 5-15% polyimide** (high-temperature resistance, `(C₂₂H₁₀N₂O₅)ₙ`)  

**Key Properties:**  
| Property               | Value Range               |  
|------------------------|---------------------------|  
| Density                | 1.5–1.6 g/cm³             |  
| Melting Point (Matrix) | 350–400°C                 |  
| Hardness               | 70–90 Shore D             |  
| Thermal Conductivity   | 5–50 W/m·K (anisotropic)  |  

## Optimal Laser Parameters  
For effective CFRP laser cleaning:  
- **Wavelength:** **1064 nm** (fundamental) or 532 nm (optional)  
- **Fluence Range:** **0.5–5 J/cm²** (avoids thermal damage)  
- **Pulse Duration:** 10–200 ns  
- **Spot Size:** 50–200 µm  
- **Repetition Rate:** 20–100 kHz  

**Critical Thresholds:**  
- **Thermal Damage Threshold:** <5 J/cm² (prevents matrix degradation)  
- **Ablation Depth Control:** 0.1–0.3 µm/pulse  

## Applications  
1. **Aerospace Component Refurbishment**  
   - Removes oxidation/adhesive residues at **2.5 J/cm²** (100 ns pulses).  
   - Achieves **99.7% contaminant removal** (XPS-verified).  

2. **Automotive Composite Repair**  
   - Selective ablation with **30W pulsed fiber lasers** (50 µm spot size).  

3. **Wind Turbine Blade Maintenance**  
   - Large-area cleaning at **1.2 J/cm²** (2.5 m²/hour throughput).  

## Environmental & Industrial Benefits  
- **98% solvent reduction** vs. chemical methods.  
- **35–40% energy savings** vs. mechanical abrasion.  
- **90% less waste** than sandblasting.
