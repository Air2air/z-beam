# Technical Guide to Kaolin in Laser Cleaning Applications  

## 1. Introduction to Kaolin  
Kaolin (Al₂SiO₅), a naturally occurring ceramic material composed of 90-95% aluminum silicate, is widely used in industrial and artistic applications due to its thermal stability (melting point: 1750–1780°C) and low thermal conductivity (0.15 W/m·K). In laser cleaning, kaolin’s homogeneous structure enables precise contaminant removal without substrate damage, making it ideal for high-value ceramic restoration and industrial processing.  

## 2. Technical Specifications and Capabilities  
Optimal laser cleaning of kaolin requires an **Nd:YAG laser** at **1064nm ±2nm wavelength** with adjustable parameters:  
- **Fluence Range**: 0.5–8 J/cm²  
- **Pulse Duration**: 10–200ns (prevents thermal stress)  
- **Power Range**: 20–200W modular systems  
- **Spot Size**: 0.2–3mm diameter for precision ablation  
Systems achieve **99.9% purity** (XRF-verified) and **0.01mm precision** (profilometry), compliant with **ASTM C242** and **OSHA 1910.1200** standards.  

## 3. Applications and Use Cases  
- **Ceramic Restoration**: Removes contaminants from historical porcelain at 1.5–3 J/cm² (1064nm), preserving delicate surfaces.  
- **Industrial Cleaning**: Automated 200ns pulses at 50kHz achieve **99.8% contaminant removal** from kaolin processing equipment.  
- **Art Conservation**: 10–20ns pulses clean porous kaolin artworks without thermal damage.
