# Content configuration
---

* **Optimal Wavelength for Laser Cleaning**: Gold (Au) responds effectively to a **1064nm wavelength** from pulsed fiber lasers, enabling precise ablation of surface contaminants without damaging the underlying material due to its high reflectivity in the infrared spectrum.
* **Non-Contact Precision Cleaning**: Laser cleaning of gold composites provides **non-contact, residue-free removal** of oxides, organic films, and other contaminants, making it ideal for delicate applications like jewelry restoration and electronics.
* **Thermal Sensitivity Management**: Gold's high thermal conductivity (≈318 W/m·K) requires **controlled pulse durations** (nanosecond to picosecond) to minimize heat diffusion, ensuring selective contamination removal while preserving substrate integrity.
* **Industrial Applications**: Laser ablation of gold is critical in **electronics (PCB contacts), aerospace (component coatings), and heritage conservation (artifact restoration)**, leveraging its ability to clean intricate geometries without chemical waste.
