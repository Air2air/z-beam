* **Low Melting Point (29.76°C)**: Gallium's near-room-temperature melting point makes it highly sensitive to thermal input, requiring precise laser parameters (e.g., low pulse energy, short dwell time) to avoid unintended liquefaction during laser cleaning.
* **High Reflectivity at 1064nm**: Gallium's reflective properties at common fiber laser wavelengths (e.g., 1064nm) necessitate optimized beam delivery and pulse shaping to ensure effective energy absorption for contamination removal without excessive scattering.
* **Non-Contact Cleaning Advantage**: Laser ablation of gallium composites eliminates mechanical stress, preventing surface deformation or oxidation, which is critical for delicate applications like semiconductor or optical component restoration.
* **Industrial Parameter Constraints**: Optimal cleaning requires pulsed fiber lasers with controlled fluence (typically <1 J/cm²) and repetition rates (<100 kHz) to balance contamination removal with minimal thermal diffusion, preserving substrate integrity.
