- **[APPLICATIONS]** Laser cleaning of Silicon Carbide is extensively employed within the European semiconductor sector for the precise removal of photoresist and particulate contamination from wafers prior to packaging. This non-abrasive technique is also vital for restoring high-value components in the power electronics and aerospace industries, aligning with stringent EU manufacturing quality directives.
- **[PARAMETERS]** Optimal cleaning leverages a nanosecond-pulsed fiber laser at a 1064 nm wavelength, as its high peak power effectively ablates contaminants without damaging the SiC's inherent lattice structure. A fluence range of 0.5 to 1.5 J/cm² is typically maintained to ensure complete contaminant removal while staying well below the material's ablation threshold.
- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** This dry process completely eliminates the need for chemical solvents, drastically reducing hazardous waste generation and supporting the goals of the European Green Deal for sustainable manufacturing. The significant reduction in water consumption compared to traditional wet cleaning methods also offers a substantial ecological advantage for water-conscious regional operations.
- **[SAFETY]** Strict adherence to IEC 60825-1 is mandatory, requiring complete laser enclosure with safety interlocks and the use of wavelength-specific protective eyewear for all operating personnel. Proper fume extraction systems are equally critical to capture any nanoparticles generated during the ablation process, ensuring a safe work environment.
- **[CHALLENGES]** The primary difficulty involves meticulously controlling thermal input to prevent any micro-cracking or phase alterations on the SiC surface, which would compromise the component's structural integrity. This is mitigated through sophisticated real-time monitoring systems that adjust laser parameters on-the-fly to maintain process stability and output quality.
