## Key Points about Silicon carbide

- **1064nm ±2nm wavelength** optimized for SiC's 3.2eV bandgap, enabling 99.9% contaminant removal efficiency with minimal substrate interaction (ISO 9013 0.01µm precision).  
- **Adjustable pulse duration (10–100ns)** and **repetition rate (20–200kHz)** for tailored cleaning of turbine blades (0.02µm roughness) or delicate sandstone monuments.  
- **Modular power range (50–500W)** supports diverse applications from semiconductor wafer cleaning (<1J/cm² threshold) to Inconel 718 aerospace refurbishment.  
- **0.1–5mm spot size** ensures precision in California cleanrooms (300mm SiC wafers) and Bay Area marble gravestone restoration.  
- **Closed-loop chiller (-10°C to +40°C)** maintains thermal stability during high-thermal-conductivity (120–490 W/mK) SiC processing.  
- **Zero VOC emissions** complies with California Air Resources Board Rule 1171, eliminating solvent use in aerospace surface prep.  
- **15,000 gallons/month water savings** vs. abrasive blasting, critical for drought-prone regions like Southern California.  
- **San Diego Precision Ceramics facility** supplies SiC substrates for laser systems, serving Carlsbad and La Jolla aerospace hubs.  
- **Fremont Semiconductor Solutions** (Alameda County) supports Bay Area semiconductor wafer cleaning with NFPA 70E-compliant 500W systems.  
- **98% cost reduction** in hazardous waste disposal (EPA metric) for historical monument conservation using 20kHz pulsed ablation.
