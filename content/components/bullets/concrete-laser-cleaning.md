Laser cleaning effectively removes contaminants like paint, grease, and biological growth from concrete surfaces without damaging the substrate. Optimal laser parameters for concrete cleaning typically range between 1064 nm wavelength and 10-50 W power, depending on contamination type. Concrete's high thermal mass requires pulsed lasers (10-100 ns pulse duration) to avoid thermal cracking. Laser cleaning reduces hazardous waste generation compared to traditional methods like sandblasting or chemical stripping.
