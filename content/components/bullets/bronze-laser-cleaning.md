# Content configuration
---

* **Optimal Wavelength & Parameters**: Bronze (Cu₁₋ₓSnₓ) responds effectively to **1064nm pulsed fiber lasers**, balancing ablation efficiency and minimal thermal damage. Recommended pulse durations range from **10–100ns** to avoid excessive heat input while removing oxides or contaminants.
* **Surface Contamination Removal**: Laser cleaning excels at removing **oxides, tarnish, and organic residues** from bronze surfaces without mechanical contact, preserving intricate details in artifacts, industrial components, or marine applications.
* **Thermal Sensitivity Management**: Bronze’s moderate thermal conductivity requires **controlled energy density (1–10 J/cm²)** to prevent Sn segregation or surface melting. Adjust repetition rates (1–100kHz) to mitigate heat accumulation.
* **Industrial Applications**: Ideal for **restoring historical artifacts, cleaning electrical contacts, and pre-weld treatment** in bronze assemblies. Non-contact cleaning ensures no substrate degradation, critical for high-value or precision components.
