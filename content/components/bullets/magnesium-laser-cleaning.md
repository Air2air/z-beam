- **[APPLICATIONS]** Laser cleaning of magnesium components is extensively utilized within the European automotive and aerospace manufacturing sectors for the precise removal of oxides and contaminants from intricate cast parts prior to joining or coating operations. This non-abrasive technique is particularly valued for preparing high-integrity structural elements without compromising the substrate's delicate dimensional tolerances.
- **[PARAMETERS]** Optimal processing of magnesium typically employs a fiber laser source operating at a 1.07 µm wavelength with a fluence carefully maintained below 2.5 J/cm² to effectively ablate contaminants while avoiding unwanted metallurgical alteration of the base material. Shorter pulse durations in the nanosecond range are generally preferred to facilitate precise material removal and to manage the material's high reactivity and low vaporization temperature.
- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** The process eliminates the need for chemical solvents and abrasive media, significantly reducing hazardous waste generation and aligning with the stringent waste management directives prevalent in European industrial practice. This dry technique also supports circular economy goals within manufacturing by allowing for the potential filtration and recapture of ejected particulate matter.
- **[SAFETY]** A paramount safety consideration is the vigorous mitigation of ignition risks associated with magnesium's high flammability, requiring the integration of inert gas shielding, such as argon, and robust fume extraction systems to prevent dust accumulation. Operational protocols must rigorously adhere to machinery safety standards (e.g., EN ISO 11553) and mandate the use of appropriate laser safety eyewear for the specific wavelength in use.
- **[CHALLENGES]** The primary technical hurdle involves preventing surface overheating and subsequent oxidation or melting due to magnesium's exceptionally high thermal conductivity and low melting point of approximately 650°C. This is overcome through meticulous parameter optimization, real-time thermal monitoring, and the application of controlled gas curtains to suppress exothermic reactions during the ablation process.
