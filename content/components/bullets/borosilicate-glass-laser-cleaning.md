* **Material Composition & Properties**: Borosilicate glass (SiO₂·B₂O₃·Na₂O·Al₂O₃) comprises 80.6% SiO₂, 12.6% B₂O₃, 4.2% Na₂O, and 2.6% Al₂O₃, offering low thermal expansion (1.2 W/m·K), high hardness (6.5 Mohs), and chemical resistance. Melting point is 820°C with a density of 2.23 g/cm³.
* **Laser Cleaning Parameters**: Optimal processing requires excimer (248 nm) or Nd:YAG (1064 nm) lasers, with fluence range of 0.5–5 J/cm², pulse duration of 10–100 ns, and spot size of 50–500 µm. Power ranges from 10–200W at repetition rates of 1–100 kHz.
* **Key Applications**: Includes pharmaceutical glassware cleaning (355 nm UV laser at 1.2 J/cm²), optical component preparation (1064 nm at 3.5 J/cm²), and semiconductor equipment maintenance (248 nm excimer at 0.8 J/cm²). Compatible with stainless steel, PTFE, and alumina ceramics.
* **Environmental & Performance Benefits**: Reduces chemical usage by 95%, consumes 40% less energy than thermal methods, and produces 99.9% less waste. Achieves Ra < 0.1 µm surface roughness and >99.9% contaminant removal efficiency at 0.5–2 m²/hour processing speed.
