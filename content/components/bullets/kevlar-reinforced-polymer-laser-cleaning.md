1. **Technical Focus: Material-specific laser parameters** - The optimal laser parameters for cleaning Kevlar-Reinforced Polymer (KRP) involve a 1064 nm wavelength, a fluence range of 0.8-1.5 J/cm², and nanosecond pulse durations to effectively ablate contaminants while preserving the aramid fiber's integrity and the polymer matrix.
2. **Technical Focus: Industrial applications** - Within the local high-tech manufacturing sector, laser cleaning is primarily employed for precision surface preparation of KRP components used in semiconductor handling equipment and advanced electronics enclosures requiring non-abrasive contaminant removal.
3. **Technical Focus: Safety considerations** - Operators must utilize Class 1 enclosure systems with integrated fume extraction, adhering to local occupational safety regulations, as the process can generate fine particulate matter and trace volatiles from the composite's epoxy matrix.
4. **Technical Focus: Environmental benefits** - This dry process eliminates the need for chemical solvents and abrasive media, significantly reducing hazardous waste streams and aligning with the region's stringent industrial waste management protocols for electronics manufacturing.
