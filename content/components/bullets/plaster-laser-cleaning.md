# Content configuration
---

Laser cleaning effectively removes surface contaminants from plaster without damaging the substrate when using appropriate parameters (typically 1064 nm wavelength, 10-50 W power). The porous nature of plaster requires precise control of laser fluence (0.5-2 J/cm²) to prevent subsurface damage. Plaster's calcium sulfate composition can produce sulfur oxides during laser ablation, necessitating proper fume extraction systems. For historic preservation, low-frequency pulsed lasers (20-100 Hz) with spot sizes of 0.5-2 mm provide optimal cleaning precision.
