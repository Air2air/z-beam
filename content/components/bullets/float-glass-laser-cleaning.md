* **Optimal Laser Parameters for Float Glass Cleaning**: Float glass responds effectively to pulsed fiber lasers at 1064nm wavelength, ensuring precise ablation of surface contaminants without substrate damage. Pulse duration and energy must be calibrated to avoid thermal stress or micro-cracking.
* **Non-Contact Cleaning Advantages**: Laser cleaning offers a non-contact, chemical-free method for removing oxides, dust, and organic residues from float glass surfaces, preserving optical clarity and reducing mechanical wear risks associated with traditional methods.
* **Industrial Applications**: Ideal for high-precision industries such as automotive (windshields), architecture (façade panels), and electronics (display glass), where contamination-free surfaces are critical for performance and aesthetics.
* **Thermal and Surface Integrity Considerations**: Controlled laser parameters prevent excessive heat accumulation, maintaining float glass's low thermal expansion properties and ensuring surface restoration without distortion or wavefront aberrations.
