* **Material Composition & Properties**: Fiberglass is a composite of 50–70% silica (SiO₂) and 30–50% polymer matrix (epoxy/PE), with a density of 2.4–2.7 g/cm³ and thermal conductivity of 0.04–0.08 W/m·K. Its silica component has a hardness of 5.5–6.5 Mohs and melts at 1120–1200°C, making it suitable for controlled laser ablation.
* **Optimal Laser Parameters**: Best results achieved with Nd:YAG or fiber lasers at 1064nm (IR) or 355nm (UV), with fluence ranges of 0.5–10 J/cm². Typical settings include 20–200W pulsed power, 10–100ns pulse duration, 50–500μm spot size, and 20–100kHz repetition rate for precise cleaning.
* **Key Applications**: Used in aerospace (radome oxidation removal at 2.5 J/cm²), wind turbine blade maintenance (epoxy residue cleaning at 1.8 J/cm²), and marine restoration (marine growth removal with 30W fiber laser). Compatible with aluminum, carbon fiber, and stainless steel substrates.
* **Environmental & Performance Benefits**: Reduces chemical waste by 95%, lowers energy use by 40% vs. abrasive blasting, and preserves 99.7% substrate material. Achieves 99.9% contaminant removal (FTIR-verified) and improves surface roughness from Ra 12.5μm to 3.2μm. Complies with ISO 11553-1 and ASTM E1256-17 standards.
