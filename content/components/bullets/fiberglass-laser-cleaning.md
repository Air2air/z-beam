* **Material Composition**: Fiberglass (FG) is a composite material primarily composed of silicon dioxide (SiO₂) and organic polymers (CₓHᵧOᵤ)ₙ, offering a unique combination of high thermal resistance and mechanical strength, making it suitable for laser cleaning applications.
* **Laser Parameters**: Optimal laser cleaning of fiberglass is achieved using a **pulsed fiber laser at 1064nm wavelength**, which ensures precise ablation of surface contaminants without damaging the underlying substrate due to controlled thermal processing.
* **Applications**: Ideal for **non-contact cleaning** in industrial settings, fiberglass laser cleaning is used for **surface contamination removal**, **surface restoration**, and preparing composite surfaces for coatings or adhesives, minimizing mechanical wear.
* **Benefits**: Laser cleaning offers **selective material removal**, reduced chemical usage, and enhanced precision compared to traditional methods, while maintaining the structural integrity of the fiberglass composite.
