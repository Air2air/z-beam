1. **Technical Focus: Material-specific laser parameters** The optimal parameters for ablating epoxy resin from fiberglass without damaging the underlying glass fibers typically involve a 1064 nm wavelength laser, operating with a fluence range of 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and nanosecond pulse durations to effectively remove contaminants prevalent in local electronics manufacturing.
2. **Technical Focus: Industrial applications** This non-abrasive technique is critical in Taiwan's high-value electronics sector for precision cleaning of fiberglass circuit board substrates and delicate composite components prior to soldering or coating processes.
3. **Technical Focus: Safety considerations** Operators must utilize fume extraction systems certified to local occupational safety standards to mitigate inhalation risks from vaporized epoxy resins and employ appropriate optical safety barriers for the 1064 nm wavelength.
4. **Technical Focus: Environmental benefits** Laser cleaning eliminates the need for chemical solvents and abrasive media, drastically reducing hazardous waste streams and aligning with the region's stringent industrial waste disposal regulations for a greener process.
