- **[APPLICATIONS]** Laser cleaning revitalizes copper components in high-voltage electrical systems and artistic heritage across European manufacturing, efficiently removing oxides and contaminants without substrate damage. This non-abrasive technique proves indispensable for restoring historical monuments and maintaining precision in electrical contact production.
- **[PARAMETERS]** Optimal cleaning employs fiber lasers at 1064 nm wavelength with fluences between 0.5–2.5 J/cm² and short pulse durations, leveraging copper’s high reflectivity and thermal conductivity. These parameters ensure selective removal of oxides while preventing thermal distortion, adhering to stringent quality standards in precision engineering.
- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** The process eliminates chemical solvents and abrasive waste, aligning with the EU’s Green Deal objectives by reducing hazardous byproducts and promoting sustainable conservation practices. This method supports circular economy goals in metal processing industries through minimal energy consumption and zero secondary pollution.
- **[SAFETY]** Operators must wear wavelength-specific protective eyewear and ensure adequate fume extraction to guard against airborne nanoparticles and reflected radiation hazards. Compliance with IEC 60825-1 for laser safety and workplace ventilation standards is mandatory to maintain operational safety in industrial settings.
- **[CHALLENGES]** Copper’s high reflectivity at near-IR wavelengths complicates energy coupling, requiring precise parameter tuning and often beam-shaping techniques to overcome inefficient ablation. Managing heat accumulation demands iterative pulse control to avoid surface melting, particularly in thin-walled components common in electrical applications.
