* **Material Properties**: Fused silica (SiO₂) is a high-purity (99.995%) amorphous glass with exceptional thermal stability (up to 1200°C), low thermal expansion (0.55×10⁻⁶/K), and UV-IR transparency. Key properties include a density of 2.20 g/cm³, Mohs hardness of 6.5, and thermal conductivity of 1.38 W/m·K, making it ideal for precision laser cleaning.
* **Laser Processing Parameters**: Optimal laser cleaning uses excimer (193-308nm) or Nd:YAG (266-1064nm) lasers, with UV wavelengths (193-355nm) preferred. Key settings include fluence range of 0.5-5 J/cm², pulse duration of 5-50ns (optimal 20ns), and repetition rates of 10-100kHz, ensuring contaminant removal without substrate damage.
* **Applications & Performance**: Ideal for semiconductor wafer cleaning (Class 1 cleanliness), lithography mask repair (sub-100nm particle removal), and optical coatings maintenance (99.9% contaminant removal). Achieves surface roughness <0.5nm Ra, >99.7% removal efficiency, and throughput of 15cm²/min at 1.5J/cm².
* **Environmental & Safety Benefits**: Eliminates solvent use (100% chemical reduction), consumes 60% less energy than plasma cleaning (2.1kW vs 5.4kW), and minimizes hazardous waste (0.5kg/m² less). Complies with safety standards (EN 60825-1) and industry regulations (ISO 10110-17, SEMI F47-0706).
