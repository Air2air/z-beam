1. **Technical Focus: Material-specific laser parameters** - The optimal laser cleaning parameters for fused silica, widely used in Taiwan's semiconductor photomask substrates, typically employ a 355 nm UV wavelength with a fluence carefully maintained below 2 J/cm² and nanosecond pulse durations to effectively remove contaminants without inducing subsurface damage to its high-purity, amorphous SiO₂ structure.
2. **Technical Focus: Industrial applications** - This non-contact process is indispensable within the local electronics sector for precision cleaning of critical optical components, including EUV lithography optics and large-area display glass, where it removes particulate and molecular contaminants without compromising surface integrity.
3. **Technical Focus: Safety considerations** - Strict adherence to Taiwan's CNS 15566 laser safety standards is mandatory, requiring Class 1 enclosures, proper laser-interlocked workstations, and certified personal protective equipment to mitigate risks from reflected beams and airborne nanoparticles generated during the ablation process.
4. **Technical Focus: Environmental benefits** - Laser cleaning provides a significant ecological advantage over traditional solvent-based methods by completely eliminating the consumption and subsequent disposal of hazardous chemical waste, aligning with the region's stringent green manufacturing initiatives for high-tech industries.
