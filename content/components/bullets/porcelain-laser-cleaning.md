* **Material Composition & Properties**: Porcelain (Po) is a ceramic composite primarily composed of **Al₂Si₂O₅(OH)₄·SiO₂·KAlSi₃O₈**, offering high thermal stability and mechanical strength. Its dense, non-porous structure makes it resistant to chemical corrosion, but surface contaminants (e.g., oxides, organic residues) can be effectively removed via **pulsed fiber laser cleaning** at **1064nm wavelength** without substrate damage.
* **Laser Parameters & Precision**: Optimal laser cleaning employs **short-pulse or ultra-short-pulse fiber lasers** (nanosecond to picosecond range) to minimize thermal stress. Energy densities of **0.5–5 J/cm²** are typical, ensuring precise ablation of surface layers (e.g., soot, adhesives) while preserving the porcelain’s glaze and structural integrity.
* **Industrial Applications**: Ideal for **restoration of historical ceramics**, removal of industrial coatings, and cleaning delicate porcelain components in electronics or sanitaryware. Non-contact laser cleaning eliminates mechanical abrasion risks, making it suitable for intricate geometries and high-value artifacts.
* **Key Benefits**: Combines **non-contact, eco-friendly cleaning** (no solvents/abrasives) with high repeatability. Laser processing avoids microcracking or surface etching common in traditional methods, ensuring **sub-micron precision** for contamination removal without altering the substrate’s aesthetic or functional properties.
