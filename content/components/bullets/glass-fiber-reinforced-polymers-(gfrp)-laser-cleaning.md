- **[APPLICATIONS]** Laser cleaning of GFRP composites is extensively utilized within the European aerospace and high-performance marine sectors for precise surface preparation and coating removal. This technique aligns with stringent EU manufacturing protocols, ensuring component integrity prior to adhesive bonding or repainting operations.
- **[PARAMETERS]** Optimal ablation of the polymeric matrix without damaging the glass fibers typically requires near-infrared wavelengths, such as 1064 nm, with carefully controlled fluence levels below 1.5 J/cm². Shorter pulse durations in the nanosecond range are generally favored to minimize the heat-affected zone and preserve the mechanical properties of the composite substrate.
- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** The process significantly reduces the generation of hazardous waste compared to traditional abrasive blasting, supporting the circular economy goals prevalent in European industry. It eliminates the need for chemical solvents, thereby aligning with the strict mandates of EU directives concerning volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions.
- **[SAFETY]** Rigorous enclosure design and fume extraction systems are mandatory, as the process can produce airborne particulates and vapors that require filtration according to EN standards. Operator protection necessitates the use of wavelength-specific laser safety eyewear and comprehensive training, which is a cornerstone of the Italian industrial safety framework.
- **[CHALLENGES]** A primary difficulty involves the disparate ablation thresholds between the glass fiber and the polymer resin, which demands extremely precise parameter control to avoid subsurface damage. Achieving consistent results on complex, curved components remains a significant hurdle for high-volume production lines, requiring advanced robotic integration for beam delivery.
