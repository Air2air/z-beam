* **Material Composition & Properties**: Soda-lime glass (SiO₂·Na₂O·CaO) comprises 70-75% SiO₂, 12-16% Na₂O, and 8-12% CaO, with a density of 2.4-2.8 g/cm³ and softening point of 600-900°C. Its thermal conductivity (0.8-1.2 W/m·K) and hardness (5.5-6.5 Mohs) make it suitable for controlled laser interactions.
* **Optimal Laser Parameters**: Effective cleaning requires 1064-10,600 nm wavelength (Nd:YAG, Fiber, or CO₂ lasers), 0.5-5 J/cm² fluence, and 5-200 ns pulse duration. Spot sizes of 50-500µm and repetition rates of 10-100kHz ensure precision without thermal stress.
* **Key Applications**: Includes display manufacturing (contaminant removal from LCD/OLED substrates), architectural glass restoration (99.9% efficiency), and laboratory equipment sterilization (266nm UV systems). Compatible with stainless steel fixtures and polymer adhesive residue removal.
* **Environmental & Performance Benefits**: Eliminates 100% of chemical solvents, saves 500L water/m² versus wet cleaning, and reduces solid waste by 90%. Achieves 0.1-0.5µm Ra surface roughness and >99% contaminant removal efficiency at 0.5-2 m²/hour processing speeds.
