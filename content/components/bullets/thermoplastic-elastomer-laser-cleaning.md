- **[APPLICATIONS]** Laser cleaning of Thermoplastic Elastomer components is extensively employed within the European automotive supply chain for precise de-molding and surface preparation of seals and grips. This non-abrasive technique aligns with lean manufacturing principles by significantly reducing manual rework and component handling time on production lines.

- **[PARAMETERS]** Optimal cleaning is typically achieved using a near-infrared fiber laser wavelength, often around 1064 nm, with a fluence carefully maintained below the material's ablation threshold to prevent subsurface thermal degradation. Pulse duration in the nanosecond range is standard, providing sufficient peak power for contaminant removal while ensuring controlled energy deposition that preserves the polymer matrix.

- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** The process eliminates the need for chemical solvents, directly supporting the stringent waste reduction goals of the European Green Deal and minimizing hazardous effluent from manufacturing sites. This dry technique also contributes to a reduced overall carbon footprint by curtailing the energy consumption associated with traditional cleaning and ventilation systems.

- **[SAFETY]** Operator protection mandates strict adherence to EN 60825-1 for laser safety, requiring fully enclosed interlocked workstations and appropriate personal protective equipment to guard against scattered radiation. Fume extraction and filtration systems are also imperative to manage any nano-scale particulates released during the cleaning process, ensuring a safe industrial environment.

- **[CHALLENGES]** A primary difficulty involves the precise calibration of laser parameters to account for the wide variety of TPE formulations and filler materials used across different suppliers, which can alter absorption characteristics. Furthermore, achieving consistent results on complex geometries or pigmented components requires sophisticated beam delivery and real-time monitoring to avoid surface gloss changes or minimal material removal.
