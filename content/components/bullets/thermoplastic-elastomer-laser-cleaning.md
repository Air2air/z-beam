* **Material Composition & Properties**: Thermoplastic Elastomer (TPE) consists of styrenic block copolymers (30–70%), polyolefins (20–50%), and mineral fillers (5–20%). Key properties include a density range of 0.90–1.30 g/cm³, Shore A hardness of 30–95, and thermal conductivity of 0.15–0.30 W/m·K, with a melting point varying between 120–220°C by grade.
* **Optimal Laser Parameters**: Laser cleaning of TPE is most effective with Nd:YAG or fiber lasers at 1064 nm wavelength (532 nm for selective processing), fluence range of 0.5–10 J/cm², and power settings of 10–100W (pulsed). Pulse duration of 10–200ns and repetition rates of 20–200kHz ensure precision without substrate damage.
* **Key Applications & Performance**: TPE laser cleaning is critical in medical device manufacturing (<0.1µm residue), automotive refurbishment (oxidized layer removal), and consumer electronics (selective ablation). Achieves <0.2µm residual contamination, 0.5–2.0 m²/hr processing speed, and <5µm substrate depth variation.
* **Environmental & Regulatory Benefits**: Eliminates VOC emissions (100% solvent-free), reduces energy use by 60% compared to thermal methods, and enables 95%+ TPE recycling efficiency. Complies with ISO 18558, FDA 21 CFR 177.2600, and RoHS Directive 2011/65/EU standards.
