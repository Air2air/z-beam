* **Material Composition & Properties**: Stoneware (SiO₂·Al₂O₃·MgO·K₂O) is a ceramic with 50–60% silica, 20–30% alumina, and minor oxides (MgO, K₂O, Fe₂O₃). Key properties include density (2.3–2.5 g/cm³), hardness (6–7 Mohs), and thermal conductivity (1.5–2.0 W/m·K), making it suitable for precise laser cleaning without substrate damage.
* **Optimal Laser Parameters**: Pulsed fiber laser (1064 nm ±2 nm) with fluence range (0.8–3.5 J/cm²), power (20–150W), pulse duration (30–100 ns), and spot size (0.1–0.5 mm) ensures effective contaminant removal while preserving microstructure. Achieves 99% cleanliness (SEM-EDS verified) with Ra <0.5 μm surface roughness change.
* **Key Applications**: Industrial (oxide layer removal from chemical processing components), cultural heritage (gentle cleaning of artifacts), and architectural restoration (biological growth removal). Compatible with stainless steel fixtures and historical artifacts, offering non-abrasive, in-situ cleaning.
* **Environmental & Efficiency Benefits**: Reduces chemical waste by 95%, consumes 40% less energy than abrasive methods, and preserves 99.7% substrate material. Complies with ISO 11553-1:2020 and EN 15886:2010 standards, achieving 0.5–2 m²/hour processing speed.
