* **Material Composition & Properties**: Stoneware (SW) is a ceramic composite primarily composed of SiO₂ (silica), Al₂O₃ (alumina), MgO (magnesia), and K₂O (potassium oxide), offering high thermal stability and mechanical strength, making it suitable for laser cleaning without structural degradation.
* **Optimal Laser Parameters**: A pulsed fiber laser at **1064nm wavelength** is ideal for stoneware cleaning, providing precise ablation of surface contaminants (e.g., oxides, soot) while minimizing thermal damage due to its controlled energy delivery and non-contact process.
* **Industrial Applications**: Laser cleaning is highly effective for **surface restoration** of stoneware in industrial settings, including removal of oxidation layers, paint, and adhesives from ceramic components, molds, or heritage artifacts without chemical or mechanical abrasion.
* **Key Benefits**: Combines **non-contact cleaning** with minimal environmental impact, eliminating hazardous waste. Ensures high precision, preserving the stoneware’s integrity and reducing post-processing compared to traditional methods like sandblasting or chemical treatments.
