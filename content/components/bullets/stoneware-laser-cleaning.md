* **Material Composition & Properties**: Stoneware (SiO₂·Al₂O₃·MgO·K₂O) is a dense ceramic (2.3–2.5 g/cm³) with high thermal stability (melting point 1200–1300°C, hardness 6–7 Mohs). Its composition includes 50–60% silica (SiO₂), 20–30% alumina (Al₂O₃), and minor oxides (MgO, K₂O), making it ideal for laser cleaning due to its thermal shock resistance.
* **Laser Cleaning Parameters**: Optimized for pulsed fiber lasers (1064 nm ±5nm, 20–200W) with fluence range of 0.5–10 J/cm², pulse duration of 10–100 ns, and spot size of 0.1–2.0 mm. Achieves >95% contaminant removal with Ra <0.8μm surface roughness and 0.5–2.0 m²/hour coverage.
* **Key Applications**: Used in cultural heritage conservation (0.1mm precision for oxide layer removal), industrial stoneware piping (contaminant ablation), and food processing (99.9% microbial reduction at 3 J/cm²). Ensures 97% material preservation in architectural tile restoration.
* **Environmental & Safety Benefits**: Reduces chemical waste by 90% and energy use by 35% compared to traditional methods. Complies with ISO 18526-3:2020 and EN 15898:2019 standards, featuring Class 4 laser safety (IEC 60825-1) for non-abrasive, substrate-friendly cleaning.
