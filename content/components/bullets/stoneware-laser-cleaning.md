* **Material Composition & Laser Interaction**: Stoneware (SiO₂·Al₂O₃·MgO·K₂O) is a dense ceramic with high thermal stability, making it suitable for pulsed fiber laser cleaning (e.g., 1064nm wavelength). Its composite structure ensures controlled ablation of surface contaminants without substrate damage.
* **Optimal Laser Parameters**: A pulsed fiber laser (1064nm) is ideal for stoneware, balancing energy absorption and thermal diffusion. Key settings include pulse durations of 10–100ns and fluences of 1–5 J/cm² to avoid microcracking or phase changes.
* **Applications & Benefits**: Non-contact laser cleaning effectively removes oxides, soot, and biological growth from stoneware in industrial and cultural heritage contexts. Benefits include precision, no chemical residue, and preservation of intricate surface details.
* **Challenges & Mitigation**: Stoneware’s low porosity requires careful parameter tuning to prevent thermal stress. Pre-testing on small areas and using lower pulse frequencies (1–10kHz) minimizes risks of surface degradation or discoloration.
