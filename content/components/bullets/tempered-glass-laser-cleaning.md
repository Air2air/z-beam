- **[APPLICATIONS]** Within European manufacturing, laser cleaning excels at removing anti-adhesive coatings and surface contaminants from tempered glass panels prior to assembly, a critical step for ensuring optimal bonding in high-performance fenestration systems. This non-abrasive technique is also indispensable for restoring historical architectural glass facades without compromising their structural integrity.

- **[PARAMETERS]** Optimal cleaning of tempered glass typically employs a near-infrared fiber laser wavelength, such as 1064 nm, with a fluence carefully maintained below the material's ablation threshold to prevent surface damage. A nanosecond pulse duration is commonly selected to effectively dislodge contaminants through rapid thermal expansion while preserving the glass's inherent compressive stress layer.

- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** The process eliminates the need for chemical solvents and abrasive media, aligning with the stringent waste disposal directives of the European Green Deal and promoting sustainable manufacturing cycles. This dry technique significantly reduces the generation of hazardous waste, contributing to a cleaner production environment and simplifying end-of-life material handling.

- **[SAFETY]** Strict adherence to EU Machinery Directive 2006/42/EC is mandatory, requiring fully enclosed interlocked workstations and certified laser safety eyewear to protect operators from scattered radiation. Process extraction systems with HEPA filtration are also essential to capture any nanoparticles released during the cleaning operation, ensuring a safe workplace atmosphere.

- **[CHALLENGES]** A primary difficulty involves meticulously calibrating energy density to avoid micro-fractures within the tempered layer, which can compromise the component's entire mechanical strength. Furthermore, achieving uniform cleaning efficacy across large, curved surfaces demands sophisticated scanning optics and precise motion control, increasing the initial system integration complexity.
