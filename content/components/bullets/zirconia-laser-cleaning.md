* **Material Properties & Laser Compatibility**: Zirconia (ZrO₂) exhibits exceptional thermal stability (melting point ~2715°C), hardness (8.5 Mohs), and chemical resistance. Optimal laser cleaning uses 1064nm (Nd:YAG/fiber) or 532nm (frequency-doubled) wavelengths at 1-10 J/cm² fluence, with pulse durations of 10-100 ns and spot sizes of 50-200 μm.
* **Key Applications**: Precision cleaning of dental YSZ crowns (532nm, 2.5 J/cm²), surface activation for thermal barrier coatings (1064nm, 8 J/cm², 100Hz), and selective cleaning of solid oxide fuel cell electrolyte layers. Compatible with titanium alloys, alumina substrates, and nickel superalloys for hybrid and medical applications.
* **Performance Outcomes**: Achieves Ra <0.2μm surface roughness (from 1.5μm), >99.9% organic contaminant removal (XPS-verified), and minimal phase transformation (<0.5% monoclinic content via XRD). Enables 99.7% material reuse, outperforming mechanical methods (85%).
* **Environmental & Regulatory Advantages**: Reduces chemical waste by 95% vs. solvent cleaning and consumes 40% less energy than thermal methods. Complies with ISO 13356 (medical Y-TZP), ASTM F1873 (surgical zirconia), and IEC 60825-1 (laser safety Class 4).
