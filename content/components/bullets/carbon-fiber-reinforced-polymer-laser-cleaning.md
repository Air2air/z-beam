1. **Technical Focus:** Material-specific laser parameters: Optimal CFRP ablation is achieved using a 1,064 nm fiber laser with fluences precisely controlled between 0.5 to 1.5 J/cm² and nanosecond pulse durations to selectively remove contaminants while preserving the epoxy matrix and fiber integrity, a critical calibration for high-value electronics manufacturing.
2. **Technical Focus:** Industrial applications: This non-abrasive process is indispensable for precision cleaning of CFRP fixtures and handling tools within semiconductor fabrication cleanrooms, ensuring particulate-free surfaces without compromising dimensional stability or introducing mechanical stress.
3. **Technical Focus:** Safety considerations: Strict adherence to Class 4 laser operational protocols, including the use of interlocked enclosures and fume extraction systems certified to local air quality standards, is mandatory to mitigate risks from airborne carbon particulates and intense reflected beams.
4. **Technical Focus:** Environmental benefits: Laser cleaning eliminates chemical solvents and abrasive media waste streams, significantly reducing the industrial hazardous waste burden and aligning with the region's stringent green manufacturing initiatives for the electronics sector.
