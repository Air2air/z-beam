  "articleBody": "Plywood is an engineered wood material composed of multiple thin wood veneers bonded together with adhesives, creating a cross-grained structure that provides exceptional strength, dimensional stability, and resistance to cracking. These properties make Plywood particularly suitable for laser cleaning applications where traditional methods might cause damage. The optimal laser parameters for Plywood cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with controlled fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-200 nanoseconds. These parameters enable selective removal of surface contaminants, aged coatings, and biological growth without compromising the wood's structural integrity or adhesive bonds. Key applications include restoration of architectural Plywood elements, preparation for refinishing in furniture manufacturing, and surface treatment in marine and construction industries where precise contamination removal is critical. Challenges include managing the varying absorption characteristics across different wood grains and adhesive layers, which requires dynamic parameter adjustment during processing. Safety considerations mandate proper ventilation systems to manage pyrolysis byproducts, Class 4 laser safety protocols including appropriate eyewear, and fire suppression equipment due to Plywood's combustible nature. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical stress on the delicate wood fibers, making it ideal for preserving historical artifacts and high-value Plywood components.",
