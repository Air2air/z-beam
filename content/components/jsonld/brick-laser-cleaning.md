These properties make Brick particularly sensitive to traditional cleaning methods that can cause micro-fracturing, surface erosion, or chemical penetration. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, biological growth, paint coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths typically between 1064nm and 532nm, fluence levels of 0.5-2.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Brick substrate. Key applications include historical building restoration, industrial facility maintenance, and architectural conservation where precise material preservation is critical. Challenges include managing the material's porosity, which can trap contaminants, and variations in Brick composition that require parameter adjustments. Technical solutions involve multi-pass cleaning protocols and real-time monitoring systems.
