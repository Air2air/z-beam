  "articleBody": "Limestone, primarily composed of calcium carbonate (CaCO₃), is a sedimentary rock with moderate porosity and varying hardness that requires careful surface treatment. Laser cleaning provides an optimal solution for removing contaminants, biological growth, and surface deposits without mechanical abrasion or chemical residues. The process utilizes precise laser parameters, typically near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds, which selectively ablate contaminants while preserving the underlying calcite matrix. Key applications include architectural restoration, historical monument conservation, and industrial component preparation where surface integrity is critical. Challenges include managing the material's thermal sensitivity and variable porosity, addressed through precise parameter optimization and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations require appropriate laser class enclosures, fume extraction for calcium oxide byproducts, and operator eye protection against reflected beams.
