Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths of 1064nm nanosecond pulses, fluence levels of 0.8-1.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Ruthenium substrate. Key applications include regeneration of Ruthenium-based catalyst surfaces in chemical processing, cleaning of electrical contacts in high-reliability systems, and preparation of Ruthenium thin films in semiconductor manufacturing. Challenges include Ruthenium's high reflectivity requiring optimized beam delivery angles and thermal management to prevent microcracking due to its brittle nature.
