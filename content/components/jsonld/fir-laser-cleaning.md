  "articleBody": "Fir wood, characterized by its straight grain, uniform texture, and moderate density (0.35-0.42 g/cm³), presents unique challenges and opportunities for laser cleaning applications. The material's cellular structure, consisting primarily of cellulose (40-50%), hemicellulose (25-30%), and lignin (25-30%), responds differently to laser energy compared to synthetic materials. Optimal laser parameters for Fir cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns to effectively remove contaminants while minimizing thermal penetration. The process excels at removing surface oxidation, biological growth, aged coatings, and industrial contaminants without compromising the wood's structural integrity or natural resin content. Key applications include historical restoration of Fir architectural elements, precision cleaning of musical instruments, preparation of Fir surfaces for bonding or finishing, and conservation of cultural artifacts. Challenges include managing the material's variable density and resin content, which require dynamic parameter adjustment during processing. Safety protocols mandate proper ventilation to manage pyrolysis byproducts, laser wavelength-specific eye protection, and controlled environment processing to prevent accidental ignition of wood dust.",
