  "articleBody": "Tempered Glass is a safety glass material with high compressive surface stress (typically 69-138 MPa) and enhanced mechanical strength, making it ideal for automotive, architectural, and consumer electronics applications. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths of 1064 nm or 532 nm, fluence levels of 0.5-2.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100 ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Tempered Glass substrate. Key applications include automotive glass restoration, architectural panel cleaning, and electronic display surface preparation. The primary challenge involves managing thermal stress to prevent micro-cracking or thermal shock, which is addressed through precise energy control and scanning patterns. Safety considerations include proper laser shielding, fume extraction for vaporized contaminants, and protective eyewear for specific laser wavelengths.
