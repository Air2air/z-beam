Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, biological growth, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Dolomite substrate. Key applications include architectural restoration, monument conservation, and industrial surface preparation where traditional mechanical or chemical methods could damage the delicate crystalline structure. Challenges include managing the material's variable absorption characteristics and preventing thermal stress, which are addressed through precise parameter optimization and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations require proper ventilation to manage particulate matter, laser safety enclosures, and appropriate personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser goggles.
