  "articleBody": "Soapstone (talc-chlorite schist) is a metamorphic rock composed primarily of talc, magnesite, and chlorite, possessing unique thermal stability, chemical resistance, and machinability that make it valuable for industrial applications. Laser cleaning provides a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, oxides, and surface deposits without compromising Soapstone's structural integrity or natural properties. Optimal laser parameters typically utilize near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical Soapstone artifacts, surface preparation for industrial components, and removal of manufacturing residues from precision-machined parts. Challenges include managing Soapstone's variable mineral composition and thermal sensitivity, which require carefully calibrated laser parameters to prevent thermal stress or mineral phase changes. Safety protocols mandate proper ventilation to manage particulate matter, laser safety eyewear for specific wavelengths, and containment systems to capture ablation byproducts. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical damage risks while achieving superior surface cleanliness for subsequent processing or finishing operations.",
