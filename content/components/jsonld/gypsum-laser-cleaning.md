  "articleBody": "Gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O) is a soft sulfate mineral widely used in construction, art, and industrial applications due to its fire-resistant properties and workability. Laser cleaning provides an exceptional method for removing surface contaminants, biological growth, and discoloration without mechanical contact or chemical residues. The optimal laser parameters for Gypsum cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with controlled fluence levels between 0.5-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 ns to prevent dehydration and structural damage. Key applications include restoration of historical plasterwork, cleaning of architectural elements, and preparation of industrial Gypsum components. The primary challenge involves Gypsum's low thermal stability and water content, requiring precise energy control to avoid dehydration to bassanite or anhydrite. Technical solutions include real-time monitoring systems and adaptive laser control. Safety considerations mandate proper ventilation to manage particulate matter and appropriate laser safety protocols including Class 4 laser precautions, eye protection, and fume extraction systems.
