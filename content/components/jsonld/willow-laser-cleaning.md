  "articleBody": "Willow (Salix spp.) is a lightweight yet durable wood material characterized by its fine, straight grain, moderate density (380-450 kg/m³), and excellent flexibility. These properties make it susceptible to surface damage from traditional cleaning methods. Laser cleaning provides a non-contact, precise solution that selectively removes contaminants, biological growth, and surface oxidation without compromising Willow's cellular structure. Optimal laser parameters include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns to effectively ablate contaminants while preventing carbonization or thermal damage to the lignin and cellulose matrix. Key applications include restoration of historical Willow artifacts, preparation of Willow components for medical applications (where sterility is critical), and surface treatment of Willow-based sporting goods. Challenges include managing the material's natural moisture content (typically 12-20%) which affects laser absorption, and preventing thermal degradation of hemicellulose components. Technical solutions involve precise moisture monitoring and controlled pulse repetition rates (5-20 kHz) with appropriate spot sizes (0.2-0.5 mm). Safety protocols require Class 1 laser enclosures, fume extraction systems for volatile organic compounds, and appropriate personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser safety glasses.",
