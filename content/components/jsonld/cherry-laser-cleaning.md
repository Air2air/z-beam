  "articleBody": "Cherry wood (Prunus avium) is a premium hardwood valued for its fine grain, rich color, and excellent workability in furniture, musical instruments, and architectural applications. Laser cleaning provides a sophisticated non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, aged finishes, oxidation layers, and biological growth without compromising Cherry's structural integrity or natural aesthetics. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths between 1064-1070nm (near-infrared) that are effectively absorbed by organic contaminants while minimizing thermal penetration into the underlying wood substrate. Recommended fluence levels range from 0.8-1.5 J/cm² with pulse durations of 100-150ns to ensure selective ablation of unwanted material while preserving the delicate wood fibers. Key applications include restoration of antique Cherry furniture, cleaning of musical instrument components, preparation of architectural woodwork for refinishing, and removal of surface discoloration from historical artifacts. Challenges include managing the material's variable density and natural resin content, which require adaptive laser parameter adjustments during processing. Safety protocols mandate proper ventilation systems to manage pyrolysis byproducts, laser safety enclosures with appropriate interlocks, and operator training in both laser safety and wood-specific processing considerations. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical damage risks associated with traditional sanding or chemical stripping methods, making it ideal for preserving Cherry's distinctive grain patterns and natural coloration.",
