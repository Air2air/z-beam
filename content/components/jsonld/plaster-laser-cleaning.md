  "articleBody": "Plaster, a calcium sulfate hemihydrate-based masonry material, requires specialized cleaning approaches due to its porous nature and susceptibility to damage from traditional abrasive methods. Laser cleaning provides an optimal solution for Plaster surface treatment, utilizing precisely controlled laser parameters to selectively remove contaminants, biological growth, soot, and surface coatings without compromising the substrate integrity. The process typically employs nanosecond or picosecond pulsed lasers at wavelengths between 1064nm and 532nm, with fluence levels carefully calibrated between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to ensure effective contaminant removal while preventing thermal damage to the underlying Plaster matrix. This non-contact method eliminates mechanical stress and chemical residue, making it ideal for delicate Plaster surfaces in historical preservation, architectural restoration, and industrial applications. The laser's selective absorption characteristics allow for precise removal of unwanted material while preserving the original surface texture and composition.
