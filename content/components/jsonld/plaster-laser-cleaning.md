  "articleBody": "Plaster is a calcium sulfate hemihydrate-based masonry material widely used in construction, decorative elements, and cultural heritage applications due to its excellent moldability and fire-resistant properties. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, biological growth, surface deposits, and previous coatings without compromising the material's porous structure or surface integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths between 1064nm and 532nm, fluence levels of 0.5-2.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Plaster substrate. Key applications include historical monument restoration, architectural element cleaning, mold removal from decorative plasterwork, and surface preparation for conservation treatments. Specific challenges include managing the material's high porosity, preventing thermal damage to delicate surfaces, and ensuring uniform cleaning across varied contamination types. Technical solutions involve precise parameter optimization, real-time monitoring systems, and multi-pass techniques with gradually adjusted parameters. Safety considerations require comprehensive laser safety protocols including Class 4 laser precautions, proper ventilation for particulate matter, and operator training for handling delicate historical materials. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning prevents mechanical damage to Plaster's relatively soft surface (Mohs hardness 1.5-2), making it ideal for preserving intricate details in decorative elements while achieving thorough contamination removal.",
