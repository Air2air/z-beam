The optimal laser parameters for Aluminum cleaning typically utilize fiber lasers with wavelengths of 1064 nm, fluence levels between 2-8 J/cm², and pulse durations of 100-200 ns to effectively remove oxides, contaminants, and coatings without causing surface damage. Key applications include aerospace component restoration, automotive part refurbishment, electronics manufacturing, and architectural element conservation. The primary challenges involve Aluminum's high reflectivity, which requires precise laser parameter optimization to ensure efficient energy absorption, and its low melting point (660°C), necessitating careful thermal management to prevent surface deformation. Technical solutions include using pulsed lasers with controlled energy density and implementing real-time monitoring systems to adjust parameters dynamically. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 laser enclosures, proper ventilation to manage aluminum oxide fumes, and appropriate personal protective equipment including laser safety glasses with OD 7+ rating at the operational wavelength.
