  "articleBody": "Indium is a soft, malleable metal with a low melting point of 156.6°C, making it particularly sensitive to thermal damage during cleaning processes. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxide layers, and surface oxidation without compromising Indium's structural integrity or altering its electrical properties. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064 nm wavelength with fluences between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Indium substrate. Key applications include cleaning Indium-tin-oxide (ITO) coatings for display manufacturing, preparing surfaces for soldering and bonding in semiconductor applications, and maintaining precision components in aerospace and electronics. Challenges include managing thermal input to prevent melting, addressing the material's high reflectivity at certain wavelengths, and ensuring complete oxide removal without surface damage. Technical solutions involve precise parameter optimization, real-time monitoring systems, and controlled atmospheric conditions. Safety considerations require proper ventilation to manage airborne particles, laser safety protocols including appropriate eyewear, and fume extraction systems to handle potential byproducts.
