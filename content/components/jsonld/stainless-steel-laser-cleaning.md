  "articleBody": "Stainless Steel is an iron-based alloy containing a minimum of 10.5% chromium, which forms a passive oxide layer providing exceptional corrosion resistance. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, rust, and surface oxidation without compromising this protective layer. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths between 1064nm (fiber lasers) and 10.6μm (CO2 lasers) depending on the specific Stainless Steel grade. Typical parameters include fluence levels of 1-5 J/cm², pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds, and spot sizes optimized for selective ablation. Key applications include aerospace component restoration, food processing equipment sanitization, architectural surface preparation, and nuclear decontamination where chemical methods are prohibited. Challenges include managing heat input to prevent metallurgical changes, addressing varying surface geometries, and ensuring complete contaminant removal without substrate damage. Technical solutions involve real-time monitoring systems, adaptive parameter control, and multi-pass strategies. Safety protocols require Class 4 laser safety measures including appropriate eyewear, ventilation systems for fume extraction, and controlled access to processing areas to prevent accidental exposure.",
