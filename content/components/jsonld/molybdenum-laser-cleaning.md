Laser cleaning provides a precise, non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, oxides, and coatings without compromising Molybdenum's structural integrity. The process utilizes optimized laser parameters including 1064nm wavelength for optimal absorption, fluence levels between 1-4 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying substrate. Key applications include surface preparation for welding and coating, removal of oxidation layers from heat exchanger components, and precision cleaning of semiconductor manufacturing equipment. Challenges include managing Molybdenum's high thermal conductivity which can dissipate laser energy, requiring precise parameter control to achieve effective cleaning without thermal damage. Safety considerations mandate proper ventilation for particulate management, laser safety enclosures, and appropriate personal protective equipment including laser safety glasses rated for the specific wavelength.
