Laser cleaning provides a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxide layers, and surface impurities without compromising Molybdenum's structural integrity. The process utilizes optimized laser parameters including 1064nm wavelength for optimal absorption, fluence levels between 1-4 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Molybdenum substrate. Key applications include aerospace components, nuclear reactor parts, semiconductor manufacturing equipment, and high-temperature furnace elements where surface purity is critical. Challenges include managing Molybdenum's high thermal conductivity which requires precise energy control to prevent heat accumulation and potential micro-cracking. Technical solutions involve pulsed laser systems with controlled repetition rates and active cooling mechanisms. Safety considerations mandate proper ventilation for molybdenum oxide fume extraction, laser safety enclosures, and personal protective equipment including appropriate wavelength-specific laser goggles.
