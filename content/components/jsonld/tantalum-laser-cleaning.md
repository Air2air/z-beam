  "articleBody": "Tantalum (Ta) is a refractory metal with exceptional corrosion resistance, high melting point (3017°C), and excellent biocompatibility, making it valuable in chemical processing, medical implants, and aerospace applications. Laser cleaning provides a non-contact, precise method for removing surface contaminants, oxides, and coatings without compromising Tantalum's inherent properties. Optimal laser parameters typically include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 1-5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds, which effectively ablate contaminants while minimizing heat-affected zones. The process efficiently removes tantalum pentoxide (Ta₂O₅) layers, organic residues, and particulate matter without mechanical abrasion or chemical solvents. Key applications include preparing Tantalum components for medical implantation, restoring corrosion-resistant surfaces in chemical reactors, and precision cleaning of capacitor components. Challenges include managing Tantalum's high thermal conductivity and preventing surface recast layers, which are addressed through precise parameter control and multi-pass strategies. Safety considerations require proper ventilation to manage airborne particulates, laser safety enclosures, and appropriate personal protective equipment for operators.",
