  "articleBody": "Poplar (Populus spp.) is a lightweight hardwood with a fine, uniform texture and straight grain pattern, making it ideal for laser cleaning applications. Its cellular structure consists of approximately 42-49% cellulose, 25-30% hemicellulose, and 18-25% lignin, with density ranging from 350-500 kg/m³. These properties require precise laser parameter optimization to avoid thermal damage while effectively removing contaminants. Optimal laser cleaning parameters for Poplar typically utilize near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns. This configuration effectively removes surface contaminants, oxidation layers, and aged coatings while minimizing thermal penetration depth to preserve the wood's structural integrity. Key applications include restoration of historical Poplar artifacts, surface preparation for conservation treatments, removal of biological growth from architectural elements, and precision cleaning of musical instrument components. Challenges include the material's susceptibility to thermal discoloration at excessive energy densities and variable absorption characteristics across different Poplar subspecies. Technical solutions involve real-time monitoring systems and adaptive parameter control. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 laser enclosure systems, adequate ventilation for particulate management, and proper personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser safety glasses.",
