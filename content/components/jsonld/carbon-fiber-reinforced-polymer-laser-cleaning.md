  "articleBody": "Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer (Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer) is a high-performance composite material characterized by its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio, chemical resistance, and anisotropic properties. Laser cleaning provides a superior alternative to traditional mechanical and chemical methods by offering precise, non-contact removal of contaminants, coatings, and oxidation layers without compromising the carbon fiber-epoxy matrix interface. Optimal laser parameters for Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064nm Nd:YAG lasers) with fluence levels carefully controlled between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate surface contaminants while avoiding thermal degradation of the epoxy matrix. Key applications include aerospace component refurbishment, automotive composite repair, wind turbine blade maintenance, and sporting goods manufacturing. The primary challenge involves managing the significant difference in ablation thresholds between contaminants (typically 0.1-0.5 J/cm²) and the Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer substrate (2.5-4.0 J/cm²), requiring precise parameter control to prevent matrix damage while ensuring complete contaminant removal. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 enclosure systems with integrated fume extraction to capture volatile organic compounds and carbon particulates generated during the ablation process, along with proper laser safety eyewear and industrial ventilation systems.",
