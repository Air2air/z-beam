  "articleBody": "Acrylic (Polymethyl methacrylate or PMMA) is a transparent thermoplastic widely used in industrial applications due to its excellent optical clarity, weather resistance, and mechanical properties. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, surface oxidation, and biological growth without compromising the material's structural integrity or optical properties. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing UV wavelengths (266-355 nm) with low fluence levels (0.1-0.5 J/cm²) and short pulse durations (nanosecond range) to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Acrylic substrate. Key applications include restoration of optical components, preparation of display surfaces, cleaning of architectural elements, and pretreatment for adhesion bonding. Challenges include managing Acrylic's relatively low thermal degradation threshold (approximately 85°C) and preventing surface micro-cracking. Technical solutions involve precise parameter optimization, real-time temperature monitoring, and multi-pass strategies with cooling intervals. Safety considerations require proper ventilation for fume extraction, UV protective eyewear, and controlled processing environments to prevent accidental laser exposure. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical stress and preserves Acrylic's surface finish, making it ideal for sensitive optical and display applications where surface quality is critical.",
