  "articleBody": "Silicon, with its chemical symbol Si and atomic number 14, is a crystalline semiconductor material with a diamond cubic crystal structure, exhibiting a melting point of 1414°C and a band gap of 1.12 eV at room temperature. These properties make Silicon particularly suitable for laser cleaning applications in semiconductor manufacturing, where surface purity is critical. Optimal laser parameters for Silicon cleaning typically involve nanosecond to femtosecond pulsed lasers at wavelengths of 1064 nm or 532 nm, with fluence levels carefully controlled between 0.5-2.0 J/cm² to avoid surface damage while effectively removing contaminants. The process excels at eliminating organic residues, metallic particles, and native oxide layers without introducing mechanical stress or chemical contamination. Key applications include pre-deposition cleaning of Silicon wafers, removal of photoresist layers, and surface preparation for microelectronics fabrication. Challenges include managing the material's high reflectivity at common laser wavelengths and preventing thermal damage to delicate structures. Technical solutions involve precise beam shaping, wavelength optimization, and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations mandate proper laser classification compliance, eye protection with appropriate optical density, fume extraction for ablation byproducts, and controlled environments to prevent particulate recontamination. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning preserves the crystalline structure and electrical properties of Silicon substrates, making it superior to traditional chemical and mechanical cleaning methods for high-precision applications.",
