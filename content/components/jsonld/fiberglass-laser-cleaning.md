articleBody: "Fiberglass, composed of glass fibers embedded in a polymer matrix (typically epoxy or polyester resin), presents unique cleaning challenges due to its composite nature and sensitivity to mechanical stress. Laser cleaning provides an optimal solution by delivering controlled energy pulses that selectively remove contaminants, oxidation layers, and degraded resin without affecting the underlying glass fibers. The process typically employs nanosecond or picosecond pulsed lasers at wavelengths between 1064nm and 355nm, with fluence levels carefully calibrated between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to avoid matrix degradation. Critical parameters include pulse duration (1-100ns), repetition rate (10-100kHz), and spot overlap (50-80%) to ensure complete contaminant removal while maintaining surface temperature below 150°C to prevent resin melting. This non-contact method eliminates mechanical abrasion risks, preserves fiber orientation integrity, and prepares surfaces for subsequent coating applications with superior adhesion properties. Industrial applications include aerospace component refurbishment, marine structure maintenance, wind turbine blade cleaning, and automotive composite part restoration."
