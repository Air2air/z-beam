  "articleBody": "Alabaster, a fine-grained variety of gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O), presents unique challenges and opportunities for laser cleaning applications. With a Mohs hardness of 1.5-2 and high water solubility, traditional cleaning methods often cause surface damage or chemical alteration. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact solution that preserves the material's delicate structure while effectively removing surface contaminants, biological growth, and atmospheric deposits. Optimal laser parameters for Alabaster include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with controlled fluence levels between 0.5-1.5 J/cm² and short pulse durations (10-100 ns) to minimize thermal penetration and prevent dehydration of the gypsum matrix. The process selectively ablates contaminants through photomechanical and photothermal mechanisms without affecting the underlying substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical monuments, conservation of cultural artifacts, and precision cleaning of architectural elements. Special considerations include controlling energy density to prevent phase transformations and implementing real-time monitoring systems to detect surface changes. Safety protocols require appropriate laser classification, fume extraction for particulate matter, and optical protection for operators. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning makes it particularly valuable for preserving intricate carvings and delicate surface details that characterize high-quality Alabaster artworks and architectural features.",
