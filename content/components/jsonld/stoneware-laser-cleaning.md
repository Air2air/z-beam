  "articleBody": "Stoneware is a dense, vitrified ceramic material composed primarily of clay, feldspar, and quartz, characterized by its high mechanical strength, chemical resistance, and thermal stability. These properties make it ideal for industrial applications but present unique challenges for surface cleaning. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. Optimal laser parameters for Stoneware typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds, allowing selective ablation of unwanted material while preserving the underlying substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical Stoneware artifacts, cleaning of industrial ceramic components in manufacturing, removal of surface contaminants from architectural elements, and preparation of Stoneware surfaces for subsequent treatments or coatings. Challenges include managing thermal stress to prevent micro-cracking, addressing varying surface porosity, and ensuring uniform cleaning across complex geometries. Safety considerations require proper laser safety enclosures, fume extraction systems for airborne particulates, and appropriate personal protective equipment including laser safety glasses rated for the specific wavelength.
