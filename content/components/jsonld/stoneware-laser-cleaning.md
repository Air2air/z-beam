  "articleBody": "Stoneware is a dense, vitrified ceramic material characterized by high mechanical strength, chemical resistance, and thermal stability, making it ideal for industrial applications where durability is essential. Laser cleaning provides a superior non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, oxidation layers, and unwanted coatings without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters, typically employing near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds. These parameters are carefully optimized to selectively ablate contaminants while preserving the underlying Stoneware substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical Stoneware artifacts, surface preparation for industrial coatings, removal of manufacturing residues from technical ceramics, and cleaning of architectural Stoneware elements. Challenges include managing thermal stress to prevent microcracking and ensuring uniform cleaning across the material's naturally varied surface texture. Technical solutions involve implementing scanning patterns with overlapping pulses and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations mandate proper laser shielding, fume extraction for airborne particulates, and operator protection against reflected radiation. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical damage risks associated with traditional methods, making it particularly valuable for delicate Stoneware applications where surface integrity is critical.",
