  "articleBody": "Rhenium is a refractory metal with exceptional properties including one of the highest melting points (3186°C) among metals, excellent high-temperature strength, and remarkable wear resistance. These characteristics make it ideal for extreme environment applications but present unique challenges for surface cleaning. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxides, and surface impurities without compromising Rhenium's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths between 1064-1070nm nanosecond pulses, fluence levels of 2-8 J/cm², and pulse durations of 100-200ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Rhenium substrate. Critical applications include cleaning turbine blades, rocket engine components, electrical contacts, and high-temperature thermocouples where surface purity directly impacts performance. Challenges include Rhenium's high reflectivity to certain wavelengths and thermal sensitivity, requiring precise parameter optimization to prevent micro-cracking or surface modification. Safety protocols mandate Class 4 laser precautions with appropriate eyewear, ventilation for particulate management, and containment of potentially hazardous ablation byproducts. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical stress and chemical contamination, making it particularly valuable for precision components where material integrity is paramount.",
