Its unique properties, including high thermal conductivity and reactivity with oxygen, present specific challenges for surface treatment. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxides, and surface impurities without compromising Gallium's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters including 1064nm wavelength, 0.5-2.0 J/cm² fluence, and nanosecond pulse durations to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Gallium substrate. Key applications include semiconductor manufacturing, where ultra-clean Gallium surfaces are essential for GaAs wafer production, and photovoltaic applications where surface purity directly impacts device performance. Challenges include managing Gallium's low melting point through precise thermal control and preventing re-deposition of ablated material. Safety considerations require proper ventilation to manage Gallium oxide fumes and appropriate laser safety protocols including Class 4 laser precautions and personal protective equipment.
