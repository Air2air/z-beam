  "articleBody": "Inconel is a nickel-chromium-based superalloy renowned for its exceptional corrosion resistance, high-temperature strength, and mechanical stability under extreme conditions. These properties make it ideal for aerospace, chemical processing, and power generation applications but present unique challenges for surface cleaning. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing oxides, heat tint, carbon deposits, and surface contaminants without compromising Inconel's metallurgical integrity. Optimal laser parameters typically include 1064nm wavelength for effective absorption, fluence levels between 2-8 J/cm², and pulse durations of 100-200ns to achieve selective ablation without substrate damage. The process effectively removes surface contaminants while preserving the protective chromium oxide layer essential for Inconel's corrosion resistance. Key applications include pre-weld cleaning of aerospace components, oxide removal from turbine blades, and surface preparation for thermal spray coatings in nuclear reactors. Challenges include managing heat input to prevent microstructural changes and selecting appropriate parameters for different Inconel grades (600, 625, 718, X-750). Safety protocols require Class 4 laser precautions, including fume extraction for nickel-chromium particles and appropriate personal protective equipment.",
