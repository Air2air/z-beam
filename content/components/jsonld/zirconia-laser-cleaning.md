articleBody: "Zirconia (ZrO₂) is a high-performance ceramic material valued for its exceptional mechanical strength, thermal stability, and chemical resistance. Laser cleaning provides an optimal surface treatment method for zirconia components, particularly in medical implants, cutting tools, and aerospace applications where precision is critical. The process employs pulsed fiber lasers with wavelengths typically between 1064-1070nm, operating at fluences of 2-8 J/cm² to selectively remove contaminants, oxide layers, and residual coatings without inducing microcracks or phase transformations. Proper parameter optimization ensures minimal heat-affected zone while effectively eliminating organic residues, metal particles, and surface oxidation. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning prevents mechanical stress on the brittle ceramic substrate, maintaining dimensional accuracy and surface finish. This technique is particularly valuable for preparing zirconia surfaces for subsequent processes such as coating deposition, bonding, or quality inspection, where surface purity directly impacts performance and reliability."
