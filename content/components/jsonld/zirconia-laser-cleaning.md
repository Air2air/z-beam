articleBody: "Zirconia (ZrO₂) is a high-performance ceramic material widely used in dental prosthetics, industrial components, and aerospace applications due to its exceptional strength and biocompatibility. Laser cleaning offers a non-contact, precise method for removing contaminants from zirconia surfaces without damaging the substrate. This process utilizes focused laser beams to vaporize organic residues, oxides, and surface impurities while preserving the material's structural integrity. Key advantages include no chemical waste, selective cleaning capability, and suitability for complex geometries. Optimal laser parameters for zirconia typically involve pulsed Nd:YAG or fiber lasers with wavelengths between 1064-1070nm, ensuring effective contaminant removal while minimizing thermal stress. Applications range from pre-sintered dental crown preparation to post-processing of industrial zirconia components."
