Laser cleaning of Silicon Carbide requires precise parameter optimization due to its unique material properties. Optimal laser parameters typically include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to effectively remove contaminants without damaging the substrate. Key applications include semiconductor wafer cleaning, power electronics manufacturing, and aerospace component preparation where surface purity is critical. The primary challenge in SiC laser cleaning involves its high thermal conductivity, which requires carefully controlled energy deposition to prevent thermal stress and microcracking. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 laser enclosures, proper fume extraction for particulate matter, and appropriate personal protective equipment including laser safety goggles rated for the specific wavelength.
