The material's calcium carbonate content and natural veining patterns require precise laser parameter optimization to avoid thermal damage, discoloration, or surface etching. Optimal laser cleaning utilizes near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with controlled fluence levels (0.8-1.5 J/cm²) and short pulse durations (10-100 ns) to selectively ablate contaminants while preserving the underlying stone matrix. Key applications include architectural restoration, luxury interior surfaces, artistic conservation, and high-end decorative elements where traditional cleaning methods risk surface abrasion or chemical penetration. The process effectively removes biological growth, atmospheric pollution deposits, adhesive residues, and surface oxidation without compromising Onyx's natural translucency or structural integrity. Technical challenges include managing thermal conductivity variations between banded layers and preventing micro-fracturing in more porous sections. Advanced scanning systems with real-time thermal monitoring ensure uniform energy distribution across heterogeneous surfaces.
