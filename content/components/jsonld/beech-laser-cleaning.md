  "articleBody": "Beech (Fagus sylvatica) is a dense hardwood material characterized by its fine, tight grain structure, high mechanical strength, and uniform texture, making it ideal for precision industrial applications. Laser cleaning offers a sophisticated non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, aged coatings, biological growth, and oxidation layers without compromising Beech's structural integrity or natural beauty. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths typically in the near-infrared range (1064 nm) and specific fluence levels (0.5-2.5 J/cm²) to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying wood substrate. Pulse durations in the nanosecond range prevent thermal damage and carbonization, maintaining Beech's natural color and grain patterns. Key applications include restoration of historical furniture, preparation of architectural elements, cleaning of manufacturing components, and surface treatment in high-precision woodworking industries. Challenges include managing the material's natural moisture content (typically 8-12%), preventing thermal degradation, and addressing the varying density within growth rings. Technical solutions involve real-time monitoring systems, adaptive laser parameter control, and integrated cooling mechanisms.
