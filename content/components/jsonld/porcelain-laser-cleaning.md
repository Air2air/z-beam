  "articleBody": "Porcelain is a vitrified ceramic material composed primarily of kaolin, feldspar, and quartz, characterized by its high mechanical strength, chemical inertness, and thermal stability. These properties make it ideal for demanding industrial applications but present unique challenges for surface cleaning. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal settings at 1064 nm wavelength, 0.5-2.0 J/cm² fluence, and nanosecond pulse durations to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Porcelain substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical porcelain artifacts, surface preparation for bonding in electrical insulators, and removal of surface contaminants from medical and dental porcelain components. Challenges include the material's brittleness and thermal sensitivity, which are addressed through precise parameter control and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations require appropriate laser safety eyewear, fume extraction systems for ablated particulates, and controlled environment operation to prevent thermal shock to the porcelain material.",
