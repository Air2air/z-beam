articleBody: "Porcelain is a vitrified ceramic material composed primarily of kaolin, quartz, and feldspar, valued for its hardness, chemical resistance, and electrical insulation properties. Laser cleaning provides a superior alternative to traditional mechanical or chemical methods for porcelain surface treatment. The process employs nanosecond or picosecond pulsed lasers with wavelengths typically between 1064nm and 532nm, carefully calibrated to match porcelain\'s absorption characteristics. Optimal parameters range from 0.5-5 J/cm² fluence with pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to ensure selective removal of contaminants like oxidation layers, organic deposits, or previous coatings without inducing microcracking or thermal damage. The non-contact nature eliminates mechanical stress risks while achieving micron-level precision, particularly crucial for intricate industrial components and heritage artifacts. Industrial applications include cleaning electrical insulators, restoring architectural elements, and preparing surfaces for subsequent processes like coating or bonding. The method effectively removes environmental soiling, biological growth, and industrial contaminants while maintaining the material\'s original surface morphology and mechanical properties."
