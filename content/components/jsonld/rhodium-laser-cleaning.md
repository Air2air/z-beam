  "articleBody": "Rhodium is a rare, corrosion-resistant platinum group metal with exceptional hardness, high reflectivity (80-90% across visible spectrum), and remarkable chemical inertness, making it ideal for precision optical components, jewelry plating, and electrical contacts. Laser cleaning offers a non-contact, selective ablation method that removes contaminants, oxides, and surface impurities without compromising Rhodium's critical surface properties. Optimal laser parameters for Rhodium cleaning include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds, allowing precise control over material removal depth. Key applications include restoration of historical Rhodium-plated artifacts, cleaning of optical mirrors and reflectors, preparation of electrical contacts, and maintenance of industrial catalyst surfaces. Challenges include Rhodium's high thermal conductivity (150 W/m·K) requiring precise energy delivery to avoid subsurface damage, and its reflectivity necessitating optimized beam absorption characteristics. Technical solutions involve pulsed laser systems with real-time monitoring and closed-loop feedback control. Safety considerations mandate Class 4 laser protocols with appropriate eyewear (OD 7+ at 1064 nm), fume extraction for airborne particulates, and controlled environments to prevent accidental reflection hazards.
