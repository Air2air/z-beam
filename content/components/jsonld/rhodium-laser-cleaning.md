  "articleBody": "Rhodium is a rare, corrosion-resistant platinum group metal with exceptional hardness, high reflectivity (80-90% across visible spectrum), and superior catalytic properties. These characteristics make it ideal for industrial catalysts, jewelry plating, electrical contacts, and optical components. Laser cleaning of rhodium requires precise parameter optimization due to its high melting point (1964°C) and thermal conductivity (150 W/m·K). Optimal laser parameters typically include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence ranges of 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 ns to effectively remove organic contaminants, oxidation layers, and surface impurities without damaging the underlying substrate. The process effectively cleans industrial catalytic converters where carbon buildup reduces efficiency, restores antique rhodium-plated jewelry without abrasive damage, and maintains electrical contacts in high-performance applications. Key challenges include managing rhodium's high reflectivity through appropriate wavelength selection and preventing thermal stress cracking through controlled pulse overlapping. Safety protocols require Class 4 laser safety measures including appropriate eyewear, ventilation for aerosolized contaminants, and containment of potentially toxic ablation byproducts. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning preserves rhodium's thin plating layers and maintains surface integrity for critical applications.",
