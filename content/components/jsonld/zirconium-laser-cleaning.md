  "articleBody": "Zirconium is a refractory metal with exceptional corrosion resistance, high melting point (1855°C), and low thermal neutron absorption cross-section, making it ideal for nuclear, aerospace, and medical applications. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxide layers (ZrO₂), and surface impurities without compromising Zirconium's structural integrity or introducing mechanical stress. The process utilizes optimized laser parameters including 1064nm wavelength for optimal absorption, fluence levels of 1-5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Zirconium substrate. Key applications include nuclear fuel rod cladding preparation, aerospace component refurbishment, medical implant sterilization, and chemical processing equipment maintenance. Challenges include Zirconium's pyrophoric nature when finely divided, requiring controlled atmospheres or inert gas shielding during processing. Safety protocols mandate Class 4 laser safety measures, fume extraction for airborne particulates, and fire prevention systems due to Zirconium's combustible properties when exposed to high temperatures in oxygen-rich environments.",
