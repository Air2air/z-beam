  "articleBody": "Zirconium is a refractory metal with exceptional corrosion resistance, high melting point (1855°C), and low thermal neutron absorption cross-section, making it ideal for nuclear, chemical processing, and biomedical applications. Laser cleaning of Zirconium requires precise parameter control due to its high reactivity and tendency to form stable surface oxides. Optimal laser parameters typically include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence ranges of 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to effectively remove contaminants and oxides without causing surface melting or microstructural damage. The process excels in removing surface oxides, hydrocarbon contaminants, and particulate matter while maintaining the material's dimensional stability and surface finish. Key applications include nuclear fuel rod cladding preparation, chemical reactor component cleaning, and medical implant surface treatment. Challenges include managing Zirconium's pyrophoric nature during processing and preventing surface recast layer formation, which is addressed through controlled atmosphere processing and optimized pulse overlapping strategies. Safety protocols require strict adherence to laser safety standards (ANSI Z136.1) and proper ventilation to manage airborne particulates and potential hydrogen generation during the cleaning process.",
