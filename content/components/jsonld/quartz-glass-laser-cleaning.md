articleBody: "Quartz glass laser cleaning utilizes pulsed laser technology to remove organic residues, oxides, and particulate contaminants from high-purity silica surfaces. This non-contact method preserves the material's exceptional UV transmission properties (typically 90% at 185 nm wavelength) while eliminating traditional cleaning risks like surface scratching or chemical etching. The process operates at controlled energy densities between 0.5-3.0 J/cm², with pulse durations optimized for quartz's low thermal expansion coefficient (5.5×10⁻⁷/°C). Industrial applications range from semiconductor wafer production to precision optics manufacturing, where surface cleanliness below 0.1 μm particulate levels is critical. Modern systems integrate real-time surface monitoring with 1064 nm or 532 nm wavelength lasers, achieving >99.9% contaminant removal without altering the glass's 1670°C softening point characteristics."
