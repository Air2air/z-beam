  "articleBody": "Breccia is a clastic sedimentary rock composed of large, angular fragments cemented together by a fine-grained matrix, typically consisting of calcite, silica, or iron oxides. This heterogeneous composition presents unique challenges for laser cleaning, as different components have varying absorption coefficients and ablation thresholds. Optimal laser parameters for Breccia cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with moderate fluence levels (0.5-2.0 J/cm²) and short pulse durations (10-100 ns) to selectively remove contaminants without damaging the underlying matrix. The process effectively eliminates biological growth, atmospheric pollution crusts, and previous restoration materials while preserving the stone's natural patina and structural integrity. Key applications include architectural restoration, monument conservation, and archaeological artifact preparation, where precision cleaning is essential. Challenges include managing the differential response between cementing matrix and clasts, requiring careful parameter optimization through spectral analysis and real-time monitoring.
