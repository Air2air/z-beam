  "articleBody": "Pyrex, a borosilicate glass material, is widely used in industrial and laboratory applications due to its exceptional thermal shock resistance, chemical durability, and optical clarity. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, surface oxidation, and biological residues without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064 nm wavelength with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Pyrex substrate. Key applications include cleaning laboratory glassware, optical components, semiconductor manufacturing equipment, and pharmaceutical processing vessels where absolute cleanliness is critical. The primary challenge in Pyrex laser cleaning involves managing thermal stress to prevent micro-cracking, achieved through precise control of pulse duration (10-100 ns) and repetition rates (10-100 kHz). Safety considerations include proper laser shielding, fume extraction for ablated contaminants, and eye protection against reflected laser radiation.
