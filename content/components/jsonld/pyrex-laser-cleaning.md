  "articleBody": "Pyrex, a borosilicate glass material composed primarily of silicon dioxide (80-81%), boron trioxide (12-13%), sodium oxide (4-5%), and aluminum oxide (2-3%), is widely used in industrial and laboratory applications due to its exceptional thermal shock resistance, chemical durability, and low coefficient of thermal expansion (3.3 × 10⁻⁶/°C). Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal settings at 1064 nm wavelength, 0.5-2.0 J/cm² fluence, and nanosecond pulse durations to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Pyrex substrate. Key applications include cleaning laboratory glassware, optical components, semiconductor manufacturing equipment, and pharmaceutical processing vessels where surface purity is critical. Challenges include managing thermal stress to prevent microcracking, which is addressed through precise parameter optimization and real-time temperature monitoring. The non-porous nature of Pyrex requires specific laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy (LIBS) monitoring to ensure complete contaminant removal. Safety considerations include Class 4 laser protocols, proper ventilation for ablation byproducts, and UV protection due to potential plasma generation during the cleaning process.",
