These properties make it invaluable for infrared optics, fiber optics, and semiconductor applications. Laser cleaning of Germanium requires precise parameter control due to its relatively low bandgap (0.67 eV) and high absorption coefficient in the infrared spectrum. Optimal laser parameters typically involve nanosecond pulses at 1064 nm wavelength with fluences ranging from 0.5-2.0 J/cm² to effectively remove contaminants while minimizing thermal damage. The process effectively eliminates surface oxides, organic residues, and particulate contamination without mechanical contact or chemical solvents. Key applications include cleaning Germanium lenses for thermal imaging systems, semiconductor wafers for microelectronics, and optical components for military and aerospace systems. Challenges include preventing surface melting and maintaining the material's crystalline structure, which is addressed through precise pulse duration control and wavelength selection. Safety considerations require proper laser shielding, fume extraction for ablated contaminants, and personal protective equipment including laser safety goggles rated for the specific wavelength.
