  "articleBody": "Float Glass, primarily composed of silica (SiO₂ ~72%), soda (Na₂O ~14%), and lime (CaO ~9%), presents unique challenges for laser cleaning due to its thermal expansion coefficient of 8.5-9.0 × 10⁻⁶/K and annealing point of approximately 550°C. Optimal laser parameters for Float Glass cleaning typically utilize nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064 nm wavelength with fluence levels carefully controlled between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to avoid micro-cracking and thermal stress. Pulse durations of 10-100 ns provide sufficient energy for contaminant ablation while minimizing heat transfer to the substrate. Key applications include architectural glass restoration, automotive glass manufacturing, and display panel production where surface purity is critical. The primary challenge involves the material's low thermal shock resistance, requiring precise energy deposition to prevent fracture formation. Technical solutions include scanning systems with overlapping rates below 30% and active cooling mechanisms. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 laser enclosures, proper fume extraction for evaporated contaminants, and comprehensive operator training in handling optical materials.
