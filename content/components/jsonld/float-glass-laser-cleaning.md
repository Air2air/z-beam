  "articleBody": "Float Glass, produced through the Pilkington process, is characterized by its exceptional surface flatness, optical clarity, and uniform thickness, making it ideal for architectural, automotive, and display applications. Laser cleaning provides a superior alternative to traditional mechanical and chemical methods by offering precise, non-contact removal of surface contaminants, organic residues, and oxidation layers without compromising the material's structural integrity or optical properties. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064 nm wavelength with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 ns. These parameters are optimized to selectively ablate contaminants while minimizing thermal stress and preventing micro-cracking in the glass substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical glass structures, precision cleaning of optical components, and preparation of glass surfaces for subsequent coating or bonding processes. Challenges include managing thermal expansion differences, preventing laser-induced damage to the pristine glass surface, and ensuring complete contaminant removal without residual ablation products. Safety considerations mandate proper laser shielding, fume extraction systems, and operator protection against reflected laser radiation and particulate matter generated during the cleaning process.",
