  "articleBody": "Indium Phosphide (InP) is a III-V semiconductor compound with exceptional electronic and optoelectronic properties, including high electron mobility and direct bandgap of 1.35 eV, making it ideal for high-frequency transistors, photodetectors, and laser diodes. Laser cleaning of InP requires precise parameter optimization due to its relatively low thermal conductivity (0.68 W/cm·K) and thermal expansion coefficient (4.5 × 10⁻⁶/K). Optimal laser parameters typically include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.0 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to effectively remove surface contaminants, oxides, and particulates without inducing thermal damage or altering the material's stoichiometry. Key applications include preparation of InP wafers for epitaxial growth, cleaning of photonic device surfaces, and removal of processing residues from microwave integrated circuits. Challenges include avoiding phosphorus depletion from the surface, preventing thermal stress cracking, and maintaining surface roughness below 1 nm RMS. Safety considerations involve proper ventilation to manage phosphorus oxide byproducts, laser safety protocols for Class 4 laser systems, and appropriate personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser goggles and fume extraction systems.",
