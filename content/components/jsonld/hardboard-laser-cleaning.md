  "articleBody": "Hardboard is a high-density wood composite material manufactured through steam pressing and heat treatment of wood fibers. Its uniform density (approximately 900-1000 kg/m³) and smooth surface make it ideal for laser cleaning applications. The material's cellulose and lignin composition responds well to specific laser wavelengths, particularly near-infrared (1064 nm) and green (532 nm) lasers. Optimal laser parameters for Hardboard cleaning include fluence levels of 0.8-1.5 J/cm², pulse durations of 50-150 ns, and repetition rates of 20-50 kHz. These parameters effectively remove surface contaminants, adhesives, and coatings while minimizing thermal penetration that could cause charring or discoloration. Key applications include furniture manufacturing restoration, architectural component cleaning, and industrial mold preparation. Challenges include controlling heat accumulation to prevent resin degradation and maintaining consistent surface quality across the material's grain structure. Technical solutions involve precise beam scanning patterns, real-time temperature monitoring, and adaptive power control systems. Safety considerations require proper ventilation to manage particulate emissions, laser safety enclosures, and appropriate personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser goggles. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning preserves Hardboard's dimensional stability and surface integrity while achieving superior cleaning results compared to traditional mechanical or chemical methods.",
