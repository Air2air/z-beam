  "articleBody": "Concrete is a composite masonry material composed of cement, water, and aggregates (sand, gravel, or crushed stone) that forms a rigid structure through hydration. Its heterogeneous composition and porous surface present unique challenges for laser cleaning, requiring precise parameter optimization. The optimal laser parameters for Concrete cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds. These parameters effectively remove surface contaminants, biological growth, paint, and graffiti while minimizing thermal damage to the cement matrix. Key applications include historical monument restoration, industrial facility maintenance, bridge infrastructure cleaning, and architectural surface preparation. The primary challenges include controlling heat absorption differentials between aggregate particles and cement paste, preventing micro-cracking from thermal stress, and addressing surface porosity variations. Technical solutions involve scanning systems with real-time monitoring, adjustable pulse repetition rates, and controlled beam overlap patterns. Safety considerations mandate comprehensive eye protection (OD7+ for 1064 nm), proper ventilation for particulate matter control, and containment systems for laser-generated aerosols.
