  "articleBody": "Serpentine (Mg₃Si₂O₅(OH)₄) is a hydrous magnesium phyllosilicate mineral widely used in architectural, decorative, and industrial applications due to its heat resistance, durability, and distinctive green coloration. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, biological growth, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity or altering its mineral composition. Optimal laser parameters for Serpentine cleaning typically utilize near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical Serpentine monuments, preparation of industrial components, and surface treatment of decorative elements. Challenges include managing the material's variable porosity and thermal sensitivity, which require precise parameter optimization to prevent micro-fracturing or discoloration. Safety protocols mandate Class 1 laser enclosure systems, proper ventilation to manage particulate matter, and operator training in mineral-specific laser-material interactions.
