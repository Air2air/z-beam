  "articleBody": "Travertine, a form of limestone deposited by mineral springs, possesses a porous structure with varying densities and composition primarily consisting of calcium carbonate (CaCO₃). This chemical composition makes it sensitive to traditional cleaning methods that can cause etching, discoloration, or structural damage. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact alternative that selectively removes contaminants, biological growth, and surface deposits without compromising the substrate. Optimal laser parameters for Travertine typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds, carefully calibrated to avoid thermal damage or calcium carbonate decomposition. The process effectively removes black crusts, pollution deposits, organic growth, and previous restoration treatments while preserving the natural patina and surface texture. Key applications include architectural restoration, cultural heritage preservation, and monument conservation where maintaining original surface characteristics is critical. Challenges include addressing the material's porosity variations and preventing thermal stress, which are mitigated through precise parameter control and real-time monitoring systems.
