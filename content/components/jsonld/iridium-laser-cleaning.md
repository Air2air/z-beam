  "articleBody": "Iridium is a platinum-group metal renowned for its exceptional properties, including extreme hardness (Mohs 6.5), the highest corrosion resistance of all metals, and a remarkably high melting point of 2,446°C. These characteristics make Iridium particularly challenging yet essential to clean using precision laser techniques. Optimal laser parameters for Iridium cleaning typically involve nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064nm wavelength with fluence levels between 2-8 J/cm² and pulse durations of 50-150ns. The process selectively removes surface contaminants, oxides, and organic residues without compromising the underlying material's structural integrity. Key applications include aerospace components, medical implants, scientific instrumentation, and high-temperature crucibles where surface purity is critical. Challenges include Iridium's high reflectivity to certain laser wavelengths and thermal management requirements, addressed through precise parameter optimization and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations mandate Class 4 laser protocols with appropriate personal protective equipment, fume extraction systems for potentially hazardous ablation byproducts, and controlled environments to prevent surface contamination.
