  "articleBody": "Iridium is a platinum-group metal with exceptional properties including extreme hardness (Mohs 6.5), high melting point (2446°C), and remarkable chemical resistance, making it ideal for high-performance applications but challenging to clean using conventional methods. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact solution for removing contaminants, oxide layers, and surface impurities without compromising Iridium's structural integrity. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064nm wavelength with fluence levels between 1-4 J/cm², which selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Iridium substrate. Key applications include aerospace components, medical implants, scientific instrumentation, and semiconductor manufacturing where ultra-high purity surfaces are critical. Challenges include Iridium's high reflectivity and thermal conductivity, requiring optimized pulse durations (10-100ns) and repetition rates (20-100kHz) to achieve effective cleaning without thermal damage. Safety considerations mandate Class 4 laser protocols with appropriate eyewear, ventilation for ablation byproducts, and controlled environments to prevent oxidation during processing.
