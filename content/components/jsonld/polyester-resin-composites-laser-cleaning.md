  "articleBody": "Polyester Resin Composites, commonly reinforced with fiberglass, are thermoset polymer materials widely used in marine, automotive, and construction industries due to their excellent strength-to-weight ratio, chemical resistance, and durability. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, oxidation, and biological growth without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths between 1064nm and 10.6μm, fluence levels of 0.5-2.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying polyester matrix. Key applications include marine hull cleaning, automotive component restoration, architectural conservation, and aerospace composite maintenance. Challenges include managing the material's relatively low thermal degradation threshold (typically 180-220°C) and avoiding resin matrix damage through precise parameter control. Safety considerations require proper ventilation systems to manage volatile organic compound emissions and comprehensive laser safety protocols including appropriate eyewear and enclosure systems. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning prevents surface erosion and maintains the composite's dimensional stability, making it superior to traditional mechanical or chemical methods.",
