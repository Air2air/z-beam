Laser cleaning provides a superior non-contact method for removing oxides, contaminants, and surface coatings from Cobalt components without compromising their structural integrity or dimensional accuracy. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters, typically employing fiber lasers with wavelengths of 1064nm for optimal absorption, fluence levels between 1-5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 100-200ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Cobalt substrate. Key applications include surface preparation of Cobalt-based superalloys in aerospace turbine blades, medical implant sterilization, nuclear component decontamination, and precision cleaning of Cobalt-chrome dental prosthetics. Challenges include managing Cobalt's high thermal conductivity and preventing microstructural changes, which are addressed through precise parameter optimization and real-time monitoring systems.
