  "articleBody": "Borosilicate Glass is a specialized glass material composed of silica and boron trioxide, widely used in industrial and scientific applications due to its excellent thermal shock resistance, low coefficient of thermal expansion (3.3 × 10⁻⁶/K), and high chemical durability. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, oxides, and organic residues without compromising the material's structural integrity or optical clarity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters including 1064 nm wavelength for optimal absorption, fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100 ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Borosilicate Glass substrate. Critical applications include cleaning laboratory glassware, optical components, pharmaceutical containers, and semiconductor manufacturing equipment where surface purity is essential. Challenges include managing thermal stress to prevent microcracking, addressing the material's transparency to certain wavelengths, and ensuring uniform cleaning across complex geometries. Technical solutions involve precise parameter optimization, real-time monitoring systems, and controlled scanning patterns. Safety considerations require Class 1 laser enclosures, appropriate PPE including laser safety glasses, fume extraction systems for airborne particulates, and comprehensive operator training in laser safety protocols (ANSI Z136.1 standards).",
