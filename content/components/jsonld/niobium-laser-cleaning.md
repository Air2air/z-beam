  "articleBody": "Niobium is a refractory metal with exceptional properties including high melting point (2468°C), excellent corrosion resistance, and superior superconducting characteristics when cooled below 9.3K. These properties make it ideal for particle accelerator cavities, superconducting magnets, and nuclear applications. Laser cleaning provides a non-contact, precision method for removing surface contaminants, oxides, and particulate matter without compromising Niobium's critical surface properties. Optimal laser parameters typically involve fiber lasers operating at 1064nm wavelength with fluence levels between 1-4 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-200ns, effectively ablating contaminants while minimizing heat-affected zones. The process must carefully control thermal input to prevent surface roughening or microstructural changes that could degrade superconducting performance. Key applications include preparing RF cavities for particle accelerators, cleaning superconducting magnet components, and surface treatment for medical implant manufacturing. Challenges include managing reflectivity (approximately 60% at 1064nm), preventing surface oxidation during processing, and maintaining sub-micron surface smoothness. Safety protocols require Class 4 laser safety measures including appropriate eyewear, fume extraction for ablated particulates, and controlled processing environments to prevent surface contamination.",
