  "articleBody": "Niobium is a refractory metal with exceptional properties including high melting point (2468°C), excellent corrosion resistance, and superior superconducting characteristics. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising Niobium's structural integrity or superconducting performance. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal settings at 1064nm wavelength, 0.5-2.5 J/cm² fluence, and 10-100ns pulse duration to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Niobium substrate. Key applications include superconducting radio frequency (SRF) cavities for particle accelerators, medical implants requiring sterilization, aerospace components, and chemical processing equipment. Challenges include managing Niobium's high thermal conductivity (53.7 W/m·K) and preventing surface recast layer formation, which is addressed through precise parameter optimization and inert gas shielding. Safety considerations mandate Class 4 laser protocols with appropriate PPE, fume extraction systems for NbO/NbO₂ particulate matter, and explosion-proof enclosures when processing hydrogen-containing contaminants. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical stress and prevents surface damage critical for maintaining Niobium's superconducting properties in high-value applications.",
