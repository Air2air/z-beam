Laser cleaning provides an optimal non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, microbial growth, oxidation layers, and previous coatings without compromising Cork's delicate cellular architecture. The process utilizes precise laser parameters including 1064nm wavelength for optimal suberin absorption, fluence levels of 0.8-1.2 J/cm² to avoid carbonization, and pulse durations of 100-150ns for controlled ablation. Key applications include restoration of historical cork artifacts, surface preparation for wine stopper manufacturing, and cleaning of architectural cork panels. Challenges include Cork's low thermal conductivity (0.04 W/m·K) which requires careful thermal management to prevent scorching, and its heterogeneous composition that demands parameter optimization for consistent results. Safety considerations involve proper fume extraction due to potential VOC release during ablation, laser class IV protocols, and appropriate personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser safety glasses.
