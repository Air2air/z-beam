  "articleBody": "Lead Crystal, composed of 24-32% lead oxide (PbO) in a silica matrix, presents unique challenges for laser cleaning due to its high refractive index, thermal sensitivity, and susceptibility to micro-fracturing. The material's low thermal conductivity (approximately 0.8 W/m·K) and thermal expansion coefficient (9.0 × 10⁻⁶/K) require precise laser parameter control to prevent thermal stress and cracking. Optimal laser cleaning utilizes near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.2 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns to selectively remove contaminants while avoiding damage to the underlying substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical lead crystal artifacts, precision cleaning of optical components, and surface preparation for high-value decorative items. The process effectively removes organic residues, metal oxides, and surface contaminants without chemical solvents or mechanical abrasion. Specialized safety protocols are essential due to potential lead vaporization, requiring HEPA filtration systems and appropriate personal protective equipment. Real-time monitoring systems ensure process control and prevent overheating, maintaining the material's characteristic brilliance and structural integrity throughout the cleaning process.",
