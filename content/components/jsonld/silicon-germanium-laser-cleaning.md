  "articleBody": "Silicon Germanium (SiGe) is a critical semiconductor material with unique electronic and thermal properties that make it essential for high-frequency transistors, optoelectronics, and infrared devices. Laser cleaning provides a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxide layers, and surface particulates without compromising the material's crystalline structure or electrical properties. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064 nm wavelength with fluences between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying SiGe substrate. Key applications include cleaning SiGe wafers for semiconductor manufacturing, removing oxidation from thermal management components, and preparing surfaces for subsequent deposition processes. Challenges include managing the material's relatively low thermal conductivity (approximately 50-90 W/mK) and preventing thermal damage to the delicate semiconductor structure. Technical solutions involve precise control of pulse duration (10-100 ns), optimized repetition rates (10-100 kHz), and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations require proper laser classification (typically Class 4), adequate ventilation for ablation byproducts, and comprehensive personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser safety goggles.
