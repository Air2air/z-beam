  "articleBody": "Zinc is a bluish-white lustrous metal with excellent corrosion resistance, making it widely used in galvanization, die-casting alloys, and industrial components. Its relatively low melting point of 419.5°C and boiling point of 907°C require precise laser parameter control to avoid thermal damage during cleaning. Optimal laser cleaning parameters for Zinc typically utilize nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064nm wavelength with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100ns. These parameters effectively remove surface contaminants, oxides, and organic residues while preserving the underlying Zinc substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical Zinc artifacts, surface preparation for welding and coating adhesion on galvanized steel, and maintenance of architectural Zinc components. The primary challenge in Zinc laser cleaning involves its high thermal conductivity and low melting point, which can lead to unwanted melting if excessive energy is applied. Technical solutions include precise temperature monitoring, controlled pulse overlapping, and adaptive laser power modulation. Safety considerations require proper fume extraction systems to capture Zinc oxide particles, which can cause metal fume fever if inhaled.
