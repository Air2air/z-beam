  "articleBody": "Phenolic Resin Composites are thermoset polymer composites widely used in industrial applications due to their excellent thermal stability, chemical resistance, and mechanical strength. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths between 1064-1070 nm (fiber lasers) or 10.6 μm (CO2 lasers), fluence levels of 0.5-2.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100 ns to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying phenolic substrate. Key applications include aerospace component refurbishment, electrical insulation cleaning, automotive part restoration, and mold maintenance. Challenges include preventing thermal degradation of the resin matrix, which requires precise control of laser parameters and thermal management through pulsed operation with adequate cooling intervals. Safety considerations mandate proper ventilation systems to manage volatile organic compounds released during ablation, along with Class 1 laser safety enclosures and appropriate personal protective equipment including laser safety goggles rated for the specific wavelength.",
