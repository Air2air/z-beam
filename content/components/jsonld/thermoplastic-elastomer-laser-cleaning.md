  "articleBody": "Thermoplastic Elastomer (TPE) is a versatile composite material combining the processing advantages of thermoplastics with the elastic properties of rubbers, widely used in automotive, medical, and consumer applications. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, release agents, mold residues, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity or elastic properties. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal wavelengths between 1064-1070nm (fiber lasers) and fluence levels of 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying TPE substrate. Pulse durations of 100-200ns provide sufficient energy for contaminant removal without causing thermal degradation or surface melting. Key applications include medical device manufacturing where sterile surfaces are critical, automotive component preparation for bonding, and restoration of consumer products. Challenges include the material's low thermal conductivity and sensitivity to heat, requiring precise parameter control to prevent surface deformation. Safety considerations include proper fume extraction due to potential VOC release and appropriate laser shielding to protect operators. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical stress on flexible TPE components, making it ideal for delicate applications where traditional methods could cause deformation or damage.",
