  "articleBody": "Birch (Betula spp.) is a dense hardwood material characterized by its fine grain, pale color, and excellent mechanical properties, making it suitable for various industrial applications. The material's chemical composition, primarily cellulose (40-45%), hemicellulose (25-30%), and lignin (20-25%), responds well to laser cleaning when proper parameters are applied. Optimal laser cleaning of Birch typically utilizes near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns to effectively remove contaminants, coatings, and surface oxidation without causing thermal damage or charring. Key applications include restoration of historical Birch artifacts, preparation of Birch surfaces for finishing in furniture manufacturing, and cleaning of precision Birch components in musical instrument production. Challenges include managing the material's variable density and preventing surface burning, which are addressed through precise parameter control and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations require proper ventilation to manage particulate matter, laser safety enclosures, and appropriate personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser goggles. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning preserves Birch's structural integrity while achieving superior cleaning results compared to traditional abrasive methods.",
