articleBody: "Beryllium is a lightweight, high-strength metal with exceptional thermal conductivity and dimensional stability, making it valuable in aerospace, defense, and nuclear applications. Laser cleaning provides an optimal surface treatment method for Beryllium components, utilizing precise wavelength control (typically 1064nm Nd-YAG lasers) and pulse durations to selectively remove contaminants without mechanical contact. The process effectively eliminates surface oxides, lubricants, and particulate matter while maintaining the material\'s critical mechanical properties. Key parameters include fluence levels of 2-8 J/cm², pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds, and repetition rates optimized for specific contamination types. This non-abrasive technique prevents surface damage and eliminates secondary waste generation, crucial for Beryllium\'s toxicity concerns. Laser cleaning enables restoration of surface finish to Ra < 0.4μm while preserving dimensional tolerances within ±5μm. The method is particularly valuable for precision components in optical systems, X-ray equipment, and satellite structures where surface integrity is paramount. Advanced monitoring systems ensure complete contaminant removal while preventing overheating that could compromise Beryllium\'s crystalline structure."
