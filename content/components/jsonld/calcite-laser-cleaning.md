  "articleBody": "Calcite (CaCO₃) is a calcium carbonate mineral widely used in industrial applications, construction, and art due to its optical clarity, chemical stability, and workability. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, biological growth, coatings, and surface oxidation without compromising the material's structural integrity or causing thermal damage. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying Calcite substrate. Key applications include restoration of calcite-based artworks, cleaning of optical components, preparation of geological specimens, and surface treatment in industrial manufacturing. Challenges include Calcite's relatively low thermal stability and susceptibility to thermal shock, which require precise parameter optimization.
