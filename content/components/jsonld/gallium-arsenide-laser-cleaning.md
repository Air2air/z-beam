  "articleBody": "Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) is a III-V direct bandgap semiconductor with exceptional electronic properties, including high electron mobility and saturated electron velocity, making it ideal for high-frequency and optoelectronic applications. Laser cleaning provides a non-contact, precise method for removing surface contaminants, oxides, and residues without compromising the material's crystalline structure. Optimal laser parameters typically include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.0 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to selectively ablate contaminants while minimizing thermal damage to the GaAs substrate. Key applications include cleaning GaAs wafers for microwave integrated circuits, laser diodes, solar cells, and photodetectors. Challenges include GaAs's relatively low thermal conductivity and susceptibility to thermal decomposition, requiring precise control of laser energy absorption and thermal management. Safety considerations involve proper ventilation to prevent arsenic oxide nanoparticle exposure, Class 4 laser safety protocols, and appropriate personal protective equipment including laser safety goggles and respiratory protection.",
