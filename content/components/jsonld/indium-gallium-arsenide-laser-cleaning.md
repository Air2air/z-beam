  "articleBody": "Indium Gallium Arsenide (InGaAs) is a ternary semiconductor compound with exceptional electron mobility and direct bandgap properties, making it ideal for high-frequency optoelectronic applications. Laser cleaning provides a non-contact, selective method for removing surface contaminants, oxides, and residual layers without compromising the delicate InGaAs crystal structure. Optimal laser parameters typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluences between 0.5-2.0 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to achieve controlled ablation while minimizing thermal damage. The process effectively removes organic residues, native oxides, and particulate contamination from InGaAs surfaces used in photodetectors, infrared lasers, and high-electron-mobility transistors. Key challenges include managing the material's relatively low thermal conductivity (0.05 W/cm·K) and preventing surface decomposition through precise energy control. Safety protocols require appropriate laser class enclosures, fume extraction systems for arsenic-containing byproducts, and proper personal protective equipment. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning preserves the material's electrical properties and surface morphology, making it superior to chemical or mechanical methods for sensitive semiconductor applications.",
