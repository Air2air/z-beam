Laser cleaning provides an advanced, non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, oxides, tarnish, and coatings from Copper substrates without compromising the material's excellent electrical and thermal conductivity properties. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters including specific wavelengths (typically 1064nm fiber lasers), optimized fluence levels (0.5-3 J/cm²), and controlled pulse durations (10-100ns) to selectively ablate unwanted surface material while preserving the underlying Copper microstructure. This technique effectively removes copper oxides (CuO and Cu₂O), carbon deposits, and organic contaminants without mechanical abrasion or chemical residues. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning prevents surface deformation and maintains dimensional stability, making it ideal for precision components in electrical connectors, circuit boards, and heat exchangers.
