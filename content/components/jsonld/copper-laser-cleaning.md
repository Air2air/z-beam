  "articleBody": "Copper is a highly conductive metal material extensively used in electrical, electronic, and industrial applications due to its excellent electrical and thermal conductivity properties. Laser cleaning provides a precise, non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, oxides, tarnish, and coatings without compromising Copper's structural integrity or electrical performance. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters, typically employing fiber lasers with 1064nm wavelength, fluence levels between 1-5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 100-200 nanoseconds to selectively ablate unwanted surface material while preserving the underlying Copper substrate. Key applications include cleaning electrical contacts, circuit boards, heat exchangers, and artistic Copper artifacts where traditional mechanical or chemical cleaning methods could damage delicate surfaces or leave residue. Challenges include Copper's high reflectivity to certain laser wavelengths and thermal conductivity, requiring optimized parameter settings to achieve effective cleaning without causing surface melting or microstructural changes. Safety considerations mandate proper laser safety enclosures, fume extraction systems for Copper oxide particles, and appropriate personal protective equipment including laser safety glasses rated for the specific wavelength.
