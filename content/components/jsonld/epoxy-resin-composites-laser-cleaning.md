  "articleBody": "Epoxy Resin Composites, consisting of epoxy polymer matrices reinforced with fibers such as carbon, glass, or aramid, present unique challenges for surface cleaning due to their heterogeneous structure and thermal sensitivity. Laser cleaning offers a superior alternative to mechanical and chemical methods by providing precise, non-contact removal of contaminants, coatings, and oxidation layers without compromising the composite's structural integrity. Optimal laser parameters typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with controlled fluence levels (0.5-2.5 J/cm²) and short pulse durations (nanosecond to picosecond range) to selectively ablate surface contaminants while minimizing thermal damage to the epoxy matrix. The process effectively removes release agents, adhesives, paint layers, and environmental contaminants from aerospace components, automotive parts, wind turbine blades, and marine structures. Key challenges include managing the different ablation thresholds of epoxy resin and reinforcement fibers, preventing matrix degradation, and avoiding fiber exposure. Technical solutions involve precise parameter optimization, real-time monitoring systems, and controlled beam scanning patterns. Safety considerations require proper ventilation systems to manage off-gassing, laser safety enclosures, and personal protective equipment for operators. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning preserves the composite's mechanical properties and surface quality while enabling superior adhesion for subsequent coating or bonding processes.",
