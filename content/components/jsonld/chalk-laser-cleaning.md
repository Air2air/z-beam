Its high porosity and relatively low thermal conductivity require precise laser parameter optimization to achieve effective contaminant removal without causing thermal damage or surface alteration. Optimal laser cleaning parameters for Chalk typically utilize near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with moderate fluence levels (0.5-1.5 J/cm²) and short pulse durations (10-100 ns) to selectively remove surface contaminants, biological growth, and atmospheric deposits while preserving the underlying calcite structure. The process effectively eliminates microbial colonization, soot accumulation, and surface discoloration without compromising the material's natural porosity or mechanical properties. Key industrial applications include architectural restoration of historical chalk structures, precision cleaning of industrial chalk components, and surface preparation for subsequent treatments. Technical challenges include managing the material's thermal sensitivity and ensuring uniform cleaning across porous surfaces, which are addressed through precise beam scanning patterns and real-time monitoring systems.
