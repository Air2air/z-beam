  "articleBody": "Silver (Ag) is a precious metal with exceptional electrical conductivity, thermal conductivity, and reflectivity, making it valuable in numerous industrial applications including electronics, jewelry, and medical devices. Laser cleaning provides an advanced, non-contact method for removing contaminants, tarnish, oxidation layers, and coatings from Silver surfaces without compromising the material's structural integrity or fine surface details. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters optimized for Silver's specific properties, including its high reflectivity and thermal conductivity. Optimal parameters typically involve nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064nm wavelength with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm², pulse durations of 10-100ns, and repetition rates of 20-100kHz. These parameters ensure selective ablation of contaminants while minimizing heat transfer to the Silver substrate. Key applications include cleaning of electrical contacts, restoration of historical Silver artifacts, preparation of jewelry surfaces, and removal of oxidation from industrial Silver components. Challenges include managing Silver's high reflectivity, which requires precise wavelength selection and angle optimization, and preventing surface melting or discoloration through careful parameter control. Safety considerations include proper laser shielding, fume extraction for removed contaminants, and eye protection against reflected laser radiation. The process effectively removes silver sulfide tarnish (Ag₂S) and other surface compounds while preserving the underlying metal's microstructure and surface quality.",
