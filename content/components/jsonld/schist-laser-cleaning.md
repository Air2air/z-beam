  "articleBody": "Schist is a medium-grade metamorphic rock characterized by its foliated structure composed of platy minerals such as mica, chlorite, and talc. This distinct layered composition presents unique challenges for laser cleaning, as different mineral components exhibit varying absorption characteristics. Optimal laser parameters for Schist cleaning typically utilize near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns. These parameters effectively remove surface contaminants, biological growth, and oxidation layers while minimizing thermal stress on the delicate mineral structure. Key applications include architectural restoration, museum artifact conservation, and industrial component refurbishment where precision cleaning is essential. The primary challenge involves the differential absorption rates between mineral components, requiring carefully calibrated laser settings to prevent selective ablation of specific minerals. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 laser enclosure systems with integrated fume extraction to handle particulate matter generated during the cleaning process.
