  "articleBody": "Gallium Nitride (GaN) is a wide bandgap semiconductor material with exceptional thermal stability, high electron mobility, and chemical resistance, making it ideal for high-power electronics, optoelectronics, and LED applications. Laser cleaning provides a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxide layers, and residual coatings without compromising GaN's crystalline structure. The process utilizes optimized laser parameters including 355nm UV wavelength for high absorption, fluence levels of 0.5-2.0 J/cm² to avoid substrate damage, and nanosecond pulse durations for controlled material removal. Key applications include surface preparation for epitaxial growth, removal of photoresist residues from GaN wafers, and cleaning of high-electron-mobility transistors (HEMTs). Challenges include GaN's transparency to near-IR wavelengths requiring UV lasers, thermal management to prevent decomposition, and maintaining stoichiometric surface composition. Technical solutions involve wavelength optimization, precise energy density control, and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 laser enclosures, proper ventilation for gallium-containing byproducts, and personal protective equipment including laser safety goggles with appropriate optical density for the specific wavelength employed.",
