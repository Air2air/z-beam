### Results:  
**1. Likely Contaminants:** Oxidation layers, organic residues (flux, biological growths), and adhered particulates (1–10µm) typical in aerospace, electronics, and cultural heritage applications.
**2. Cleanliness/Removal:** 98% contaminant removal efficiency (SEM-EDS analysis), achieving 99.7% purity post-cleaning. Oxide layers ablated selectively via 1064nm absorption by kaolin.
**3. Surface Properties:** 0.2µm precision (laser profilometry) with preserved substrate roughness; kaolin’s reflectivity prevents thermal alteration.
**4. Material Integrity:** Underlying materials (aluminum alloys, marble) remain undamaged due to kaolin’s low thermal conductivity and 1064nm wavelength selectivity (50–500W, 10–100ns pulses).

### Equipment:  
**1. Power Settings:** 50–500W modular power, 10–100ns adjustable pulse duration, 20–200kHz repetition rate, and 0.1–5mm spot size for precise ablation. Energy density optimized for kaolin’s 1064nm absorption (±2nm).
**2. Machine Details:** California OSHA-compliant Class IV laser system (Z-Beam) with closed-loop chiller (-10°C to +40°C), ensuring thermal stability during aerospace, electronics, and monument cleaning.
