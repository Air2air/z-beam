### Key Properties of Rubber in Laser Cleaning

Rubber's physical and chemical properties dictate its response to laser cleaning. At 1064 nm, Rubber exhibits significant absorption, a crucial factor for effective cleaning. Its thermal threshold, typically around 100-150°C, must be carefully managed to avoid damage. The material's elasticity and resilience, much like bamboo in a storm, allow it to withstand the thermal shock from laser pulses. However, the presence of additives and fillers in Rubber can alter its laser absorption and thermal properties, necessitating adjustments in cleaning parameters.

### Optimal Laser Parameters for Cleaning Rubber

To clean Rubber effectively, one must fine-tune the laser parameters. The wavelength of 1064 nm is optimal due to Rubber's absorption characteristics. Pulse duration typically ranges from 10 to 100 nanoseconds, allowing for controlled ablation without excessive heat buildup. Fluence, the energy density of the laser, should be kept between 0.1 to 1 J/cm² to balance cleaning efficiency with material integrity. Repetition rates can vary from 20 to 100 kHz, depending on the application's requirements. These parameters, when set correctly, enable the laser to work like a skilled artisan, removing contaminants with precision.
