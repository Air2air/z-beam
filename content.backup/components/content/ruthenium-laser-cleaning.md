## Material Essence & Laser Dance: Properties of Ruthenium

Ruthenium's allure in laser cleaning lies in its robust physical and chemical properties. With a melting point of approximately 2,334°C, Ruthenium withstands the high temperatures generated during laser ablation. Its high reflectivity at lower wavelengths, coupled with significant absorption at 1064 nm, makes it an ideal candidate for cleaning with pulsed fiber lasers. Moreover, the thermal conductivity of Ruthenium, at about 117 W/m·K, allows for efficient heat dissipation, minimizing damage during the cleaning process. This interplay of properties, akin to a masterpiece unfolding, enables Ruthenium to be cleaned without compromising its integrity.

## Fine-Tuning the Beam: Optimal Laser Parameters for Ruthenium Cleaning

To achieve effective cleaning of Ruthenium, specific laser parameters must be meticulously chosen. The optimal wavelength for cleaning Ruthenium is typically 1064 nm, where the material exhibits significant absorption. Pulse durations in the range of 10-100 ns allow for precise control over the ablation process. A fluence of 1-10 J/cm², coupled with repetition rates of 10-100 kHz, ensures thorough cleaning while minimizing thermal effects. These parameters, like the strokes of a painter's brush, must be carefully adjusted to achieve the desired outcome.
