  "articleBody": "Medium Density Fiberboard (MDF) is an engineered wood composite consisting of wood fibers bonded with synthetic resins, primarily urea-formaldehyde, under heat and pressure. Its homogeneous structure and lack of grain make it ideal for laser cleaning applications. The optimal laser parameters for MDF cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.0 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-200 nanoseconds. These parameters effectively remove surface contaminants, adhesives, and coatings while minimizing thermal damage to the cellulose-lignin matrix. Key applications include furniture restoration, mold cleaning, surface preparation for painting, and removal of biological growth from architectural elements. Challenges include controlling heat penetration to prevent resin degradation and charring, which is addressed through precise parameter optimization and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations require proper ventilation to manage formaldehyde emissions during processing, laser safety enclosures, and particulate filtration systems to capture airborne wood dust. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical stress on the delicate MDF surface while providing unmatched precision in contaminant removal.",
