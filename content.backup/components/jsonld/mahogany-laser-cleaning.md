  "articleBody": "Mahogany is a dense, durable hardwood prized for its rich reddish-brown color, straight grain, and excellent workability. Its chemical composition includes cellulose (40-50%), hemicellulose (20-30%), lignin (20-30%), and various extractives that contribute to its natural resistance to decay. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, oxidation, and biological growth without compromising Mahogany's structural integrity or surface quality. Optimal laser parameters typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-200 ns to effectively ablate contaminants while minimizing thermal penetration. Key applications include heritage furniture restoration, architectural woodwork conservation, marine interior maintenance, and high-end musical instrument refurbishment. Challenges include managing the material's variable density and resin content, which require careful parameter adjustment to prevent uneven cleaning or thermal damage. Safety protocols mandate proper ventilation to manage pyrolysis byproducts, Class 4 laser safety enclosures, and appropriate personal protective equipment including wavelength-specific laser safety glasses.
