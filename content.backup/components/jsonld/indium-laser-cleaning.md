  "articleBody": "Indium is a soft, malleable metal with a low melting point of 156.6°C, making it particularly sensitive to thermal damage during cleaning processes. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, surface oxides, and residues without compromising Indium's structural integrity or altering its electrical properties. Optimal laser parameters for Indium cleaning typically utilize nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064nm wavelength with fluences between 0.5-2.0 J/cm² to avoid melting while effectively ablating contaminants. The process requires precise control of pulse duration (10-100ns) and repetition rates (20-100kHz) to manage thermal input. Key applications include cleaning Indium tin oxide (ITO) coatings for display manufacturing, preparing bonding surfaces for semiconductor packaging, and removing oxidation from electrical contacts. Challenges include preventing surface melting and maintaining consistent cleaning across Indium's relatively soft surface, which requires carefully calibrated laser parameters and scanning patterns. Safety considerations involve proper fume extraction due to potential Indium oxide particle generation and implementation of appropriate laser safety protocols including Class 4 laser enclosure systems and personnel protective equipment.",
