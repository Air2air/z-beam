  "articleBody": "Basalt is a dense, fine-grained volcanic rock composed primarily of plagioclase feldspar, pyroxene, and olivine minerals, with a Mohs hardness of 6-7 and high compressive strength up to 300 MPa. These properties make it challenging to clean using traditional methods without causing surface damage. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, coatings, rust, biological growth, and surface oxidation while preserving the material's structural integrity. Optimal laser parameters for Basalt cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 1-4 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-200 nanoseconds, allowing selective ablation of contaminants without thermal damage to the mineral matrix. Key applications include architectural restoration of Basalt monuments, surface preparation for industrial flooring, removal of marine growth from coastal structures, and precision cleaning of Basalt fiber composites. Challenges include the material's variable mineral composition, which requires parameter adjustments to account for different absorption characteristics, and the potential for thermal stress cracking if excessive energy is applied. Safety considerations mandate Class 4 laser protocols including appropriate eyewear, fume extraction systems for airborne particulates, and controlled access to the processing area to prevent accidental exposure.",
