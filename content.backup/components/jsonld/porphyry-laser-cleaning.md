  "articleBody": "Porphyry is a hard, dense igneous rock characterized by its distinctive porphyritic texture with large crystals (phenocrysts) embedded in a fine-grained matrix. Its composition typically includes feldspar, quartz, and various ferromagnesian minerals, creating a complex surface with varying absorption coefficients that require precise laser parameter optimization. Optimal laser cleaning utilizes near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to effectively remove biological growth, atmospheric pollution crusts, and previous restoration materials without thermal damage to the crystalline structure. The key challenge lies in the heterogeneous composition of Porphyry, which requires adaptive laser parameters to prevent differential ablation rates between mineral components. Applications include architectural restoration of historical monuments, industrial floor maintenance, and precision cleaning of decorative elements where mechanical methods would cause micro-fracturing. Safety protocols mandate Class 4 laser safety measures including appropriate eyewear (OD 7+ at 1064 nm), fume extraction systems for particulate matter, and controlled access zones to prevent accidental exposure.
