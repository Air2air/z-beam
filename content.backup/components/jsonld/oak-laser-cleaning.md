  "articleBody": "Oak (Quercus spp.) is a dense hardwood characterized by its high tannin content, pronounced grain structure, and natural durability, making it challenging for conventional cleaning methods. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact solution for removing contaminants, aged finishes, biological growth, and surface oxidation without compromising the wood's structural integrity or natural patina. Optimal laser parameters for Oak typically utilize near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns, allowing selective ablation of unwanted material while preserving the underlying substrate. The process effectively removes centuries of dirt, soot, and deteriorated coatings from historical artifacts, architectural elements, and fine furniture without mechanical abrasion or chemical residues. Key applications include heritage conservation, museum artifact restoration, architectural woodwork rehabilitation, and precision manufacturing of high-end wood products. Challenges include managing the variable absorption characteristics due to Oak's natural grain patterns and preventing thermal damage to the lignin matrix. Technical solutions involve real-time monitoring systems and adaptive parameter control.
