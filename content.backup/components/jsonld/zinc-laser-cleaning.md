  "articleBody": "Zinc is a bluish-white lustrous metal with atomic number 30 and symbol Zn, known for its excellent corrosion resistance through sacrificial protection. With a melting point of 419.5°C and boiling point of 907°C, Zinc responds well to laser cleaning due to its relatively low thermal conductivity (116 W/m·K) and specific heat capacity (0.39 J/g·K). Optimal laser parameters for Zinc cleaning typically utilize fiber lasers with 1064nm wavelength, fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 100-200 nanoseconds to effectively remove oxides, contaminants, and surface coatings without melting the substrate. Key applications include galvanized steel surface preparation, automotive component restoration, architectural element conservation, and electronic component manufacturing. The primary challenge in Zinc laser cleaning is controlling thermal input to prevent surface melting or zinc vaporization, which requires precise parameter optimization and real-time monitoring systems. Zinc's relatively low vaporization temperature (907°C) necessitates careful fluence control to avoid excessive material removal. Safety considerations include proper fume extraction to capture zinc oxide particles, which can cause metal fume fever if inhaled, and appropriate laser safety eyewear for the specific wavelength employed.
