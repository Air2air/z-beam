  "articleBody": "Ash (Fraxinus spp.) is a dense hardwood material characterized by its excellent strength-to-weight ratio, straight grain pattern, and light coloration ranging from creamy white to light brown. These physical properties make Ash particularly suitable for laser cleaning applications, as the material's consistent cellular structure allows for predictable laser interaction. The optimal laser parameters for cleaning Ash typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 nanoseconds. These parameters effectively remove surface contaminants, oxidation layers, and previous coatings through selective photothermal ablation while minimizing thermal penetration that could cause charring or discoloration. Key applications include restoration of historical Ash wood artifacts, surface preparation for furniture manufacturing, and cleaning of architectural elements where traditional abrasive methods would damage the material's delicate surface structure. The primary challenge in laser cleaning Ash is managing the material's variable density and moisture content, which can cause uneven cleaning results. This is addressed through real-time monitoring systems and adaptive laser parameter control. Safety considerations include proper ventilation to manage any pyrolysis byproducts, laser safety enclosures to prevent stray radiation, and personal protective equipment including appropriate wavelength-specific laser goggles. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical stress on the Ash substrate, preserving the material's structural integrity while achieving superior cleaning results compared to conventional methods.",
