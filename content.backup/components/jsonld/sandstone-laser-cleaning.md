  "articleBody": "Sandstone is a clastic sedimentary rock composed mainly of sand-sized mineral particles (0.0625-2mm) or rock fragments, typically quartz and feldspar, bound together by silica, calcium carbonate, or iron oxides. Its porous nature (10-30% porosity) and variable mineral composition present unique challenges for laser cleaning. Optimal laser parameters for Sandstone typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds to effectively remove biological growth, atmospheric pollution, and previous restoration treatments without causing thermal damage or mineral phase changes. The key applications include architectural restoration of historical monuments, cultural heritage conservation, and industrial surface preparation where precise contaminant removal is required without mechanical abrasion. Challenges include the material's heterogeneity, varying absorption characteristics, and potential for thermal stress cracking, which are addressed through precise parameter optimization and real-time monitoring systems. Safety considerations mandate comprehensive eye protection (OD7+ for 1064 nm), proper ventilation to handle aerosolized particles, and structural integrity assessments before treatment.
