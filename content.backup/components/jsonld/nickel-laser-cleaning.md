These properties make Nickel particularly suitable for laser cleaning applications where precision and material preservation are critical. The optimal laser parameters for Nickel cleaning typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 1-4 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-200 nanoseconds. These parameters effectively remove oxides, contaminants, and surface coatings while minimizing thermal effects on the Nickel substrate. Key applications include aerospace components, chemical processing equipment, battery manufacturing, and marine hardware where surface purity is essential. Challenges in Nickel laser cleaning include managing reflectivity (approximately 60-70% at 1064 nm) and preventing surface oxidation during processing, which is addressed through precise parameter optimization and controlled atmosphere environments. Safety considerations mandate proper fume extraction systems to capture Nickel oxide particles and appropriate laser safety protocols including Class 4 laser precautions, protective eyewear, and adequate ventilation.
