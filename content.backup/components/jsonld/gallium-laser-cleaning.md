  "articleBody": "Gallium is a unique metal with a low melting point of 29.76°C, making it highly sensitive to traditional cleaning methods that could cause thermal damage or phase changes. Laser cleaning provides an ideal non-contact solution for Gallium materials, particularly in semiconductor and electronics applications where surface purity is critical. The process utilizes carefully controlled laser parameters—typically nanosecond pulses at 1064 nm wavelength with fluences between 0.5-2.5 J/cm²—to selectively remove surface oxides, organic contaminants, and processing residues without melting the underlying Gallium substrate. This precision ablation preserves the material's crystalline structure and prevents the formation of additional oxides that can occur with mechanical or chemical cleaning methods. For Gallium arsenide and other compound semiconductors, laser cleaning achieves surface preparation with atomic-level precision, removing native oxides while maintaining stoichiometric balance. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning eliminates particulate generation and prevents surface scratching, making it essential for microelectronics fabrication where even nanometer-scale defects can impact device performance.
