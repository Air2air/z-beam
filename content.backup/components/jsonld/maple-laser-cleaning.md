  "articleBody": "Maple (Acer spp.) is a dense hardwood material characterized by its fine, uniform texture, moderate hardness (1450 on Janka scale), and light cream to reddish-brown coloration. Its cellular structure consists of approximately 60-70% cellulose, 20-30% lignin, and 5-10% hemicellulose, making it responsive to specific laser parameters. Optimal laser cleaning utilizes near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns to effectively remove surface contaminants, oxidation, and previous coatings without carbonizing the cellulose structure. The process selectively ablates unwanted material through photothermal and photomechanical mechanisms while preserving the underlying Maple substrate's integrity. Key applications include restoration of antique furniture, musical instruments, flooring, and architectural elements where traditional methods would damage the delicate surface. Challenges include managing the material's variable density and avoiding thermal degradation, which is addressed through precise parameter control and real-time monitoring systems. Safety protocols require Class 4 laser safety measures including appropriate eyewear, ventilation systems for particulate matter, and fire suppression equipment due to Maple's combustible nature.",
