  "articleBody": "Marble, primarily composed of calcium carbonate (CaCO₃) with minor impurities creating its characteristic veining, presents unique challenges for laser cleaning due to its susceptibility to thermal damage and chemical alteration. The optimal laser parameters for marble cleaning typically utilize near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.5-2.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 nanoseconds. These parameters enable selective removal of surface contaminants, biological growth, and atmospheric deposits while preserving the marble's crystalline structure and natural patina. Key applications include architectural restoration of historical monuments, conservation of cultural artifacts, and precision cleaning of high-value decorative elements. The primary challenge involves managing the thermal input to prevent calcium carbonate decomposition into calcium oxide, which can cause whitening and surface deterioration. Advanced laser systems incorporate real-time monitoring and closed-loop feedback to maintain optimal processing conditions. Safety considerations mandate comprehensive eye protection (OD7+ for 1064 nm), proper ventilation to manage ablation byproducts, and controlled environment conditions to prevent moisture-related damage during processing.
