  "articleBody": "Inconel, a nickel-chromium-based superalloy, exhibits exceptional high-temperature strength, oxidation resistance, and corrosion resistance, making it ideal for aerospace, chemical processing, and nuclear applications. Laser cleaning provides a superior method for removing oxides, coatings, and contaminants from Inconel surfaces without mechanical abrasion or chemical residues. Optimal laser parameters typically include 1064 nm wavelength for maximum absorption, fluence levels between 2-8 J/cm², and pulse durations of 100-200 ns to effectively ablate surface contaminants while minimizing thermal effects on the substrate. The process effectively removes tenacious oxides that form during high-temperature service without compromising the protective chromium oxide layer inherent to Inconel. Key challenges include managing the material's high reflectivity and thermal conductivity, which require precise parameter optimization to achieve efficient ablation without surface melting or microstructural changes. Applications span turbine blade refurbishment, weld preparation in nuclear components, and surface restoration in chemical processing equipment. Safety protocols mandate Class 4 laser safety measures including appropriate eyewear, fume extraction for nickel-containing particles, and controlled access to processing areas.
