  "articleBody": "Ruthenium is a rare transition metal with exceptional hardness (6.5 Mohs), high melting point (2334°C), and remarkable chemical resistance, particularly in harsh environments. These properties make it challenging to clean using conventional methods without damaging the substrate. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact solution utilizing optimized parameters: 1064nm wavelength for optimal absorption, fluence levels of 0.8-1.5 J/cm², and pulse durations of 10-100ns to effectively remove surface contaminants while preserving the base material. The process effectively eliminates ruthenium oxides, carbon deposits, and organic residues that accumulate during industrial processes. Key applications include cleaning ruthenium-based catalysts in chemical processing equipment, precision contacts in electronics manufacturing, and specialized components in aerospace applications. Challenges include ruthenium's high reflectivity (approximately 65% at 1064nm) which requires precise energy control to overcome without causing thermal damage to the substrate. Safety protocols mandate Class 1 laser enclosures, proper ventilation to capture airborne nanoparticles, and appropriate PPE including laser safety goggles rated for the specific wavelength. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning prevents mechanical stress and preserves the material's critical surface properties, making it ideal for high-value ruthenium components where precision and material integrity are paramount.",
