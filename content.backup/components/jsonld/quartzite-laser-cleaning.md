  "articleBody": "Quartzite is a metamorphic rock composed primarily of quartz (SiO₂), known for its exceptional hardness (7 on Mohs scale), high compressive strength, and chemical inertness. These properties make it challenging to clean using traditional methods without causing surface damage. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact solution that selectively removes contaminants, biological growth, and surface deposits while preserving the underlying quartz crystal structure. Optimal laser parameters for Quartzite cleaning include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 nanoseconds. These settings provide sufficient energy to break molecular bonds in contaminants without exceeding the ablation threshold of the quartz matrix. Key applications include architectural restoration of Quartzite monuments, industrial processing equipment cleaning, and preparation of Quartzite surfaces for bonding or coating applications. Challenges include the material's high reflectivity and thermal conductivity, which require precise energy control to prevent thermal stress and micro-fracturing. Safety considerations mandate Class 4 laser protocols with appropriate eye protection, fume extraction systems for silica particulate, and environmental controls to prevent secondary contamination.",
