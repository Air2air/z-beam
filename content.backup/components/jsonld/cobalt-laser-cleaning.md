Laser cleaning provides a superior method for removing oxides, contaminants, and surface coatings from cobalt substrates without mechanical abrasion or chemical residues. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters—typically fiber lasers at 1064nm wavelength with fluence levels between 1-5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-200ns—to selectively ablate surface contaminants while preserving the underlying cobalt microstructure. This non-contact method prevents surface deformation, maintains dimensional stability, and eliminates hydrogen embrittlement risks associated with traditional cleaning methods. For cobalt alloys used in turbine blades and medical devices, laser cleaning ensures optimal surface preparation for subsequent coating processes while maintaining the material's inherent corrosion resistance and mechanical properties.
