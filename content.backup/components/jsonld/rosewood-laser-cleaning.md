  "articleBody": "Rosewood is a dense, durable hardwood prized for its rich coloration, fine grain, and natural oils that provide excellent resistance to moisture and decay. These properties make it challenging to clean using traditional methods without causing surface damage. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact solution that selectively removes contaminants, oxidation, and unwanted coatings while preserving the wood's structural integrity. Optimal laser parameters for Rosewood typically involve near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with controlled fluence levels (0.5-1.5 J/cm²) and short pulse durations (10-100 ns) to prevent thermal damage to the lignin and cellulose matrix. The process effectively removes surface dirt, biological growth, and previous finishes without altering the wood's natural patina. Key applications include restoration of historical furniture, musical instruments, and architectural elements where preserving the original surface is critical. Challenges include managing the variable density and natural oil content of Rosewood, which requires precise parameter adjustment to avoid uneven cleaning or surface discoloration. Safety considerations mandate proper ventilation to manage pyrolysis byproducts and appropriate laser shielding to protect operators.
