  "articleBody": "Alumina (Al2O3) is a high-performance ceramic material valued for its exceptional hardness, thermal stability, and chemical resistance, making it ideal for demanding industrial applications. Laser cleaning provides a superior non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxide layers, and surface impurities without compromising Alumina's structural integrity. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters, typically employing nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064 nm wavelength with fluence levels between 1-5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 10-100 ns. These parameters enable selective ablation of unwanted surface material while preserving the underlying Alumina substrate. Key applications include cleaning semiconductor components, medical implants, cutting tools, and electronic substrates where surface purity is critical. Challenges include managing thermal stress to prevent microcracking and ensuring uniform cleaning across complex geometries, which are addressed through optimized beam shaping and scanning patterns. Safety considerations mandate proper laser shielding, fume extraction for airborne particulates, and appropriate personal protective equipment. The non-abrasive nature of laser cleaning eliminates mechanical damage risks and eliminates chemical waste, making it an environmentally preferred surface treatment method for high-precision Alumina components.",
