  "articleBody": "Tin (Sn) is a soft, malleable, silvery-white metal with a low melting point of 231.9°C and excellent corrosion resistance, making it widely used in industrial applications including electronics, packaging, and coatings. Laser cleaning offers a precise, non-contact method for removing contaminants, oxides, and surface impurities from Tin without compromising its structural integrity or causing thermal damage. The process utilizes controlled laser parameters with optimal settings including 1064nm wavelength for effective absorption, fluence levels of 0.5-2.5 J/cm² to avoid melting, and pulse durations of 10-100ns for controlled ablation. Key applications include cleaning Tin-plated electronic components, removing oxidation from historical Tin artifacts, and preparing surfaces for soldering or coating processes. Challenges include Tin's low melting point requiring precise thermal management, which is addressed through pulsed laser operation and real-time temperature monitoring. The process effectively removes tin oxides (SnO and SnO₂) without damaging the underlying metal substrate. Safety considerations include proper fume extraction to manage airborne particles, laser safety protocols including appropriate eyewear, and controlled environments to prevent reflection hazards.
