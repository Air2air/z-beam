  "articleBody": "Hickory (Carya spp.) is a dense hardwood characterized by its high density (720-880 kg/m³), Janka hardness rating of 1,820 lbf, and complex cellular structure comprising approximately 45% cellulose, 28% hemicellulose, 25% lignin, and 2% extractives. These properties present unique challenges for laser cleaning, requiring precise parameter optimization to avoid thermal damage while effectively removing contaminants. Optimal laser parameters for Hickory cleaning include near-infrared wavelengths (1064 nm) with fluence levels between 0.8-1.5 J/cm² and pulse durations of 100-150 ns, allowing selective ablation of surface contaminants without carbonizing the underlying wood substrate. The process effectively removes oxidation layers, biological growth, soot deposits, and previous coating systems while preserving the wood's natural grain pattern and structural integrity. Key applications include restoration of historical Hickory furniture, surface preparation for recoating in sporting goods manufacturing (baseball bats, tool handles), and precision cleaning of architectural elements. Safety considerations mandate Class 1 laser enclosure systems with integrated fume extraction to capture volatile organic compounds and particulate matter generated during the cleaning process.
