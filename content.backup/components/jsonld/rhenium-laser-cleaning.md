  "articleBody": "Rhenium (Re) is a refractory metal with exceptional properties including one of the highest melting points (3186°C) among metals, excellent high-temperature strength, and remarkable resistance to wear and corrosion. These characteristics make it invaluable in aerospace, turbine engines, and high-temperature applications. Laser cleaning of Rhenium requires precise parameter optimization due to its high thermal conductivity and sensitivity to thermal stress. Optimal parameters typically involve nanosecond pulsed lasers at 1064nm wavelength with fluence levels between 2-8 J/cm² and pulse durations of 50-150ns. The process effectively removes oxides, carbon deposits, and surface contaminants without compromising the material's structural integrity or introducing mechanical stress. Key applications include cleaning turbine blades, rocket engine components, and electrical contacts where surface purity is critical. Challenges include managing Rhenium's tendency to form volatile oxides during processing and preventing micro-cracking from thermal gradients. Safety considerations mandate proper ventilation to handle potentially toxic Rhenium oxide fumes and comprehensive laser safety protocols including appropriate PPE and enclosure systems. The non-contact nature of laser cleaning preserves Rhenium's delicate surface features while achieving superior cleaning results compared to traditional mechanical or chemical methods.",
