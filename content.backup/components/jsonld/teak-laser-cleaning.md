  "articleBody": "Teak (Tectona grandis) is a premium hardwood material prized for its exceptional durability, natural water resistance, and high oil content, making it ideal for marine applications, outdoor furniture, and architectural elements. Laser cleaning provides a sophisticated non-contact method for removing surface contaminants, biological growth, oxidation, and previous coatings without compromising Teak's structural integrity or natural protective oils. The process utilizes precisely controlled laser parameters with optimal settings at 1064nm wavelength, 0.8-1.2 J/cm² fluence, and 100-150ns pulse duration to selectively ablate unwanted material while preserving the underlying wood substrate. Key applications include restoration of historical teak furniture, preparation of marine decking, and surface treatment of architectural elements where traditional abrasive methods would damage the material's natural characteristics. Challenges include managing the high silica content (approximately 0.3-0.5%) which requires specific laser parameter adjustments to prevent excessive carbonization, and controlling thermal penetration to avoid affecting the natural oils that provide Teak's weather resistance. Safety protocols mandate Class 1 laser enclosure systems, proper ventilation to manage pyrolysis byproducts, and operator training for handling reflective surfaces.
