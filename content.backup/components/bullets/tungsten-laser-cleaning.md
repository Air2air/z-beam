- **[APPLICATIONS]** Laser cleaning is extensively employed within the European manufacturing sector for refurbishing tungsten tooling, such as inserts and electrodes, by removing stubborn oxides and contaminants without substrate damage. This non-abrasive process is also critical for pre-weld preparation on tungsten components used in high-value aerospace and energy applications, ensuring superior joint integrity.

- **[PARAMETERS]** Optimal cleaning of tungsten, known for its exceptionally high melting point and thermal conductivity, typically employs a fiber laser with a 1064 nm wavelength and nanosecond pulse durations to effectively ablate surface contaminants. Precise fluence control, often between 1-3 J/cm² as guided by applicable ISO standards, is paramount to avoid micro-cracking or melting the underlying refractory material.

- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** The process eliminates the need for chemical solvents and abrasive media, significantly reducing hazardous waste generation and aligning with the stringent waste management directives of the EU's circular economy model. This dry technique also minimizes airborne particulate compared to traditional blasting, contributing to a cleaner and more sustainable industrial workspace.

- **[SAFETY]** Strict adherence to EN 60825-1 for laser safety is mandatory, requiring fully enclosed interlocked workstations and the use of wavelength-specific protective eyewear for all operators to prevent retinal damage. Proper fume extraction and ventilation systems must be installed to capture any nanoscale particles generated during the ablation of surface coatings.

- **[CHALLENGES]** A primary difficulty is the narrow window between the removal threshold of contaminants and the damage threshold of the pure tungsten substrate, necessitating highly precise laser parameter calibration. Mitigation involves employing real-time process monitoring systems, like acoustic or plasma spectroscopy, to instantly adjust power and maintain cleaning efficacy without compromising the base material.
