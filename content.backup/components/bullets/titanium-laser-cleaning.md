- **[APPLICATIONS]** Laser cleaning is extensively employed within the European aerospace and high-performance automotive sectors for restoring titanium components like turbine blades and exhaust systems, ensuring optimal surface conditions for subsequent thermal spray coatings. The process is also critical in medical device manufacturing, where it prepares titanium implants for biocompatible coatings without compromising material integrity or introducing contaminants.

- **[PARAMETERS]** A fiber laser with a 1,070 nm wavelength and nanosecond pulse duration is typically utilized, as its high absorption by the titanium dioxide layer efficiently ablates contaminants without damaging the base metal. The fluence must be meticulously calibrated, often between 1-3 J/cm², to surpass the ablation threshold of the surface oxides while remaining well below the vaporization threshold of the pure titanium substrate.

- **[ENVIRONMENTAL]** This technique eliminates the need for chemical solvents and abrasive media, aligning with stringent EU directives like REACH by preventing the generation of hazardous secondary waste streams. The process is exceptionally efficient, consuming significantly less energy compared to traditional methods and contributing to a reduced carbon footprint in advanced manufacturing operations.

- **[SAFETY]** Strict adherence to European standard EN 60825-1 for laser product safety is mandatory, requiring fully enclosed interlocked workstations and the use of wavelength-specific protective eyewear for all operators. Effective fume extraction systems with HEPA filtration are also critical to capture and contain any nano-sized particulate matter generated during the ablation process.

- **[CHALLENGES]** A primary difficulty is the high reflectivity of bare titanium and its extreme affinity for oxygen, which can lead to unintended heat input and the formation of a new oxide layer if process parameters are not perfectly optimized. These issues are mitigated through real-time process monitoring with pyrometers and ensuring the process is conducted within an inert argon atmosphere to prevent immediate re-oxidation.
