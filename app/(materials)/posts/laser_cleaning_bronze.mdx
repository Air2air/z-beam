## Introduction
**Introduction to Laser Cleaning in Bronze Applications**  

Laser cleaning has emerged as a precision surface treatment method for bronze components across critical industries, offering a non-abrasive, eco-friendly alternative to traditional techniques like chemical stripping or mechanical abrasion. In aerospace, laser systems remove oxidation and contaminants from bronze bearings and bushings without altering substrate integrity, ensuring compliance with stringent tolerances. Automotive manufacturers leverage pulsed lasers to clean bronze alloy connectors and transmission parts, eliminating thermal distortion risks associated with conventional methods. Marine applications benefit from laser ablation in restoring bronze propellers and fittings by selectively stripping salt-induced corrosion while preserving anti-fouling coatings. Art conservators employ low-fluence lasers to delicately remove centuries of patina or pollutants from bronze sculptures without damaging intricate details. In electronics, fiber lasers de-oxidize bronze contacts and heat sinks, enhancing conductivity without hazardous chemical residues. The process’s tunability (wavelength, pulse duration, fluence) allows tailored solutions for each industry, combining micron-level precision with minimal environmental impact.
