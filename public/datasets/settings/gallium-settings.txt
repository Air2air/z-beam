Gallium's unusually low melting point sets it apart, risking surface liquefaction if energy builds up too quickly. Start with reduced power and faster scan speeds to strip away oxides or residues while keeping the material solid. Make sure you avoid prolonged exposure in one spot, as this can cause uneven melting and distort the underlying structure.
