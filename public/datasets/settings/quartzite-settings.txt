QUARTZITE LASER CLEANING SETTINGS
============================================================

Category: stone > sedimentary
Title: Quartzite Settings
Description: When laser cleaning Quartzite, the biggest challenge is its extreme hardness. This tough stone resists contaminants well, but it demands careful energy control to avoid surface cracks. I've seen setups where rushed power ramps cause micro-fractures, so start slow with gentle pulses. Its low porosity helps—dirt doesn't sink deep, making removal straightforward once you dial in. Unlike softer stones, Quartzite holds heat unevenly, so keep passes light and spaced. This works best when you focus on steady scanning without overlap buildup. Tends to shine up nicely after a few rounds. Just watch the edges at the end; overdoing dwell time there can lead to unwanted chipping, so ease off power gradually.
