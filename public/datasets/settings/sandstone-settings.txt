Title: Sandstone Settings
Description: When laser cleaning sandstone, the biggest hurdle I've seen is its porous, layered structure that traps grime deep inside while heating unevenly during treatment. This tends to cause micro-cracks if you push too hard, since the stone's sedimentary makeup varies from spot to spot. To handle it, go slow with gentle pulses that let the laser seep into those pores without overwhelming the surface, focusing on dry runs first to map weak areas. Watch out at the end—overlapping passes can still fracture edges, so always cool between them.
