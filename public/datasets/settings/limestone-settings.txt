Category: stone > sedimentary
Title: Limestone Settings
Description: When laser cleaning limestone, watch its porosity first. It soaks up dirt and moisture more than denser stones like granite. This traps contaminants deep, so you risk incomplete removal or surface damage if you blast too hard.

I've seen heat build up quickly because limestone conducts it poorly, unlike metals that spread it out. Start slow with gentle pulses to avoid cracking the stone.

Tends to absorb laser energy moderately, better than reflective marbles. Keep passes light and overlap them a bit. This clears buildup without etching the texture. Adjust if you spot whitening—dial back power right away.
