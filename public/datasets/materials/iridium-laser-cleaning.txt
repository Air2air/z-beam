  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial Nd:YAG laser cleaning of refractory metals like Iridium, starting low due to high reflectivity (~90% at 1064 nm) to avoid excessive heating; max limited to prevent melting (mp 2446°C). scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance fluence and coverage; slower for heavy contamination (e.g., oxides), faster for light surface prep. Recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal Iridium cleaning without thermal buildup, considering low thermal conductivity (14.8 W/m·K). Source: CleanLaser equipment manuals; industry standards for 1064 nm cleaning (Opt. Lasers Eng. 2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective industrial cleaning; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for minimal HAZ on heat-sensitive Iridium, but ns sufficient due to high melting point. Recommended 20 ns to limit heat diffusion. Source: Laser physics reviews (e.g., Prog. Quantum Electron. 2019); ns common in metal ablation studies.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to achieve high average power without excessive heat accumulation; higher rates speed up process but risk thermal effects on reflective Iridium. Recommended 50 kHz balances speed and safety. Source: Academic literature on Nd:YAG cleaning (Appl. Surf. Sci. 2017); trade-off noted in high-rep-rate studies.
