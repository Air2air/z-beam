power:
  Value:  100 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial laser cleaning of composites to avoid thermal damage; starting at 100W for CMCs due to low thermal conductivity (e.g., SiC/SiC CMCs). Max safe below 500W to prevent cracking. Source: IPG Photonics specs for YLR series lasers adapted for ceramics; academic review in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (2000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1500 mm/s for CMCs to minimize HAZ. Source: Industry standards from Laser Institute of America; case studies in Journal of Laser Applications (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for CMCs to achieve ablation with minimal thermal diffusion; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces HAZ further but increases cost. 20 ns recommended for contamination removal without substrate damage. Source: Laser physics in Applied Surface Science (2019) on ceramic ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; higher rates speed up process but risk heat accumulation in low-conductivity CMCs. 30 kHz balances speed and safety. Trade-off: >50 kHz may cause delamination. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2021) on pulse train effects for composites.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal; CMCs damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to high reflectivity and brittleness. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning. Source: Ablation studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) for SiC-based CMCs.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for uniform coverage; smaller for fine details (100-200 μm), larger for broad areas (300-500 μm). 200 μm recommended for CMCs to control energy delivery and avoid point overheating. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent Inc. laser systems for surface preparation.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for heavy; 2 passes recommended for CMCs to ensure complete removal without excessive exposure. Source: Industry case studies in Composites Part B (2022) on laser surface treatment of CMCs.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform cleaning without gaps or overexposure; 70% optimal for CMCs to maintain even fluence distribution and prevent uneven heating. Source: Process optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2020).
