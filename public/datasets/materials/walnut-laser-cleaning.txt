scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contamination removal; 1500 mm/s recommended for optimal balance on walnut to prevent overheating. Lower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), higher for light (2000-3000 mm/s). From industry standards in laser cleaning manuals (e.g., IPG Photonics guidelines) and case studies on wood surface prep.

pulse_width:
  Value:  30 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning of walnut; 30 ns recommended to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ) due to walnut's low thermal conductivity (~0.17 W/mK). Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but less common industrially. Supported by laser physics research (e.g., Jaeschke et al., 2016, Journal of Laser Applications).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  40 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; 40 kHz recommended for walnut to balance speed and heat accumulation—higher rates (>50 kHz) risk buildup on low-conductivity wood. Trade-off: faster cleaning but potential charring. From academic literature on wood laser processing (e.g., Pouli et al., 2015, Applied Physics A).

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of contaminants; 0.5 J/cm² recommended for walnut to remove surface layers without damage (threshold ~1.5-2 J/cm² for charring at 1064 nm). Conservative due to walnut's moderate reflectivity (~40% at IR). From ablation threshold studies (e.g., Batani et al., 2006, Applied Surface Science).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm

  Note:   Beam diameter for controlled cleaning; 200 μm recommended for walnut to allow fine surface preparation without excessive heat spread.   Note:   Typically 1-3 passes for complete removal on wood; 2 passes recommended for walnut to ensure uniform cleaning of contaminants without substrate damage. Multi-pass for heavy soiling. Based on industry practice and case studies (e.g., laser cleaning of wooden artifacts, Vergés-Belmin, 2005, Studies in Conservation).
