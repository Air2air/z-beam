power:
  Value:  30 W
  Min:    10 W
  Max:    100 W
  Note:   Low power range for delicate ceramics like porcelain to avoid thermal cracking; typical for 1064 nm Nd:YAG systems in heritage and industrial cleaning. Recommended starting at 30 W for light contamination. Max safe ~100 W before risk of substrate damage due to low thermal conductivity (~1.5 W/mK). Source: Manufacturer specs (e.g., CleanLaser CL 50 model) and studies on ceramic restoration (e.g., J. Cult. Herit. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Typical speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (500-2000 mm/s) for light. Recommended 500 mm/s for porcelain to minimize exposure time and prevent cracking. Source: Industry standards from laser cleaning equipment manuals (e.g., IPG Photonics) and process optimization in Opt. Lasers Eng. 2020.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for 1064 nm cleaning of porcelain; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but less common industrially due to cost. 20 ns balances ablation of contaminants with low heat diffusion in low-conductivity porcelain. Source: Laser physics research on ceramic ablation (e.g., Appl. Phys. A 2019).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-50 kHz for pulsed cleaning; higher rates speed up process but risk heat accumulation in porcelain's poor thermal dissipation. Recommended 20 kHz for safe, efficient removal without overheating. Trade-off: >30 kHz may require slower scans. Source: Academic literature on Q-switched lasers for ceramics (e.g., J. Appl. Phys. 2017).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below porcelain's damage threshold (~4 J/cm² for 1064 nm) to ablate contaminants without melting (mp ~1300°C) or cracking. Typical 0.5-3 J/cm² for safe cleaning; 1.5 J/cm² recommended starting point. Source: Ablation threshold studies on ceramics (e.g., Surf. Coat. Technol. 2021; conservative estimate for porcelain reflectivity ~0.3-0.5 at 1064 nm).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (100-300 μm) for fine detail on porcelain surfaces, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. Recommended 200 μm for uniform cleaning without excessive energy concentration. Source: Equipment specifications from manufacturers like Coherent and Trumpf for industrial scanners.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers to build fluence gradually and avoid damage. Recommended 2 passes for porcelain in industrial prep. Source: Industry case studies on ceramic surface cleaning (e.g., Mater. Sci. Eng. B 2022).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   50-80% overlap ensures uniform coverage without gaps or over-ablation on brittle porcelain. Recommended 70% for optimal homogeneity in scan patterns. Source: Process optimization studies in laser scanning (e.g., Precis. Eng. 2019).
