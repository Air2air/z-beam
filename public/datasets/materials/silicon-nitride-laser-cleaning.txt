  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for pulsed fiber lasers in industrial cleaning of ceramics like Si3N4 to remove contaminants without thermal damage; starting at 100W for controlled ablation. Max safe ~300-500W to avoid cracking due to low thermal conductivity (~25 W/mK). Source: IPG Photonics fiber laser specs for cleaning applications; academic review in 'Laser Cleaning of Ceramics' (Optics and Lasers in Engineering, 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for 1064 nm cleaning; slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination (e.g., oxides), faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light surface prep. Recommended 2000 mm/s balances efficiency and safety on Si3N4's brittle surface. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals; case studies in Journal of Laser Applications (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for Si3N4 cleaning to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precision but costlier. 20 ns typical for contaminant removal without substrate melting (Si3N4 mp ~1900°C). Avoid fs for industrial scale. Source: Laser physics in 'Pulsed Laser Cleaning of Ceramics' (Applied Surface Science, 2019).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 20-100 kHz for cleaning; 50 kHz recommended for Si3N4 to achieve fast coverage while limiting heat accumulation (trade-off: higher rates speed up process but risk thermal buildup on low-conductivity material). Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2017) on pulse train effects in ceramic ablation.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant removal; damage threshold for Si3N4 ~3-5 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate reflectivity (~35%). Recommended 0.5 J/cm² for cleaning without ablation of substrate. Conservative for light contamination. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2016); uncertainty noted for specific contaminants.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range: smaller (50-200 μm) for fine detail on Si3N4 components, larger (500-1000 μm) for broad area prep. 200 μm recommended for uniform cleaning balancing resolution and speed. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems for industrial cleaning.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-3) for heavy layers on Si3N4 to ensure complete removal without excessive energy input. Recommended 2 passes for industrial surface prep. Source: Industry case studies in 'Laser Surface Treatment of Ceramics' (Materials Science and Engineering A, 2021).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Typical 30-80% for uniform coverage; 50% recommended for Si3N4 to avoid uneven heating or missed spots while preventing overexposure on heat-sensitive ceramic. Higher overlap for heavy contamination. Source: Process optimization in Laser & Photonics Reviews (2019).
