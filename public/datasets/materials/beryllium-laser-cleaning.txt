scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Range for effective contaminant removal without excessive heat accumulation; 1000 mm/s recommended for optimal balance on Beryllium's high thermal conductivity (216 W/mK); slower (200-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (up to 3000 mm/s) for light. Source: Laserax equipment manuals and J. Laser Appl. 32, 022064 (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) typical for industrial cleaning to ablate contaminants while minimizing heat-affected zone (HAZ); 20 ns recommended for Beryllium to limit thermal diffusion given its conductivity; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier applications. Source: Laser physics review in Opt. Lasers Eng. 120, 102-115 (2019).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   10-100 kHz for cleaning applications; 50 kHz recommended for Beryllium to achieve fast processing without significant heat buildup (high freq increases speed but risks accumulation on reflective surfaces); trade-off noted in ablation efficiency. Source: Academic study in Appl. Surf. Sci. 475, 245-252 (2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on metals; Beryllium ablation threshold ~2-3 J/cm² at 1064 nm, so 1.5 J/cm² recommended for safe cleaning below damage; higher for heavy contamination but conservative due to reflectivity. Source: Ablation threshold studies in J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. 52, 395301 (2019).

spot_size:
