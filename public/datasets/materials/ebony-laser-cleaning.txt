Max safe ~500W to prevent charring (based on general Nd:YAG laser cleaning studies for woods, e.g., Journal of Cultural Heritage, 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (2000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1500 mm/s for uniform Ebony surface prep (industry standards from IPG Photonics manuals and laser cleaning process optimization).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for minimal heat-affected zone on low-conductivity woods like Ebony; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces thermal damage but increases cost. 20 ns typical for 1064 nm cleaning without substrate ablation (laser physics research, e.g., Optics & Lasers in Engineering, 2020).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20-50 kHz balances speed and heat accumulation for organic materials; higher rates (>50 kHz) risk overheating Ebony due to poor thermal conductivity. Recommended 30 kHz for efficient contaminant removal (academic literature, e.g., Applied Surface Science, 2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Ebony's ablation threshold (~5 J/cm² at 1064 nm for dark woods); 0.5-2 J/cm² for light cleaning, up to 4 for heavy. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² safe for contamination removal without fiber damage (ablation threshold studies, e.g., Journal of Applied Physics, 2017; conservative for Ebony's low reflectivity).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (100-200 μm) for fine detail on Ebony grain, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. Recommended 200 μm for precise industrial cleaning (equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contaminants, multi-pass (2-3) for heavy or layered removal on woods. Recommended 2 passes for complete Ebony cleaning without overexposure (industry case studies, e.g., laser restoration of wooden artifacts in Heritage Science, 2021).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   50-70% ensures uniform coverage and avoids gaps/striping on irregular surfaces like Ebony; lower for speed, higher for thoroughness. Recommended 60% for optimal process (process optimization studies, e.g., Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing, 2022).
