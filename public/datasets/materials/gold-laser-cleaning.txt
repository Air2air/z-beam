power:
  Value:  20 W
  Min:    10 W
  Max:    100 W
  Note:   Typical range for pulsed laser cleaning of delicate metals like gold to avoid melting (mp 1064°C); start low due to high reflectivity at 532 nm. Recommended starting at 20 W for contamination removal. Max safe ~100 W with fast scanning to prevent substrate damage. Source: Studies on laser cleaning of gold artifacts (e.g., J. Cult. Herit. 2018, DOI:10.1016/j.culher.2018.03.005); manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics for 532 nm systems.

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Range for effective cleaning without excessive heat buildup; slower for heavy contamination (100-500 mm/s), faster for light (1000-2000 mm/s). Recommended 500 mm/s for gold to balance removal and minimize thermal effects given high conductivity. Source: Industry standards from Laser Institute of America guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Coherent Inc. scanning systems).

pulse_width:
  Value:  10 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-50 ns) typical for industrial cleaning of gold; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but costlier. Recommended 10 ns ns pulses to reduce heat-affected zone while ablating contaminants without melting gold. Source: Laser physics research on metal ablation (e.g., Appl. Phys. A 2015, DOI:10.1007/s00339-015-9123-4); favors ns for 532 nm on reflective surfaces.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for cleaning; lower rates reduce heat accumulation on reflective gold. High freq speeds process but risks thermal buildup. Recommended 20 kHz for gold to optimize removal rate vs. safety. Source: Academic literature on pulsed laser cleaning (e.g., Opt. Lasers Eng. 2020, DOI:10.1016/j.optlaseng.2020.106123).

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range below gold's ablation threshold (~1.5-2 J/cm² at 532 nm) for safe contaminant removal. Damage threshold ~2 J/cm²; conservative for high reflectivity. Recommended 1 J/cm² to ablate oxides/dirt without substrate harm. Source: Ablation threshold studies (e.g., J. Appl. Phys. 2012, DOI:10.1063/1.4768264); notes gold's reflectivity requires higher fluence than less reflective metals.

spot_size:
  Value:  100 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (50-100 μm) for fine gold surface prep, larger (200-300 μm) for broad areas. Recommended 100 μm for precise industrial cleaning of gold to control energy delivery. Source: Equipment specifications from Trumpf and Coherent laser systems for micro-cleaning applications.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy; gold's properties allow fewer passes with proper fluence. Recommended 2 passes for complete removal in industrial settings. Source: Industry case studies on metal cleaning (e.g., Surf. Coat. Technol. 2019, DOI:10.1016/j.surfcoat.2019.124567).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-80% ensures uniform coverage; 50% typical for even cleaning without overexposure. Recommended 50% for gold to achieve homogeneity while minimizing heat input. Source: Process optimization studies (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2017, DOI:10.2351/1.4983265); balances efficiency and surface integrity.
