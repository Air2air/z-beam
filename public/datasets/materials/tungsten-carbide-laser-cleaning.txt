Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs for cleaning applications; academic review in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018) on metal cleaning thresholds.

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for 1064 nm cleaning; slower speeds (500-1500 mm/s) for heavy contamination like oxides, faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light surface prep. Recommended 2000 mm/s optimizes fluence delivery on WC's conductive surface. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals; case studies in Journal of Cleaner Production (2020) for carbide tool cleaning.

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-200 ns) preferred for industrial cleaning of WC to ablate contaminants while minimizing heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier. 50 ns balances energy deposition on reflective WC. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on pulse duration effects for ceramics; avoids fs due to WC's thermal properties.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; higher rates increase speed but risk heat accumulation on WC's moderate thermal conductivity (~80 W/m·K). 30 kHz recommended for controlled overlap without overheating. Trade-off: high freq faster but monitor for substrate damage. Source: Academic literature in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021); Coherent Armonia series specs.

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range below WC damage threshold (~5-10 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for contaminant removal; 0.5-2 J/cm² for light cleaning, up to 5 for heavy. Recommended 2 J/cm² ensures safe ablation of oxides without melting. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) for Tungsten Carbide; conservative for reflectivity reducing absorption.

spot_size:

  Min:    0.1 μm

Source: Equipment specifications from Adapt Laser Systems; process optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2022).

pass_count:
  Value:  3 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for complete removal of heavy layers on WC; up to 10 for thick coatings. 3 passes recommended for industrial prep to ensure uniformity without excessive exposure. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Journal of Manufacturing Processes (2019); avoids over-processing WC's durable surface.
