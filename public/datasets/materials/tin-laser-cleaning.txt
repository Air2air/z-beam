power:
  Value:  100 W
  Min:    20 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm pulsed laser cleaning of reflective metals like tin to balance efficiency and avoid melting (tin melting point ~232°C); starting at 100W for light contamination. Max safe ~300-500W with proper pulsing to prevent substrate damage due to high reflectivity (~70% at 1064 nm). Source: Manufacturer specs (e.g., IPG Photonics YLR series for metal cleaning); academic review in 'Laser Cleaning' by Kononenko et al. (Optics & Lasers in Engineering, 2016).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Range for effective contaminant removal on tin; slower speeds (100-500 mm/s) for heavy oxides, faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light surface prep. Recommended 1000 mm/s for optimal balance of cleaning rate and heat input, considering tin's thermal conductivity (~67 W/m·K). Source: Industry standards from Laser Institute of America guidelines; equipment manuals (e.g., CleanLaser CL series).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-200 ns) preferred for industrial tin cleaning to ablate contaminants without excessive heat; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but costlier. 20 ns recommended for tin to minimize thermal effects on reflective, low-melting substrate. Source: Laser physics research in 'Ultrashort Pulse Laser Ablation' by Bauerle (Laser Processing and Chemisty, 2011); studies on metal ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for tin to achieve fast coverage without excessive heat accumulation (trade-off: higher rates speed up process but risk melting if fluence too high). Lower rates for heavy contamination. Source: Academic literature, e.g., 'Pulsed Laser Cleaning of Metals' in Journal of Applied Physics (Anisimov et al., 2018).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on tin; ablation threshold ~0.5-1 J/cm² for ns pulses at 1064 nm, damage threshold ~3-5 J/cm² to avoid substrate melting. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning of light contaminants, accounting for high reflectivity reducing effective absorption. Source: Ablation threshold studies, e.g., 'Laser Ablation of Tin' in Applied Surface Science (Lorenz et al., 2009); conservative for industrial use.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range: 50-200 μm for fine detail on tin surfaces (e.g., electronics), 500-1000 μm for large-area industrial prep. 200 μm recommended for uniform cleaning without over-focusing heat on low-melting tin. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems for cleaning applications.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers on tin to ensure complete removal without cumulative damage. Recommended 2 passes for typical industrial surface preparation, allowing cooling between passes. Source: Industry practice in case studies, e.g., 'Laser Cleaning of Metallic Substrates' (SPI Lasers application notes); empirical from metal cleaning trials.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-80% overlap for uniform coverage in scanned cleaning; 50% recommended for tin to avoid gaps or uneven heating, optimizing for reflective surfaces and contamination uniformity. Higher overlap for heavy soiling. Source: Process optimization studies in 'Laser Surface Cleaning' (Optics and Lasers in Engineering, 2020); manufacturer guidelines (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).
