  Note:   Typical range for industrial laser cleaning of hard coatings like TiN to ablate contaminants without substrate melting (TiN melting point ~2950°C). Starting at 100W for controlled energy delivery; max limited to avoid thermal damage. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs for 1064 nm cleaning systems; academic review in 'Laser Cleaning of Metallic Substrates' (Optics and Lasers in Engineering, 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance ablation efficiency and heat accumulation on TiN's moderate thermal conductivity (~20 W/m·K). Recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal contaminant removal; slower (100-1000 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals; case studies in Journal of Laser Applications (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for industrial cleaning of TiN to achieve photothermal ablation with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm). Picosecond (ps, 1-100 ps) or femtosecond (fs) reduce HAZ further but increase cost; 50 ns recommended for balance. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on TiN ablation thresholds at 1064 nm.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to ensure pulse overlap without excessive heat buildup on reflective TiN surfaces (reflectivity ~40% at 1064 nm). 50 kHz recommended for TiN to speed up process while monitoring temperature; higher rates (>100 kHz) risk accumulation, lower for precision. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2017) on pulse repetition effects in coating cleaning.

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of contaminants on TiN; damage threshold ~2-3 J/cm² due to high melting point and conductivity. Recommended 1.0 J/cm² for effective cleaning without delamination. Conservative estimate for industrial use. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2021) for TiN at 1064 nm.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter suited for TiN surface preparation; smaller (50-100 μm) for fine details, larger (200-500 μm) for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for uniform coverage on coated surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaning systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy or layered removal on TiN to avoid over-ablation. Recommended 2 passes for complete, damage-free cleaning. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Surface & Coatings Technology (2019).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform fluence distribution on TiN for even cleaning; 60% recommended to prevent gaps or hotspots given beam Gaussian profile. Typical range 30-80% based on scan speed. Source: Process optimization studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2022).
