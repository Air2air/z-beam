  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred over ps/fs for cost-effective cleaning with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm) on heat-sensitive marble; avoids plasma shielding issues in fs. Source: Laser physics in Pouli et al. (2010), Appl. Phys. A.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20 kHz balances cleaning speed and heat accumulation (marble reflectivity ~0.3 at 1064 nm leads to ~70% absorption); higher rates risk cumulative heating/cracking. Source: Academic lit. in J. Appl. Phys. (2012) and IPG Photonics guidelines.

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below marble damage threshold (~5-8 J/cm² for CaCO3 ablation) for safe contaminant removal; 1 J/cm² effective for organic/inorganic dirt without substrate etching. Source: Ablation studies in Sansonetti & Marchesi (2001), Stud. Conserv., and threshold data from ELZA laser research.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots for fine details on marble sculptures; larger for industrial surface prep; 200 μm recommended for even energy distribution on irregular surfaces. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent Inc. and process opt. in Lasers Manuf. Mater. Process. (2018).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Multi-pass for layered contamination (e.g., 2 passes for heavy industrial grime); single pass sufficient for light cleaning; minimizes total exposure time on thermally fragile marble. Source: Industry case studies in J. Cult. Herit. (2014) and practical guidelines from Laser Cleaning Europe.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   70% overlap ensures uniform fluence coverage without gaps on marble's textured surface; higher for heavy soiling to avoid streaking. Source: Process optimization in Opt. Express (2016) and scanning system manuals from scanning galvo providers like Scanlab.
