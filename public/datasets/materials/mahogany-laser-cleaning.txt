  Value:  50 W
  Min:    10 W
  Max:    200 W
  Note:   Typical range for wood cleaning to avoid thermal damage; starting at 50 W for light contamination on mahogany, max ~200 W to prevent charring (based on Nd:YAG systems for cultural heritage wood, e.g., Lee et al., 2016, J. Cult. Herit.).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Faster speeds reduce heat accumulation on heat-sensitive mahogany; 2000 mm/s optimal for light contaminants, slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy; from industry manuals like IPG Photonics laser cleaning guidelines.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for minimal heat-affected zone in wood; 20 ns balances cleaning efficiency and substrate safety, avoiding picosecond/femtosecond for cost (Verho et al., 2019, Opt. Lasers Eng.).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  40 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   40 kHz recommended for mahogany to enable fast cleaning without excessive heat buildup; higher rates (>50 kHz) risk accumulation on low-conductivity wood (academic review in Pouli et al., 2015, Lasers Med. Sci.).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below mahogany damage threshold (~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm); 1.5 J/cm² safe for contaminant removal without ablation (ablation studies on hardwoods, e.g., Ancona et al., 2010, Appl. Surf. Sci.).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   200 μm for precise cleaning on detailed mahogany surfaces; smaller for fine work, larger for broad areas (equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems for wood processing).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Multi-pass (2 recommended) for complete removal on mahogany without single-pass overheating; single pass for light dirt (industry case studies on wood restoration, e.g., EU project LASER4ART).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   60% ensures uniform coverage on irregular wood surfaces like mahogany; 30-50% for speed, higher for heavy contamination (process optimization in Asmus, 1995, Stud. Conserv.).
