power:
  Value:  100 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for polymer cleaning to avoid thermal damage; starting at 100W for light contamination on PET, max below ~300W to prevent melting (PET melting point ~260°C). Source: Studies on Nd:YAG laser cleaning of plastics (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (2000+ mm/s) for light; 1500 mm/s optimal for uniform PET surface prep. Source: Industry manuals from IPG Photonics and Laserax equipment specs.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for 1064 nm cleaning of PET to minimize HAZ; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier; 20 ns balances ablation and low thermal diffusion (PET low conductivity ~0.15 W/mK). Source: Laser physics reviews (Opt. Lasers Eng. 2020).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; 20-50 kHz recommended for PET to enable fast processing without excessive heat accumulation; higher rates increase speed but risk substrate heating. Source: Academic literature on polymer laser ablation (Appl. Surf. Sci. 2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below PET damage threshold (~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe contaminant removal; 0.5-1.5 J/cm² for light cleaning, up to 2.5 for heavy; conservative to account for PET's moderate IR absorption (~20-30%). Source: Ablation threshold studies (J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. 2017).

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (100-300 μm) for fine detail on PET, larger (500-1000 μm) for broad areas; 300 μm recommended for balanced resolution and energy distribution in industrial cleaning. Source: Manufacturer specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-3) for complete removal without damage; 2 passes typical for PET to ensure uniformity while minimizing heat exposure. Source: Case studies in industrial laser cleaning (Proc. SPIE 2021).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage; 40-60% for PET to avoid gaps or overexposure; higher (70-80%) for heavy contamination, lower for speed. Source: Process optimization in laser scanning (Opt. Eng. 2016).
