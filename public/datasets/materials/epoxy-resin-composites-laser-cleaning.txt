  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds ensure fluence delivery without excessive dwell time; 2000 mm/s optimal for light contamination on epoxy composites to prevent charring. Slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contaminants. Source: Industry guidelines from Laserax equipment manuals; case studies in Journal of Cleaner Production (2020) for surface prep on composites.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred over ps/fs for cost-effective cleaning of polymers; 20 ns minimizes heat-affected zone (HAZ) in low-reflectivity epoxy (~5-10% at 1064 nm). Ps/fs (<1 ns) for ultra-precise but higher cost. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on pulse duration effects in composite ablation.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20-50 kHz balances cleaning speed and heat accumulation in epoxy (melting point ~150-200°C); higher rates (>50 kHz) risk thermal buildup leading to matrix degradation. Source: Academic literature in Proceedings of SPIE (2021) on pulsed laser cleaning of polymer composites; trade-offs noted in manufacturer data from Coherent Inc.
