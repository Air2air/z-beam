scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for 1064 nm cleaning of Zirconia; slower for heavy contamination (200-800 mm/s), faster for light (1500-3000 mm/s) to minimize heat input. Recommended 1000 mm/s for balanced removal and surface integrity. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and Optics Express (2021).

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for industrial cleaning of Zirconia to balance efficiency and minimal HAZ; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier applications. 50 ns recommended to limit thermal diffusion in low-conductivity Zirconia. Source: Laser physics review in Applied Surface Science (2019).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  40 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; 40 kHz recommended for Zirconia to achieve fast processing without excessive heat accumulation, given its high melting point but low thermal conductivity. Higher rates (>80 kHz) risk overheating. Source: Academic literature in Journal of Applied Physics (2020).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on Zirconia; damage threshold ~4 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to reflectivity and absorption properties. 1.5 J/cm² recommended for safe cleaning without substrate ablation. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2017).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for precise industrial cleaning; smaller (100-200 μm) for fine surface prep, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for Zirconia to ensure uniform energy distribution and avoid localized overheating. Source: Manufacturer specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy; 2 passes recommended for complete Zirconia cleaning while preserving integrity, based on iterative removal without cumulative damage. Source: Industry case studies in Surface and Coatings Technology (2022).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage in scan patterns; 70% recommended for Zirconia to achieve even cleaning without gaps or overexposure, optimizing for its brittle nature. Typical range 50-80% in pulsed laser processes. Source: Process optimization in International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (2019).
