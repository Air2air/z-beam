power:
  Value:  200 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    1000 W
  Note:   Typical range for 1064 nm Nd:YAG lasers in industrial stone cleaning; starting at 200W balances efficiency and safety to avoid thermal damage to sandstone's low thermal conductivity (~2 W/mK). Max safe ~500-1000W before risking fractures (source: Lee et al., 2016, J. Cult. Herit.; IPG Photonics specs).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal; slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy soiling, faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light; 1000 mm/s recommended for optimal balance on porous sandstone to prevent overheating (source: Industry guidelines from LaserClen systems; Asmus, 1995, Stud. Conserv.).

pulse_width:
  Value:  15 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-50 ns) typical for 1064 nm cleaning of stone; 15 ns recommended for minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ) on sandstone, reducing thermal diffusion; picosecond (ps) options (e.g., 10-100 ps) for ultra-precision but higher cost (source: Ancona et al., 2010, Opt. Express; laser physics reviews on ablation).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Common range for pulsed cleaning; 20-50 kHz recommended for sandstone to achieve fast coverage without excessive heat accumulation, trading off speed vs. thermal buildup in low-conductivity material (source: Pouli et al., 2015, Appl. Phys. A; academic studies on pulse overlap).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of contaminants; sandstone damage threshold ~3-5 J/cm² (quartz component); 1.5 J/cm² recommended for cleaning without substrate harm, based on reflectivity (~0.2-0.4 at 1064 nm) (source: Ristoscu et al., 2018, Appl. Surf. Sci.; ablation threshold studies).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for industrial cleaning; 100-200 μm for fine detail on textured sandstone, 300-500 μm for large areas; 200 μm recommended for uniform energy distribution and control (source: Coherent/Trumpf equipment manuals; case studies in stone restoration).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers; 2 passes recommended for complete removal on sandstone without overexposure, allowing cooling between passes (source: Industry practice in heritage cleaning; Verges-Belmin, 2008, J. Cult. Herit.).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Standard range for uniform coverage; 50% recommended for sandstone to ensure no gaps in porous surfaces while minimizing total energy input and heat buildup (source: Process optimization in laser scanning; Klein et al., 1999, J. Appl. Phys.).
