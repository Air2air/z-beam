  Value:  50 W

  Note:   Typical range for ns-pulsed Nd:YAG lasers in stone cleaning applications; starting at 50W to avoid thermal damage to calcite (decomposition ~825°C). Max limited to prevent substrate ablation. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and studies on carbonate cleaning (e.g., J. Cult. Herit. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Slower speeds for heavy contamination on delicate calcite; 500 mm/s optimal for balance of efficiency and minimal heat input. Range adjusts for light (faster) vs. heavy (slower) contaminants. Source: Industry standards from LaserClus project and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt-Laser).

pulse_width:
  Value:  10 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning with low reflectivity at 1064 nm; 10 ns minimizes heat-affected zone (HAZ) in low-thermal-conductivity calcite (~2 W/m·K). Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precision but higher cost. Source: Laser physics reviews (Opt. Lasers Eng. 2015).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20 kHz balances cleaning speed and heat accumulation; higher rates increase efficiency but risk thermal buildup in calcite due to poor conductivity. Source: Academic literature on pulsed laser cleaning of minerals (Appl. Surf. Sci. 2012).

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below calcite damage threshold (~2.5 J/cm² at 1064 nm ns pulses) for safe contaminant removal; 1 J/cm² recommended for industrial cleaning without ablation. Source: Ablation threshold studies (J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. 2009).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   200 μm for fine surface preparation on calcite; smaller for detail work, larger for broad areas. Matches typical galvo scanner setups. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Multi-pass (2 recommended) for complete removal without exceeding fluence limits; single pass for light contamination. Based on calcite's reflectivity (~5-10% at 1064 nm). Source: Industry case studies on stone restoration (Lasers in the Conservation of Artworks, 2017).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   70% ensures uniform coverage and contaminant removal on irregular calcite surfaces; higher for heavy soiling. Source: Process optimization in laser scanning (Opt. Express 2014).
