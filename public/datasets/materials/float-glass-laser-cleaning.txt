  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm fiber lasers in glass cleaning to balance efficiency and safety; starting at 200 W for contamination removal without overheating. Max safe below 500 W to avoid thermal stress on float glass (melting point ~500°C). Source: IPG Photonics specs and studies in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for 1064 nm cleaning; slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (up to 5000 mm/s) for light residues. Recommended 2000 mm/s for optimal contaminant removal on float glass while minimizing heat accumulation. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Europe guidelines and J. Cleaner Production (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for float glass to enable selective ablation of contaminants with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precision but costlier. 20 ns recommended to limit thermal diffusion in low-conductivity glass. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; higher rates (50-100 kHz) speed up process but risk heat buildup on reflective float glass. 50 kHz balances speed and safety for contamination removal. Trade-off: High freq increases overlap heating. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2017).

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe cleaning below float glass damage threshold (~3 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to partial IR absorption). 0.5 J/cm² recommended for contaminant removal without substrate ablation or cracking. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2021).

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for precision (100-300 μm) vs. large-area cleaning (up to 1000 μm); 300 μm recommended for uniform coverage on float glass surfaces without excessive energy concentration. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent Inc. and industry manuals.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers; 2 passes recommended for complete removal on float glass to ensure uniformity without cumulative damage. Source: Industry case studies in Surface and Coatings Technology (2022).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage; 50% recommended for float glass to avoid gaps or overexposure, balancing efficiency and preventing thermal gradients. Typical 30-80% in optimization. Source: Process studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2019).
