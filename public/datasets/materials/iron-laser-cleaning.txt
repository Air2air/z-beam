  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal on iron; higher speeds (e.g., 2000 mm/s) for light rust/oil, lower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy oxides to ensure ablation without overheating. Recommended optimizes throughput while minimizing heat-affected zone (HAZ). Source: Industry standards from CleanLaser manuals and study in Journal of Cleaner Production (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  30 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for iron cleaning to provide sufficient energy for contaminant ablation with minimal thermal diffusion (due to iron's high conductivity ~80 W/m·K); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces HAZ further but increases cost. 30 ns balances efficacy and safety. Source: Laser physics in Applied Surface Science (2019) on metal ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Common for pulsed cleaning of reflective metals like iron (reflectivity ~40-60% at 1064 nm); 30 kHz recommended for efficient pulse overlap without excessive heat accumulation, which could lead to oxidation. Higher rates speed up process but risk thermal buildup. Source: Academic review in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe removal of contaminants (e.g., rust, paint) on iron without ablating substrate; damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² for iron at 1064 nm. 1.5 J/cm² recommended for effective cleaning below threshold, considering iron's absorption. Source: Ablation studies in Optics Express (2017) and Surface & Coatings Technology (2022).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for industrial applications; smaller (100-200 μm) for precise surface prep on iron, larger for broad area cleaning. 200 μm recommended for uniform energy distribution on iron surfaces, avoiding hotspots. Source: Equipment specs from Raycus and Coherent lasers, per Journal of Laser Applications (2019).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination (e.g., oils), multi-pass (2-3) for heavy rust on iron to achieve complete removal without increasing fluence. 2 passes recommended for industrial prep, allowing progressive cleaning. Source: Case studies in Industrial Laser Solutions (2020) and practical guidelines from laser cleaning consortia.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage during scanning on iron; 70% recommended for even fluence distribution and to prevent gaps in cleaning, especially for irregular surfaces. Lower for faster processes, higher for thoroughness. Source: Process optimization in Applied Optics (2018) and manufacturer guidelines from Adapt Laser Systems.
