pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (10-200 ns) preferred for cost-effective cleaning of stones; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces heat-affected zone (HAZ) but increases cost. 50 ns recommended for Bluestone to minimize thermal diffusion given its melting point ~1200-1400°C. Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on stone ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to control energy delivery; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation on low-conductivity Bluestone. 50 kHz balances speed and safety. Trade-off: high freq increases throughput but requires monitoring for substrate heating. Source: Academic literature in J. Appl. Phys. (2021) on pulse repetition in heritage cleaning.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe removal of contaminants like soot or oxides; Bluestone damage threshold ~4-5 J/cm² due to moderate reflectivity (~20-30% at 1064 nm). Recommended 1.5 J/cm² avoids ablation of substrate while effective for industrial prep. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. (2017) for sandstones.

Source: Industry practice in case studies from Laser Cleaning Europe consortium and Opt. Express (2019).
