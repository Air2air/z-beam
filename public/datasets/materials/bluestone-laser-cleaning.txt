power:
  Value:  100 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    300 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial cleaning of natural stones like Bluestone using 1064 nm fiber lasers; starting at 100W balances efficiency and safety to avoid thermal damage to porous substrate. Max limited by Bluestone's low thermal conductivity (~1-2 W/m·K) to prevent cracking. Source: Manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics YLR series and academic review in 'Laser Cleaning of Stone Surfaces' (J. Cult. Herit., 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contamination removal on stone; slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy rust/graffiti, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light dust. Recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal balance on Bluestone to ensure uniform cleaning without overheating. Source: Industry standards from Adapt Laser Systems manuals and case studies in Opt. Lasers Eng. (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (10-200 ns) preferred for cost-effective cleaning of stones; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces heat-affected zone (HAZ) but increases cost. 50 ns recommended for Bluestone to minimize thermal diffusion given its melting point ~1200-1400°C. Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on stone ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to control energy delivery; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation on low-conductivity Bluestone. 50 kHz balances speed and safety. Trade-off: high freq increases throughput but requires monitoring for substrate heating. Source: Academic literature in J. Appl. Phys. (2021) on pulse repetition in heritage cleaning.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe removal of contaminants like soot or oxides; Bluestone damage threshold ~4-5 J/cm² due to moderate reflectivity (~20-30% at 1064 nm). Recommended 1.5 J/cm² avoids ablation of substrate while effective for industrial prep. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. (2017) for sandstones.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for cleaning; smaller (100-200 μm) for fine details on textured Bluestone, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for uniform coverage in surface preparation. Source: Equipment specifications from CleanLaser CL series and process optimization in Lasers Manuf. Mater. Process. (2022).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers to build fluence gradually and prevent damage. 2 passes recommended for Bluestone to ensure complete removal without excessive heat exposure. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Laser Cleaning Europe consortium and Opt. Express (2019).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Overlap ensures uniform fluence distribution; 30-50% for fast large-area cleaning, 60-80% for detailed work on irregular Bluestone surfaces. 60% recommended for even coverage in contamination removal. Source: Process optimization studies in J. Laser Appl. (2020) for stone substrates.
