Source: Laser physics reviews in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on pulsed laser cleaning of organics.

  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for 1064 nm cleaning; 50 kHz recommended for MDF to achieve fast coverage without excessive heat accumulation, trading off speed vs. thermal buildup in resinous material. Higher rates (>80 kHz) risk damage. Source: Academic literature, e.g., J. Appl. Phys. (2021) on pulse repetition in ablation.

  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe removal of contaminants from MDF; damage threshold ~5-10 J/cm² due to charring/melting (MDF decomposition ~200-300°C). Recommended 2 J/cm² for effective cleaning below threshold. Source: Ablation studies in Wood Sci. Technol. (2017) and laser threshold measurements.

  Note:   Beam diameter for precise industrial cleaning; 200 μm recommended for MDF to balance resolution on fibrous surface and large-area coverage, avoiding excessive heat concentration. Finer for details (<150 μm), larger for broad prep (300-500 μm). Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf lasers.
