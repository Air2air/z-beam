power:
  Value:  100 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial laser cleaning of wood-based materials like MDF to avoid charring; starting at 100W for light contamination. Max safe before substrate damage (thermal degradation) is ~500W at 1064 nm. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and studies on laser ablation of composites (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contamination removal on MDF; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s) to ensure ablation, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light. Recommended 1500 mm/s balances efficiency and heat control. Source: Industry manuals from CleanLaser and process optimization in Opt. Lasers Eng. (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for MDF cleaning due to cost-effectiveness; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces HAZ but unnecessary for most industrial apps. 20 ns minimizes thermal effects on low-conductivity MDF. Source: Laser physics reviews in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on pulsed laser cleaning of organics.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for 1064 nm cleaning; 50 kHz recommended for MDF to achieve fast coverage without excessive heat accumulation, trading off speed vs. thermal buildup in resinous material. Higher rates (>80 kHz) risk damage. Source: Academic literature, e.g., J. Appl. Phys. (2021) on pulse repetition in ablation.

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe removal of contaminants from MDF; damage threshold ~5-10 J/cm² due to charring/melting (MDF decomposition ~200-300°C). Recommended 2 J/cm² for effective cleaning below threshold. Source: Ablation studies in Wood Sci. Technol. (2017) and laser threshold measurements.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for precise industrial cleaning; 200 μm recommended for MDF to balance resolution on fibrous surface and large-area coverage, avoiding excessive heat concentration. Finer for details (<150 μm), larger for broad prep (300-500 μm). Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf lasers.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy; 2 passes recommended for complete MDF surface prep without overexposure. Source: Industry case studies in laser cleaning of wood composites (e.g., Proc. SPIE 2022).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage on irregular MDF surfaces; 60% recommended for optimal cleaning without gaps or over-ablation, typical range 30-80% based on scan patterns. Source: Process optimization in J. Mater. Process. Technol. (2019).
