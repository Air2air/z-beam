  Max:    300 W
  Note:   Typical range for Nd:YAG laser cleaning of reflective metals; starting at 100W to balance ablation and heat input. Lasers Eng. 2018); manufacturer specs like IPG Photonics YLR series limit max to prevent substrate damage.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contamination removal on metals; higher for light contaminants (e.g., oxides), lower for heavy. Recommended 1500 mm/s for Terbium to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ) due to poor thermal dissipation. Source: Industry standards from Laser Clean Corp. manuals; case studies on rare earth alloys (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for industrial cleaning to achieve plasma-mediated ablation with minimal HAZ; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precision but costlier. 20 ns recommended for Terbium to limit thermal diffusion. Source: Laser physics research on metal ablation (e.g., Ancona et al., Appl. Surf. Sci. 2010); avoids fs due to Terbium's reflectivity at 1064 nm.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; higher rates speed up process but risk heat accumulation in low-conductivity materials like Terbium. 30 kHz balances efficiency and safety. Trade-off: >50 kHz may cause subsurface heating. Source: Academic literature on Nd:YAG cleaning (e.g., Lee et al., J. Phys. D 2015); adapted for rare earth metals.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Terbium's ablation threshold (~4 J/cm² estimated for 1064 nm) for safe contaminant removal without substrate damage. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for light industrial cleaning. Terbium's high reflectivity (~70% at IR) requires higher fluence than dielectrics. Source: Ablation threshold studies on similar metals (e.g., Rubenchik et al., Appl. Opt. 2017); conservative due to limited Terbium-specific data.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for precise cleaning; smaller for fine details, larger for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for Terbium surface preparation to ensure uniform fluence without over-focusing heat. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems; optimized for metal reflectivity in industrial applications.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for heavy layers; 2 passes recommended for complete Terbium cleaning without excessive exposure. Multi-pass allows lower fluence per pass to protect soft substrate. Source: Industry practice from case studies on alloy cleaning (e.g., Proc. SPIE 2019); Terbium's malleability favors fewer passes.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage; 50% recommended for Terbium to avoid gaps or over-ablation, balancing speed and efficacy. Higher (70-80%) for heavy contaminants. Source: Process optimization studies (e.g., Katayama et al., J. Laser Micro/Nanoeng. 2012); standard for scanned laser cleaning of metals.
