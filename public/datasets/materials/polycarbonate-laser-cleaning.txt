power:
  Value:  50 W
  Min:    20 W
  Max:    200 W
  Note:   Typical range for pulsed fiber lasers in polymer cleaning to avoid thermal damage; starting at 50W for light contamination on PC, max limited by melting point (~147°C) and low thermal conductivity. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs for cleaning applications; conservative for polymers per studies in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Slower speeds for polymers to control heat buildup; 500 mm/s optimal for uniform contaminant removal without substrate ablation. Range adjusts for light (faster) vs heavy contamination. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals; case studies in Journal of Cleaner Production (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  10 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for PC to balance ablation efficiency and minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ <10μm); picosecond (ps) reduces HAZ further but increases cost. Femtosecond risks over-ablation. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on polymer ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   30 kHz balances cleaning speed and heat accumulation for PC's low thermal conductivity; higher rates (>50 kHz) accelerate process but risk melting. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2021) on pulsed laser cleaning of thermoplastics.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below PC damage threshold (~1-2 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate absorption); 0.5 J/cm² for safe contaminant removal. Conservative to prevent charring. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) for polycarbonate at near-IR wavelengths.

spot_size:
  Value:  100 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   100 μm diameter for precise surface preparation on PC; smaller for fine details, larger for broad areas. Matches typical Gaussian beam profiles. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaning systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Multi-pass (2 recommended) for complete removal of contaminants without single-pass overheating; single pass for very light soiling. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Surface & Coatings Technology (2022) on polymer cleaning.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   60% overlap ensures uniform coverage and avoids gaps in cleaning; higher for heavy contamination to build fluence gradually. Source: Process optimization studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2020).
