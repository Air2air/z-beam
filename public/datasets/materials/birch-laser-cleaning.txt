power:
  Value:  50 W
  Min:    20 W
  Max:    200 W
  Note:   Typical range for wood cleaning at 1064 nm to avoid charring; start at 50 W for light contamination on birch (low thermal conductivity ~0.16 W/mK limits heat dissipation). Max safe below substrate damage threshold. Source: Studies on Nd:YAG laser cleaning of wood (e.g., Journal of Cultural Heritage, 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contaminant removal without excessive heat buildup; recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal balance on birch. Slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light. Source: Industry manuals from IPG Photonics and laser cleaning equipment standards.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime typical for industrial cleaning of organics like birch contaminants; 20 ns recommended for minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ) due to birch's low melting point (~200-300°C for lignin). Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond better for ultra-precision but less common industrially. Source: Laser physics research in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2020).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for pulsed cleaning to balance speed and heat; 30 kHz recommended for birch to prevent accumulation (high freq increases speed but risks thermal damage on low-conductivity wood). Trade-off: >50 kHz faster but monitor for charring. Source: Academic literature on wood surface treatment (Applied Surface Science, 2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant ablation; birch damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate reflectivity (~20-30%). Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for cleaning without substrate harm. Source: Ablation threshold studies on hardwoods (Journal of Laser Applications, 2017).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for precise industrial cleaning; 200 μm recommended for birch surface preparation (fine detail for contamination removal). Smaller (100-200 μm) for detailed work, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Typically 1-3 passes for light to moderate contamination on birch; multi-pass (2 recommended) ensures complete removal without single-pass overheating. Single pass for very light dirt. Source: Industry case studies on wood restoration (Laser Cleaning Conference proceedings, 2021).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage in scan patterns; 60% recommended for birch to avoid gaps or overexposure (higher overlap for heavy contamination, lower for efficiency). Typical range 30-80% in optimization. Source: Process studies in International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (2016).
