  Max:    300 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm laser cleaning of reflective metals like brass to avoid thermal damage; starting at 100W for light contamination. Max limited by brass's high reflectivity and melting point (~900°C). Source: CleanLaser and IPG Photonics specs for metal cleaning systems.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower for heavy contamination (e.g., oxides), faster for light rust/oil. Recommended 1500 mm/s for uniform brass surface prep. Source: Industry standards from Laserax and academic studies on scan optimization (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2018).

pulse_width:
  Value:  10 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning of brass; 10 ns minimizes heat-affected zone (HAZ) due to brass's thermal conductivity (~120 W/mK). Picosecond (<1 ns) for ultra-fine work but less common industrially. Source: Laser physics research in Opt. Lasers Eng. (2015) on metal ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20-50 kHz balances speed and heat accumulation on reflective brass; higher rates (>50 kHz) risk overheating despite good conductivity. Source: Academic literature on pulsed laser cleaning (e.g., Appl. Surf. Sci. 2019), noting trade-offs for Cu-based alloys.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below brass damage threshold (~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for contaminant removal without substrate ablation; 1.5 J/cm² safe for oils/rust. Source: Ablation threshold studies in J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. (2017) for brass and similar alloys.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   200-300 μm ideal for brass to achieve uniform cleaning on detailed surfaces; smaller for precision, larger for broad areas. Accounts for beam divergence on reflective surfaces. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaning heads.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   1-3 passes typical for complete removal of contaminants on brass; multi-pass for layered dirt to stay below damage threshold. Single pass sufficient for light prep. Source: Industry case studies (e.g., Laser Cleaning Technol. reports) and practical optimization.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   70% overlap ensures uniform coverage without excessive heat buildup on brass; higher for fine contaminants, lower for speed. Source: Process optimization studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. (2020) for scanned laser cleaning of metals.
