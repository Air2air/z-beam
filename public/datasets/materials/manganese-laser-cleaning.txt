power:
  Value:  200 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    1000 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm laser cleaning of reflective metals like manganese; starting at 200 W for contamination removal without excessive heat input. Max safe before substrate melting (Mn melting point ~1246°C) is ~1000 W with proper scanning. Source: Manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics and Coherent for metal cleaning systems; academic review in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for removing light to heavy contamination on manganese; slower speeds (100-500 mm/s) for heavy oxides, faster (500-2000 mm/s) for light surface prep. Recommended 500 mm/s for optimal balance of efficiency and minimal thermal accumulation given Mn's moderate thermal conductivity (~7.8 W/m·K). Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Association guidelines and equipment manuals from Adapt Laser Systems.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) preferred for cost-effective cleaning of manganese; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but higher cost. 20 ns recommended to reduce heat-affected zone on reflective Mn surfaces while ablating contaminants. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2020) on metal ablation thresholds at 1064 nm.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning applications; 50 kHz recommended for manganese to achieve fast processing without excessive heat buildup (high freq increases accumulation on low-conductivity metals like Mn). Trade-off: higher rates speed up cleaning but risk thermal damage. Source: Academic literature in Journal of Applied Physics (2019) on pulse repetition effects in laser ablation of alloys.

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on manganese; damage threshold ~4-5 J/cm² due to high reflectivity (~60% at 1064 nm) and melting point. 2 J/cm² recommended for safe cleaning of oxides without substrate ablation. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Surface and Coatings Technology (2017) for transition metals like Mn.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for industrial cleaning; smaller (50-200 μm) for fine surface prep on manganese, larger (200-500 μm) for broad area decontamination. 200 μm recommended for uniform energy distribution on Mn's surface. Source: Equipment specifications from Trumpf and SPI Lasers for 1064 nm cleaning heads.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   1-5 passes typical for complete removal of contaminants; single pass for light rust/oxides on manganese, multi-pass for heavy buildup. 2 passes recommended to ensure thorough cleaning without risking Mn substrate damage. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Laser Institute of America reports on metal surface preparation.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-80% overlap for uniform coverage in scanning; 50% recommended for manganese to avoid gaps in cleaning while minimizing over-processing and heat input. Higher overlap for heavy contamination. Source: Process optimization studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021) on scan strategies for metallic substrates.
