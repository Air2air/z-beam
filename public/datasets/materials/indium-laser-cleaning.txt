scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective for contamination removal on reflective metals; slower for heavy contamination (e.g., oxides), faster for light. Recommended balances speed and fluence control. Source: Industry standards from Laserax manuals; adapted for Indium's high reflectivity reducing absorption.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning with minimal HAZ on soft metals; picosecond (ps) for ultra-precision but overkill for Indium. Avoids excessive heat buildup due to Indium's conductivity (82 W/m·K). Source: Laser physics in J. Appl. Phys. 2015 on ns ablation thresholds for low-mp metals.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for industrial cleaning; moderate rate prevents heat accumulation on heat-sensitive Indium while enabling efficient coverage. Higher rates increase speed but risk thermal damage. Source: Academic lit. in Appl. Surf. Sci. 2020 on pulse train effects for metals.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Indium's ablation threshold (~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to reflectivity >90%); safe for contaminant removal without substrate melting. Conservative to account for variability in contamination. Source: Ablation studies in Phys. Procedia 2013 for soft metals; estimated for Indium.

spot_size:

  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller for precise surface prep on Indium components (e.g., electronics); larger for broad areas. Balances fluence and coverage. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent Inc. cleaning systems; suitable for fine Indium features.

pass_count:

  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes

  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
