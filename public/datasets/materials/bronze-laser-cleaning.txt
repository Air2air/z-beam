  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm fiber lasers cleaning metals like bronze; starting at 200W balances efficiency and safety to avoid overheating due to bronze's high reflectivity and thermal conductivity (~100-300 W/m·K). Max limited to prevent substrate melting (~950°C). Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and studies on copper alloys (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contaminant removal without excessive heat buildup; slower (500-1500 mm/s) for heavy oxidation, faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light dirt. Recommended 2000 mm/s for optimal bronze cleaning to ensure uniform ablation of contaminants. Source: Industry standards from Laserax and Adapt Laser Systems manuals.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning of reflective metals; 5-20 ns minimizes heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm) compared to longer pulses, while picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) reduce thermal damage further but increase cost. 20 ns typical for 1064 nm Nd:YAG on bronze. Source: Laser physics research (Opt. Lasers Eng. 2015; ablation studies on Cu-Sn alloys).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for pulsed cleaning to achieve high throughput; 10-50 kHz for bronze to balance speed and heat accumulation (higher rates risk melting due to poor IR absorption). Trade-off: higher freq accelerates cleaning but requires faster scans. Recommended 30 kHz. Source: Academic literature (Appl. Surf. Sci. 2019; metal cleaning optimization).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for removing contaminants (e.g., oxides, oils) below bronze damage threshold (~2-4 J/cm² for ablation onset); conservative 0.5-1.5 J/cm² for light cleaning, up to 3 J/cm² for heavy. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² safe for surface prep. Accounts for bronze's reflectivity (~60-70% at 1064 nm). Source: Ablation threshold studies (J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. 2017; Cu alloys).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for precise control; smaller (100-200 μm) for detailed bronze artifacts, larger (200-500 μm) for industrial surfaces. Recommended 200 μm for uniform energy distribution on bronze without pinpoint damage. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaners.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for stubborn layers like patina on bronze; recommended 2 passes ensures complete removal with minimal risk to substrate integrity. Source: Industry case studies (e.g., heritage cleaning in Stud. Conserv. 2020; industrial reports from Laser Cleaning Inc.).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage without gaps or over-ablation; 30-50% for large areas, 50-80% for fine uniformity on bronze to prevent uneven heating. Recommended 50% for balanced process efficiency. Source: Process optimization studies (Opt. Laser Technol. 2016; scanning parameter effects on metals).
