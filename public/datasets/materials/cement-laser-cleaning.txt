  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns

Recommended 20 ns minimizes thermal damage. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2018) by Anisimov et al. on pulse duration effects for dielectrics like cement.

  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to control heat accumulation in low-thermal-conductivity cement; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but risk overheating/cracking. Recommended 30 kHz balances speed and safety. Source: Academic literature in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2020) by Pou et al. on repetition rate trade-offs for stone materials.

  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant ablation on cement without substrate damage; damage threshold ~4 J/cm² for Portland cement due to high reflectivity (~30% at 1064 nm) and low melting point (~1200°C). Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe removal of surface layers. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2016) by Colombier et al. on concrete-like materials.
