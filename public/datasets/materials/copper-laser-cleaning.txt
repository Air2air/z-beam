power:
  Value:  200 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm fiber lasers cleaning copper; start at 200 W to balance efficiency and avoid thermal damage due to copper's high reflectivity and conductivity. Max safe before substrate melting ~500 W with proper scanning. Source: IPG Photonics specs and study in Journal of Laser Applications (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal; 2000 mm/s recommended for light contaminants on copper to minimize heat accumulation. Slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy oxidation, faster for surface prep. Source: CleanLaser equipment manuals and Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime typical for copper cleaning at 1064 nm; 50 ns recommended for minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ) while ablating contaminants without melting substrate. Picosecond (ps) options (1-10 ps) reduce HAZ further but increase cost. Femtosecond less common industrially. Source: Laser physics review in Applied Surface Science (2019).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for pulsed cleaning; 50 kHz balances speed and heat management on reflective copper—higher rates (>80 kHz) accelerate cleaning but risk accumulation. Lower for heavy contaminants. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2017) on metal ablation.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant removal; copper damage threshold ~4-5 J/cm² at 1064 nm, so 1.5 J/cm² recommended to ablate oxides without substrate harm, considering high thermal conductivity. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Physical Review B (2015) and Journal of Applied Physics (2021).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for industrial cleaning; 200 μm recommended for copper to achieve uniform fluence on detailed surfaces vs. larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. Smaller for precision. Source: Laser equipment specs from Coherent Inc. and process studies in Surface and Coatings Technology (2019).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Typically 1-3 passes for complete removal on copper; 2 passes recommended for multi-layer contaminants to ensure uniformity without excessive heat. Single pass for light cleaning. Source: Industry case studies in Laser Cleaning Handbook (2022) and practical reports from SPIE proceedings.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Standard for uniform coverage; 60% recommended for copper to prevent gaps in cleaning while avoiding overexposure and thermal stress. Higher (70-80%) for heavy contamination, lower for speed. Source: Process optimization in Journal of Cleaner Production (2020).
