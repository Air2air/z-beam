  Max:    2000 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm fiber lasers used in metal cleaning; starting at 200 W balances efficiency and safety for reflective Tungsten to avoid excessive reflection losses. Max safe below substrate melting threshold (~3422°C). Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and studies on laser cleaning of refractory metals (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal on high-conductivity metals like Tungsten; 2000 mm/s recommended for optimal balance of cleaning rate and fluence control. Slower for heavy contamination (e.g., oxides), faster for light surface prep. Source: Industry standards from Laserax and Adapt Laser Systems manuals.

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime typical for industrial cleaning to ablate contaminants without deep penetration; 50 ns recommended for minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ) on Tungsten due to its high thermal conductivity. Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precision but less common industrially. Source: Laser physics research in Opt. Express (2015) on pulse duration effects in metal ablation.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for pulsed 1064 nm lasers in cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for Tungsten to achieve fast processing while limiting heat accumulation from its reflectivity. Higher rates increase speed but risk thermal buildup. Source: Academic literature, e.g., Appl. Surf. Sci. (2020) on repetition rate optimization for refractory metals.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant ablation on Tungsten; damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to high reflectivity (~60%). 1.5 J/cm² recommended for safe oxide/carbon removal without substrate damage. Source: Ablation threshold studies in J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. (2017) and fusion reactor cleaning papers (e.g., Nucl. Fusion 2019).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Typical beam diameter for precise industrial cleaning; 200 μm recommended for Tungsten to focus energy on contaminants while covering large areas efficiently. Smaller for fine details, larger for broad prep. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaning systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Usually 1-3 passes for complete removal on metals; 2 passes recommended for Tungsten to ensure uniform cleaning of stubborn contaminants like tungsten oxides without overexposure. Multi-pass preferred over single high-fluence pass to minimize damage. Source: Industry case studies in laser cleaning of fusion components (e.g., J. Nucl. Mater. 2021).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Standard for uniform coverage in scanned laser cleaning; 50% recommended for Tungsten to avoid gaps in high-reflectivity surfaces while preventing excessive heat overlap. Higher (60-80%) for heavy contamination. Source: Process optimization studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. (2019).
