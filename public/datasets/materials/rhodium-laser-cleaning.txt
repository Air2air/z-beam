power:
  Value:  150 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial Nd:YAG laser cleaners on reflective metals like Rhodium to balance efficiency and avoid thermal damage; recommended starting at 150W for contamination removal. Rhodium's high reflectivity (~65% at 1064 nm) requires higher power than less reflective substrates. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and studies on PGM cleaning (e.g., Opt. Lasers Eng. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for 1064 nm cleaning of metals; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (3000-5000 mm/s). Recommended 2000 mm/s for Rhodium to ensure contaminant removal without substrate heating, considering its thermal conductivity (150 W/m·K). Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and process optimization in Surf. Coat. Technol. 2020.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning of Rhodium; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond for ultra-minimal HAZ but less common industrially. 20 ns recommended to minimize heat-affected zone on high-melting-point Rhodium (1964°C). Avoid fs for industrial scale due to complexity. Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Phys. A 2019 on metal ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to achieve high throughput; higher rates (50-100 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation on reflective Rhodium. 50 kHz balances speed and safety. Trade-off: low freq reduces overlap heating. Source: Academic literature on Nd:YAG cleaning (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2021).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Rhodium's ablation threshold (~4-6 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to high reflectivity) for safe contaminant removal. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for light industrial cleaning; higher for heavy contamination risks damage. Conservative estimate based on similar PGMs. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Opt. Express 2017 and Appl. Surf. Sci. 2022.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (50-200 μm) for fine surface preparation on Rhodium; larger (200-500 μm) for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for uniform cleaning without excessive overlap heating. Suited to Rhodium's use in plating/electronics. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf industrial lasers.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination; multi-pass (2-5) for heavy or uneven layers on Rhodium. Recommended 2 passes for complete removal while preserving substrate integrity. Based on industrial practice for precious metals. Source: Case studies in Ind. Laser Sol. 2019 and process reports from Adapt Laser Systems.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage without gaps or excessive energy input; 60% recommended for Rhodium to account for beam divergence and reflectivity, preventing uneven cleaning. Typical range from process optimization. Source: Studies in Laser Ablation and Surf. Eng. (Proc. SPIE 2020).
