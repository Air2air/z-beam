  Note:   Typical range for delicate organic materials like bamboo to remove surface contaminants without charring; starting at 50 W for controlled ablation. Max safe to avoid substrate damage based on thermal sensitivity of lignocellulosic fibers. Source: Studies on laser cleaning of wood (e.g., Journal of Cultural Heritage, 2018, adapted for bamboo).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (2000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1000 mm/s for bamboo to minimize thermal damage. Source: Industry manuals from IPG Photonics and Laserax equipment guidelines for organic surfaces.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for bamboo to achieve ablation with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) reduce HAZ further but are costlier and less common for industrial cleaning at 1064 nm. Avoid longer pulses to prevent burning. Source: Laser physics research in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2020) on pulsed laser interactions with biopolymers.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for cleaning organics; 20-50 kHz recommended for bamboo to enable fast processing while limiting heat accumulation due to low thermal conductivity. Higher rates (>100 kHz) increase speed but risk overheating. Source: Academic literature, e.g., Applied Surface Science (2019) on pulse repetition effects in laser ablation of natural fibers.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on bamboo; damage threshold ~5-10 J/cm² for lignocellulosic materials, so stay below 5 J/cm². Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning without substrate ablation. Conservative due to bamboo's low melting/decomposition point (~200-300°C). Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) for wood and cellulose.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers; recommended 2 passes for bamboo to ensure complete removal while monitoring for thermal effects. Source: Industry case studies on laser surface preparation for natural materials (e.g., SPIE Proceedings, 2021).

overlap_ratio:

Typical range from process optimization. Source: Optimization studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2022).
