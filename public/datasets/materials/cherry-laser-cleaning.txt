scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contaminant removal on wood; 500 mm/s recommended for optimal balance of cleaning efficiency and heat control on cherry wood. Slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (1000-2000 mm/s) for light. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Europe guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for wood to ablate contaminants with minimal heat-affected zone; 20 ns recommended for cherry to reduce thermal diffusion given wood's low conductivity (~0.16 W/m·K). Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond optional for ultra-precision but less common industrially. Source: Laser physics research in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2020).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning of organics; 30 kHz balances speed and heat accumulation on cherry wood (reflectivity ~40% at 1064 nm, melting point N/A but charring >200°C). Higher rates increase speed but risk buildup. Source: Academic literature, e.g., Applied Surface Science (2019) on wood laser processing.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant removal; cherry wood damage threshold ~8-12 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to low thermal conductivity. 1.5 J/cm² recommended for cleaning without substrate harm. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) for hardwoods.
