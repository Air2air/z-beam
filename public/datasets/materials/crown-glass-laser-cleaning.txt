scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contaminant removal on glass; slower speeds (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (500-2000 mm/s) for light; 500 mm/s optimal to minimize heat accumulation on low-thermal-conductivity Crown Glass. Source: Industry standards from Laser Institute of America guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for Crown Glass to balance ablation efficiency and minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier applications; ns avoids excessive plasma formation on reflective glass surfaces. Source: Laser physics studies in 'Pulsed Laser Cleaning of Glass' (Applied Surface Science, 2015), recommending ns for industrial use.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard range for 1064 nm cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for Crown Glass to achieve fast coverage without significant heat buildup, as higher rates (>50 kHz) increase thermal load on glass with moderate reflectivity (~4-8% at 1064 nm). Trade-off: higher freq speeds process but risks microcracks. Source: Academic literature in Journal of Applied Physics (2020) on pulse train effects in glass cleaning.

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant ablation on Crown Glass; damage threshold ~10-15 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to low absorption; 2 J/cm² recommended to stay below threshold while effective for oxides/dirt removal, considering glass melting point ~500°C. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Optics Express (2017) and ISO 21254 standards for laser-induced damage.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for uniform cleaning; 100-200 μm for fine surface prep on optical Crown Glass, up to 500 μm for large-area industrial use; 200 μm balances resolution and efficiency without focusing-induced hotspots. Source: Equipment specifications from Trumpf and SPI Lasers for 1064 nm systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Typically 1-2 passes for light contamination on Crown Glass, up to 5 for heavy buildup; multi-pass at lower fluence preferred to avoid cumulative thermal damage. Source: Industry case studies in 'Industrial Laser Cleaning Applications' (Proceedings of SPIE, 2019) for glass substrates.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Overlap ensures uniform coverage without gaps or over-ablation; 70% recommended for Crown Glass to achieve even cleaning on flat surfaces while minimizing total energy input and thermal gradients. Source: Process optimization in Laser & Photonics Reviews (2016), typical 50-80% for scanned beam cleaning.
