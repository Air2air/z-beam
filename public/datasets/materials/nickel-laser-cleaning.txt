  Value:  100 W
  Min:    20 W
  Max:    300 W
  Note:   Typical range for pulsed fiber lasers at 1064 nm used in industrial cleaning of reflective metals like nickel; starting at 100 W balances efficiency and safety to avoid thermal damage due to nickel's high reflectivity (~60% at 1064 nm) and thermal conductivity (90 W/m·K). Max safe before substrate melting (1455°C) is ~300 W with short pulses. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and study in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018) on metal surface cleaning.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal on nickel; slower for heavy oxides (500-1000 mm/s) to ensure ablation, faster for light contaminants (2000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal balance of throughput and uniform cleaning without excessive heat buildup. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and Journal of Laser Applications (2020) case study on nickel alloys.

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (10-200 ns) is typical and cost-effective for nickel cleaning at 1064 nm; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) reduces heat-affected zone (HAZ <1 μm) but is unnecessary for most industrial apps. Recommended 50 ns for minimal HAZ (~5-10 μm) on nickel's reflective surface. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) comparing pulse regimes for metals.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but increase heat accumulation on nickel due to its conductivity, risking subsurface damage. Recommended 30 kHz for nickel to optimize energy delivery without overheating. Source: Academic literature in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021) on frequency effects in metal ablation.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant removal; nickel's ablation threshold at 1064 nm is ~1-2 J/cm² due to high reflectivity, with damage threshold ~4-6 J/cm² to avoid melting. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for effective cleaning below damage threshold. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) and Optics Express (2022) on nickel surfaces.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for cleaning; smaller (50-100 μm) for fine details, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. Recommended 200 μm for nickel to achieve uniform fluence distribution and control heat input on reflective surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems, per industrial cleaning guidelines.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers to ensure complete removal without exceeding damage thresholds. Recommended 2 passes for nickel in industrial prep to minimize thermal stress. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Surface and Coatings Technology (2019) on nickel oxide removal.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Typical 30-80% for uniform coverage; higher overlap ensures no gaps but increases heat exposure on nickel. Recommended 60% for balanced uniformity and efficiency in scan line coverage. Source: Process optimization studies in International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (2020) for laser cleaning of metals.
