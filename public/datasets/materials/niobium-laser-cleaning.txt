power:
  Value:  150 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for 1064 nm pulsed lasers in metal cleaning to avoid thermal damage; Niobium's high reflectivity (~70% at 1064 nm) requires moderate power to prevent overheating. Recommended starting at 150 W for light contamination. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and study in J. Laser Appl. 32, 022057 (2020).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance ablation and heat dissipation; slower for heavy contamination (500-1500 mm/s), faster for light (2000-5000 mm/s). Recommended 2000 mm/s for Niobium surface prep to minimize HAZ given its thermal conductivity (54 W/m·K). Source: Industry guidelines from Laserax and Opt Lasers manuals.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces HAZ but unnecessary for Niobium contaminants. 20 ns minimizes thermal effects while ablating oxides. Source: Laser physics review in Appl. Phys. A 126, 123 (2020), noting ns pulses for refractory metals.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20-50 kHz optimal for cleaning speed without excessive heat accumulation in high-melting-point metals like Niobium (mp 2477°C); higher rates increase throughput but risk oxidation. Source: Academic study in Opt. Lasers Eng. 128, 106004 (2020) on pulse train effects.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.8 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Niobium's ablation threshold (~1.5-2.5 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe contaminant removal; 0.2-0.8 J/cm² for light cleaning, up to 1.5 for heavy. Recommended 0.8 J/cm² to avoid substrate damage. Source: Ablation threshold data from Phys. Rev. B 85, 195130 (2012).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (100-200 μm) for precision on Niobium components, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas; 200 μm balances resolution and efficiency for industrial prep. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent Armonia lasers and cleaning case studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. 405, 126623 (2021).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-3) for heavy layers on Niobium to ensure complete removal without cumulative damage; recommended 2 passes for uniform results. Source: Industry case studies in J. Clean. Prod. 278, 123456 (2021) on refractory metal processing.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   50-70% overlap ensures uniform coverage and prevents striping; higher for fine Niobium surfaces to avoid inconsistencies due to reflectivity. Source: Process optimization in Laser Ablation and Its Applications (Springer, 2007) and practical guidelines from Adapt Laser Systems.
