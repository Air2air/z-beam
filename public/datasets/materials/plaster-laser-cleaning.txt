power:
  Value:  100 W
  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial laser cleaning of delicate materials like plaster to avoid thermal damage; starting at 100 W for light contamination removal. Max safe below 500 W to prevent substrate cracking due to plaster's low thermal conductivity (~0.5 W/m·K). Source: Manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics YLR series and studies on laser cleaning of gypsum-based plasters (e.g., J. Cult. Herit. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for 1064 nm cleaning on plaster; slower speeds (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light. Recommended 1500 mm/s balances efficiency and safety. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Europe guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning of plaster; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces HAZ but less common at 1064 nm for industrial use. 20 ns minimizes heat accumulation on low-melting-point plaster (~150°C dehydration). Source: Laser physics research in Opt. Lasers Eng. (2015) on ablation of calcium sulfate materials.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed 1064 nm lasers in cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for plaster to achieve fast coverage without excessive heat buildup, trading off speed vs. thermal load. Higher rates (>50 kHz) risk accumulation on low-conductivity substrates. Source: Academic literature, e.g., Appl. Surf. Sci. (2019) on stone/plaster cleaning parameters.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.8 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal without ablating plaster; damage threshold ~1.5-2.0 J/cm² for gypsum due to reflectivity (~0.3 at 1064 nm) and low melting point. Recommended 0.8 J/cm² for safe surface preparation. Source: Ablation threshold studies in J. Appl. Phys. (2017) and historical artifact cleaning papers.

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for precision vs. area coverage; 300 μm recommended for plaster to focus energy on contaminants while sparing porous substrate. Smaller for fine details, larger for broad industrial prep. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaners.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers on plaster to gradually remove without damage. Recommended 2 passes for uniform results in industrial settings. Source: Industry case studies, e.g., restoration projects in Constr. Build. Mater. (2020).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage without gaps or overexposure; 50% recommended for plaster cleaning to optimize energy distribution and minimize uneven heating. Typical 30-80% based on scan patterns. Source: Process optimization studies in Lasers Manuf. Mater. Process. (2016).
