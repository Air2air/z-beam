power:
  Value:  100 W

  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial Nd:YAG laser cleaning systems; starting at 100W balances efficiency and safety for Ti's high reflectivity and melting point (~1668°C). Max avoids substrate damage via thermal accumulation. Source: IPG Photonics specs and studies on Ti alloy cleaning (e.g., J. Laser Appl. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective for contamination removal; slower for heavy contaminants (e.g., oxides), faster for light. Recommended 1500 mm/s optimizes fluence without overheating Ti's conductive surface. Source: Industry manuals (CleanLaser) and process optimization in Opt. Lasers Eng. (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for Ti cleaning to ablate contaminants while minimizing heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm); picosecond/femtosecond for ultra-precision but costlier. 20 ns recommended for low thermal damage. Source: Laser physics reviews in Appl. Surf. Sci. (2019) on metal ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; higher rates speed up process but risk heat buildup on reflective Ti. 50 kHz balances speed and minimal accumulation. Trade-off: >80 kHz may require cooling. Source: Academic lit. e.g., J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. (2017) on pulse overlap effects.

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Ti's ablation threshold (~3 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe contaminant removal without substrate etching. Recommended 1.0 J/cm² for industrial prep. Ti's reflectivity (~40% at 1064 nm) requires higher input. Source: Ablation studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. (2021).

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller for fine detail (e.g., aerospace Ti parts), larger for broad areas. 300 μm recommended for uniform cleaning on Ti without excessive overlap heating. Source: Equipment specs from Laserax and Coherent, aligned with ISO 11553 standards.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination; multi-pass for heavy (e.g., rust/scale on Ti). Recommended 2 passes ensures complete removal with low risk to Ti's integrity. Source: Case studies in industrial laser cleaning (e.g., Proc. SPIE 2022) for metal surfaces.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage; 60% recommended for Ti to avoid gaps or over-etching while managing heat from conductivity. Higher for heavy contamination. Source: Process optimization in J. Clean. Prod. (2019) and manufacturer guidelines (e.g., Adapt Laser).
