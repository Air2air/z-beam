scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for 1064nm cleaning of polyimide; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (2000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1500 mm/s for balance of efficiency and minimal HAZ (industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and J. Cleaner Production, 2020, case studies on surface prep).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for polyimide to balance cleaning efficacy and cost; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but less common industrially. 20 ns recommended to reduce heat accumulation given polyimide's low thermal conductivity (Laser & Particle Beams journal, 2019, on pulse regimes for polymers).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for 1064nm cleaning; higher rates increase speed but risk heat buildup in low-conductivity polyimide. 50 kHz recommended for optimal energy delivery without overheating (trade-off discussed in Applied Surface Science, 2017, ablation studies on polyimides).

energy_density:
  Value:  0.8 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below polyimide damage threshold (~2.5 J/cm² at 1064nm ns pulses) for safe contaminant removal; 0.2-1.0 for light, up to 2.0 for heavy. Recommended 0.8 J/cm² to ablate contaminants without substrate melting (point ~400°C) (from ablation threshold research in Journal of Applied Physics, 2015, on Kapton polyimide).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for precise cleaning; smaller for fine details (100-200 μm), larger for areas (300-500 μm). 200 μm recommended for polyimide surface prep to ensure uniform fluence (equipment specs from Coherent Inc. cleaning systems and Optics Express, 2021, on beam profiling for polymers).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for heavy; 2 passes recommended for complete polyimide cleaning without excessive exposure (industry practice from Adapt Laser Systems case studies and Surface and Coatings Technology, 2019, on multi-pass optimization).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage; 30-50% for speed, 60-80% for thoroughness on irregular polyimide surfaces. 60% recommended to avoid gaps while minimizing overexposure (process studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing, 2022).
