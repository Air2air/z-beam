scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contamination removal; 1000 mm/s recommended for optimal balance on Hickory to avoid overheating. Slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (up to 5000 mm/s) for light. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and process optimization in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for wood cleaning to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ); 50 ns typical for Hickory to enable precise ablation without thermal damage. Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond for ultra-fine work but less common industrially. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on wood substrates.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20 kHz recommended for Hickory to achieve fast cleaning while limiting heat accumulation due to wood's low thermal conductivity. Higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but increase risk of charring; lower for delicate control. Source: Academic literature in Journal of Applied Physics (2021) on pulsed laser cleaning of organics.

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Hickory's damage threshold (~3-5 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe cleaning; 2 J/cm² recommended for contamination removal without ablation of substrate. Conservative for hardwoods to prevent carbonization. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Wood Science and Technology (2017).

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   300 μm recommended for Hickory surface preparation, balancing fine detail and large-area coverage. Smaller (100-200 μm) for precision, larger (500-1000 μm) for broad cleaning. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems for industrial cleaning.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   2 passes typical for complete removal on Hickory; single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (up to 5) for heavy layers to ensure uniformity without excessive heat. Source: Industry case studies in restoration of wooden artifacts (e.g., Studies in Conservation, 2022).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   50% overlap recommended for uniform coverage on Hickory, preventing gaps in cleaning while minimizing overexposure. Range 30-80% based on contamination density. Source: Process optimization studies in Laser Cleaning Journal (2020).
