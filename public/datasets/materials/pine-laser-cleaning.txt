  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred over ps/fs for cost-effective cleaning of pine; shorter pulses minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ) to <50 μm, avoiding charring. repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Mid-range for effective contaminant vaporization on pine; higher rates increase speed but risk heat accumulation due to pine's poor conductivity, leading to discoloration. Trade-off favors moderate for safety. Source: Academic literature in Applied Surface Science (e.g., 2020 study on rep rate effects in wood cleaning, recommending 20-50 kHz for 1064 nm).

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below pine's ablation threshold (~8-12 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe removal of surface contaminants; higher risks carbonization. Conservative for pine's reflectivity (~20-40% at IR) and low melting/pyrolysis point (~250°C). Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (e.g., 2012 paper on wood fluence thresholds, industrial adaptation from SPIE proceedings).
