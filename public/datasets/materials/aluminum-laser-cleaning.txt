scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for light to heavy contamination on aluminum; slower for heavy (e.g., oxide layers), faster for light oils. Recommended 1500 mm/s optimizes removal while minimizing heat buildup due to aluminum's conductivity. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and Journal of Cleaner Production (2020) case studies.

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective industrial cleaning; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces HAZ but increases cost. 50 ns recommended for aluminum to ablate contaminants with minimal thermal diffusion. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) comparing pulse regimes for metals.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed fiber lasers at 1064 nm; higher rates speed up cleaning but risk heat accumulation on reflective aluminum. 50 kHz balances speed and safety. Source: Academic literature in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021) on frequency effects for aluminum surface preparation.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal; aluminum damage threshold ~4-6 J/cm² at 1064 nm ns pulses. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² ensures safe cleaning below ablation threshold of substrate. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Physical Review B (2017) and Optics Express (2022) for IR laser on aluminum.

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for uniform coverage; smaller for fine details, larger for broad areas. 300 μm recommended for aluminum to achieve even fluence distribution without hotspots. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaners, with optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2019).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for heavy; aluminum's reflectivity often requires 2 passes for complete removal without damage. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Journal of Laser Applications (2020) on industrial aluminum cleaning.

overlap_ratio:

  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
