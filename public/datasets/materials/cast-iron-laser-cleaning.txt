  Min:    50 W
  Max:    1000 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial cleaning of cast iron to remove rust or contaminants without substrate damage; starting at 200W balances efficiency and safety. Max limited to avoid melting (cast iron melting point ~1150-1300°C). Source: IPG Photonics laser cleaning systems specs for metal surfaces; academic review in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for 1064 nm cleaning on cast iron; slower for heavy contamination (e.g., thick rust) to ensure ablation, faster for light oils. Recommended 2000 mm/s for optimal throughput with minimal heat input, considering cast iron's high thermal conductivity (~50 W/m·K). Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals; case studies in Journal of Cleaner Production (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime dominant for industrial cleaning of cast iron to achieve plasma-induced ablation with low heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier applications. 50 ns recommended to minimize thermal diffusion in cast iron (reflectivity ~40% at 1064 nm). Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019); avoids fs due to overkill for contamination removal.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  100 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard range for pulsed fiber lasers in metal cleaning; 100 kHz balances cleaning speed and heat accumulation on cast iron, where high rates (>150 kHz) risk subsurface heating despite good conductivity. Trade-off: higher freq accelerates process but increases cumulative fluence. Source: Academic literature in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe removal of contaminants like rust or paint on cast iron; damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² to avoid pitting or melting. 1.5 J/cm² recommended for effective cleaning below ablation threshold of cast iron (~3 J/cm² at 1064 nm). Conservative due to cast iron's variable surface roughness. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017); SPIE proceedings on laser cleaning.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for industrial scanners; smaller for fine details (e.g., crevices in castings), larger for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for uniform coverage on rough cast iron surfaces without excessive overlap heating. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems; process optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2022).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Typically 1-3 passes for light to moderate contamination on cast iron; multi-pass for layered buildup (e.g., old paint). 2 passes recommended for complete removal while minimizing total energy input and HAZ. Single pass viable for thin rust. Source: Industry practice from case studies in International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (2019); manufacturer guidelines.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Standard range for scan line overlap to ensure uniform fluence distribution; 60% recommended for cast iron to achieve even cleaning on irregular surfaces without gaps or over-ablation. Higher (70-80%) for heavy contamination. Source: Process optimization studies in Optics and Laser Technology (2020); based on Gaussian beam profiles in cleaning simulations.
