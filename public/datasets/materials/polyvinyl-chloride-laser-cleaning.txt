Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Europe guidelines; case studies in Journal of Cleaner Production (2020).

  Value:  30 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for industrial cleaning of polymers to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond for ultra-precision but costlier; 30 ns optimal for PVC to enable photothermal ablation without excessive melting. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on polymer thresholds; avoids fs/ps due to equipment availability.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; 20-50 kHz recommended for PVC to achieve fast processing while limiting heat accumulation from low reflectivity (~5-10% at 1064 nm); higher rates increase speed but risk thermal buildup. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2021) on repetition rate effects in polymer laser cleaning.

  Value:  0.3 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of contaminants on polymers; PVC damage threshold ~0.5 J/cm²; recommended 0.3 J/cm² for cleaning without substrate degradation, considering low thermal conductivity (0.19 W/mK). Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) for PVC at 1064 nm.
