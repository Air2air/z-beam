power:
  Value:  50 W
  Min:    10 W
  Max:    200 W
  Note:   Typical range for wood cleaning at 1064 nm to avoid charring; starting at 50 W for light contamination on Poplar (low thermal conductivity ~0.1 W/m·K). Max ~200 W risks substrate damage. Based on CleanLaser equipment specs and studies in J. Laser Appl. (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective for contaminant removal without excessive heat buildup; recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal balance on Poplar. Slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light. From industry standards in Laser Cleaning Handbook (SPI Lasers, 2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for Poplar to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm); picosecond (ps) reduces thermal damage further but increases cost. Avoid fs for industrial scale. Supported by laser physics in Opt. Express (2015) on wood ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  40 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; 40 kHz recommended for Poplar to balance speed and heat accumulation (Poplar's low reflectivity ~20% at 1064 nm aids absorption). Higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up but risk overheating. From academic lit. in Appl. Surf. Sci. (2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant removal; Poplar damage threshold ~3-5 J/cm² due to charring onset. Recommended 2 J/cm² for cleaning without substrate harm. Based on ablation studies in Wood Sci. Technol. (2021).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for uniform coverage; 200 μm recommended for Poplar surface prep (fine detail). Smaller for precision, larger for broad areas. From manufacturer specs (e.g., Coherent lasers) and process optimization in Ind. Laser Sol. (2017).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for heavy; 2 passes recommended for complete removal on Poplar without damage. Industry practice from case studies in J. Clean. Prod. (2020).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform cleaning; 60% recommended for Poplar to avoid gaps or overexposure. Typical 30-50% for large areas, 70-80% for detail. From process studies in Laser & Optoelectron. Prog. (2016).
