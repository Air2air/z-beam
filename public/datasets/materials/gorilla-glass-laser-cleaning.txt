  Min:    20 W
  Max:    200 W
  Note:   Typical range for delicate glass substrates like Gorilla Glass to avoid thermal damage; starting at 50W for light contamination removal. Max safe below 200W to prevent cracking due to low thermal conductivity (~1 W/mK). Source: IPG Photonics laser cleaning guidelines for optics; academic review in Journal of Laser Applications (2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Range for effective contaminant ablation without substrate heating; slower speeds (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (1000-2000 mm/s) for light. Recommended 500 mm/s for Gorilla Glass to balance efficiency and safety. Source: Industry standards from Laser Institute of America; equipment manuals from Coherent Inc.

pulse_width:
  Value:  10 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but less common industrially. 10 ns recommended to limit heat diffusion in Gorilla Glass (high reflectivity ~4% at 1064 nm). Source: Laser physics research in Applied Physics A (2020) on glass ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; lower rates reduce heat accumulation in low-conductivity Gorilla Glass, higher enable faster processing but risk overheating. 20 kHz recommended for balanced throughput. Trade-off: high freq increases cumulative fluence. Source: Academic literature in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal (e.g., oils, fingerprints); Gorilla Glass damage threshold ~2-5 J/cm² at 1064 nm ns pulses due to softening point ~500°C. Recommended 0.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning below threshold. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) on strengthened glass.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (50-200 μm) for fine detail cleaning on Gorilla Glass surfaces; larger (200-500 μm) for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for uniform coverage without pinpoint damage. Source: Equipment specifications from Trumpf and Coherent laser systems for precision optics cleaning.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination; multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers to avoid exceeding damage fluence per pass. Recommended 2 passes for Gorilla Glass to ensure complete removal while minimizing thermal stress. Source: Industry case studies in Surface and Coatings Technology (2021); Corning application notes.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-80% overlap ensures uniform fluence distribution; higher for delicate materials like Gorilla Glass to prevent uneven heating or missed spots. 50% recommended for optimal coverage in industrial cleaning. Source: Process optimization studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2022).
