  Value:  1500 mm/s

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-200 ns) dominant for cost-effective cleaning; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond for ultra-low HAZ but less common industrially. Recommended 20 ns minimizes thermal effects on steel while ablating contaminants. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2018) on ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  80 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; lower rates (20-50 kHz) reduce heat accumulation, higher (100-200 kHz) speed up process but risk overheating steel. Recommended 80 kHz for balanced throughput on metals. Trade-off: High freq increases fluence overlap. Source: Academic literature in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  2.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe cleaning; below 1 J/cm² ineffective for rust, above 5-8 J/cm² risks steel damage (ablation threshold ~4-6 J/cm² at 1064 nm). Recommended 2.5 J/cm² for contamination removal without melting. Steel's reflectivity (~50% at 1064 nm) requires higher values. Source: Ablation studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017).

spot_size:
  Value:  250 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range; smaller (100-300 μm) for precision surface prep, larger (500-1000 μm) for broad area cleaning. Recommended 250 μm for uniform steel cleaning, balancing resolution and coverage. Source: Equipment specs from Adapt Laser Systems and Coherent Inc. manuals.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers like mill scale. Recommended 2 passes for complete removal on steel without excessive heat input. Source: Industry case studies in Surface and Coatings Technology (2021) and practical guidelines from Laser Cleaning Europe.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage; 30-50% for speed, 70-80% for thorough cleaning. Recommended 60% prevents gaps on irregular steel surfaces while minimizing over-processing. Source: Process optimization in International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (2022).
