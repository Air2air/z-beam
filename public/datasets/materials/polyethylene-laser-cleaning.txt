  Note:   Typical range for industrial fiber lasers cleaning polymers like polyethylene to avoid melting (melting point ~110-130°C); starting at 150W for light contamination. Max safe ~500W to prevent substrate damage. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and studies on polymer ablation (e.g., Opt. Lasers Eng. 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for 1064 nm cleaning of polyethylene; slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination like oils, faster (500-2000 mm/s) for light dust. Recommended 500 mm/s balances efficiency and heat control given PE's low thermal conductivity (~0.3 W/mK). Source: Industry manuals from Laserax and Coherent, process optimization in J. Clean. Prod. 2020.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) preferred for industrial cleaning of polyethylene to ablate contaminants without excessive heat; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but costlier. 20 ns recommended to limit thermal diffusion in low-conductivity PE. Source: Laser physics reviews in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on polymer ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed fiber lasers in cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for polyethylene to achieve fast coverage while minimizing heat accumulation (trade-off: higher rates speed up process but risk melting). Source: Academic literature on ns-laser cleaning of plastics (e.g., Lasers Med. Sci. 2017).

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on polyethylene; damage threshold ~1.2-1.5 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to PE's reflectivity (~90% at IR) and low melting point. 0.5 J/cm² recommended for safe cleaning of organics. Source: Ablation threshold studies in J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. (2015) and Opt. Express (2021).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for 1064 nm cleaning; 50-200 μm for fine surface prep on polyethylene, 200-500 μm for large areas. 200 μm recommended for uniform energy distribution without hotspots on reflective PE. Source: Equipment specs from Trumpf and SPI Lasers, application notes for polymer processing.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   1-3 passes for light contamination removal, up to 5 for heavy layers; multi-pass preferred over single high-energy pass to avoid PE damage. 2 passes recommended for industrial prep. Source: Case studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. (2019) and industry practices from Adapt Laser Systems.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-80% overlap ensures uniform coverage in scan patterns; 50% recommended for polyethylene to prevent gaps or overexposure, optimizing for even cleaning on irregular surfaces. Source: Process optimization in Int. J. Adv. Manuf. Technol. (2022) and laser scanning guidelines from scanning head manufacturers like Scanlab.
