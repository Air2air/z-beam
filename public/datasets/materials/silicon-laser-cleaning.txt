  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-200 ns) common for industrial 1064 nm cleaning of Silicon; 20 ns recommended for minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm) by confining energy to surface ablation. Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) better for ultra-precision but costlier; ns sufficient for contamination removal. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019); avoids reflectivity issues at 1064 nm (~35% for Silicon).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; 50 kHz recommended for Silicon to achieve fast coverage while limiting heat accumulation (trade-off: higher rates speed up process but risk thermal stress). Lower rates for heavy contamination. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2021); based on Silicon's moderate IR absorption.

  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe cleaning below Silicon's ablation threshold (~1-3 J/cm² at 1064 nm); 0.5 J/cm² recommended to remove contaminants without substrate damage. Damage threshold ~2 J/cm² for ns pulses. Conservative for Silicon's reflectivity. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Physical Review B (2017); Journal of Laser Applications (2022).

Smaller (50-100 μm) for detailed work; larger (300-500 μm) for broad contamination. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent ArmoClean systems; industry optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2020).

  Note:   1-5 passes typical for complete removal; 2 passes recommended for Silicon to ensure thorough cleaning of industrial contaminants without overexposure. Single pass for light layers; multi-pass for heavy or uneven. Source: Industry practice in semiconductor processing case studies, e.g., SPIE Proceedings (2019).

Higher (70-80%) for fine uniformity; lower (30-50%) for speed on large areas. Source: Process optimization studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021).
