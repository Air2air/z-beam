scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (2000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1500 mm/s for uniform terracotta surface preparation. Source: Industry manuals from IPG Photonics and Laserax equipment guidelines.

pulse_width:
  Value:  15 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for terracotta to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ) while ablating contaminants; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precision but less common industrially. 15 ns optimal for low thermal conductivity materials. Source: Laser ablation research (e.g., Anisimov et al., 2015, Appl. Phys. A).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for 1064 nm cleaning to achieve good overlap without excessive heat buildup; higher rates speed up process but risk accumulation on porous terracotta. 20 kHz balances speed and safety. Source: Academic literature on pulsed laser cleaning (e.g., Pouli et al., 2010, Spectrochim. Acta B).

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant ablation without substrate damage; terracotta damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² due to low melting point (~1000-1200°C). Recommended 1 J/cm² for safe industrial cleaning. Source: Ablation threshold studies on ceramics (e.g., Marla et al., 2012, J. Appl. Phys.).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light soiling, multi-pass for heavy contamination; 2 passes typical for complete terracotta cleaning without overexposure. Source: Case studies in cultural heritage laser cleaning (e.g., Radvan et al., 2007, Lasers in the Conservation of Artworks).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
