pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred over picosecond/femtosecond for cost-effective industrial cleaning; 20 ns minimizes heat-affected zone (HAZ) on sapphire due to reduced thermal diffusion; shorter pulses (<10 ns) for ultra-fine control but higher cost (laser physics research in Appl. Phys. A 2015 on Al2O3 ablation; Coherent laser parameters).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Common for pulsed cleaning to achieve high throughput; 50 kHz balances speed and heat management for sapphire, where high rates risk accumulation but leverage its thermal conductivity; lower rates for heavy contamination (academic literature: J. Phys. D 2020 on pulse overlap in ceramics; manufacturer data from Trumpf).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below sapphire's damage threshold (~4-6 J/cm² at 1064 nm, 20 ns pulses) for safe contaminant ablation; 1.5 J/cm² effective for industrial removal without substrate etching, given sapphire's high reflectivity (~92% transmission, low absorption); conservative for optics-grade sapphire (ablation threshold studies: Opt. Express 2017; SPIE Proc. 2019).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm

  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for precision vs. area coverage; 200 μm recommended for sapphire surface preparation, allowing fine control on flat/polished surfaces while covering larger areas efficiently; smaller for detailed work (equipment specs from Adapt Laser Systems; industry standards for optic cleaning).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for stubborn residues; 2 passes typical for complete removal on sapphire without risking cumulative damage, based on its hardness and low absorption (industry practice in watchmaking/optics case studies; Laser Cleaning Journal 2021).

  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage without gaps or overexposure; 60% recommended for sapphire to achieve even cleaning while minimizing total energy input and HAZ, adjustable for contamination density (process optimization studies: Surf.
