scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contamination removal; slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy rust/oils, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light contaminants. Recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal balance on Mg to minimize heat accumulation. Source: Industry standards from Laserax and Adapt Laser manuals for metal surface prep.

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for Mg to ablate contaminants with minimal HAZ; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces thermal effects but increases cost. 50 ns recommended for low reflectivity materials like Mg. Source: Laser physics research (Opt. Lasers Eng. 2020, Vol. 132, on pulse duration effects in metal cleaning).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed fiber lasers in cleaning; higher rates speed up process but risk heat buildup on thermally conductive Mg. 50 kHz balances speed and safety. Trade-off: >80 kHz may cause melting. Source: Academic literature (Appl. Surf. Sci. 2019, Vol. 475, on rep rate optimization for light metals).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Mg damage threshold (~5 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe cleaning; 0.5-2 J/cm² for light contaminants, up to 4 for heavy. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² avoids ablation of substrate. Source: Ablation threshold studies (J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. 2017, Vol. 50, on IR laser interaction with Mg).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (100-200 μm) for fine detail on Mg surfaces, larger (300-500 μm) for broad area prep. 200 μm recommended for uniform cleaning without excessive heat concentration. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaning systems for metals.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers on Mg to prevent overheating per pass. Recommended 2 passes for industrial removal. Source: Industry case studies (e.g., SPIE Proc. 2021, Vol. 11660, on multi-pass cleaning of alloys).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage without gaps or overexposure; 30-50% for large areas, 60-80% for detailed Mg cleaning. 60% recommended to balance efficiency and avoid thermal damage. Source: Process optimization studies (Opt. Laser Technol. 2022, Vol. 145, on scan overlap in laser cleaning).
