power:
  Value:  500 W
  Min:    100 W
  Max:    2000 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial 1064 nm laser cleaning of concrete surfaces (e.g., graffiti or rust removal); starting at 500 W balances efficiency and safety to avoid thermal cracking. Max safe below substrate damage threshold (~1000-1500 W depending on fluence). Source: CleanLaser and IPG Photonics equipment specs; academic review in J. Laser Appl. (2018) on stone/concrete cleaning.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal on concrete; slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy soiling like rust, faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light contaminants. Recommended 1000 mm/s for optimal balance of cleaning rate and uniformity, considering concrete's low thermal conductivity. Source: Industry standards from Laser Clean Tech manuals; case study in Opt. Lasers Eng. (2020).

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (10-150 ns) typical for concrete to enable ablation with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) reduces HAZ further but increases cost. Recommended 50 ns for efficient contaminant removal without cracking porous concrete. Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Surf. Sci. (2019) on pulsed laser ablation of cementitious materials.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard range for 1064 nm cleaning; higher rates (50-100 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation in low-conductivity concrete. Recommended 50 kHz for balanced throughput and thermal management. Source: Academic literature in J. Clean. Prod. (2021) on industrial laser parameters for building materials.

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant ablation (e.g., paints or oxides); concrete damage threshold ~8-12 J/cm² to prevent spalling. Recommended 2 J/cm² for effective cleaning below threshold, accounting for variable reflectivity at 1064 nm. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. (2017) and Opt. Express (2022) on concrete laser interactions.

spot_size:
  Value:  1000 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for cleaning; smaller (100-500 μm) for fine details like cracks, larger (1-5 mm) for broad surface prep. Recommended 1000 μm (1 mm) for uniform coverage on rough concrete surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf lasers; process optimization in Lasers Manuf. Mater. Process. (2020).

pass_count:
  Value:  3 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (3-5) for heavy layers to ensure complete removal without excessive energy input. Recommended 3 passes for typical industrial concrete cleaning to achieve uniform results. Source: Industry case studies in Constr. Build. Mater. (2019) on laser surface preparation.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Standard overlap for uniform scan coverage; 30-50% for faster large-area cleaning, 50-80% for detailed work to avoid gaps on uneven concrete. Recommended 50% for balanced efficiency and homogeneity. Source: Process optimization studies in J. Laser Appl. (2021) and manufacturer guidelines from Adapt Laser Systems.
