scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal; slower (200-500 mm/s) for heavy soiling, faster (1000-2000 mm/s) for light dirt. Recommended 1000 mm/s optimizes cleaning rate while minimizing heat input on Beech. Source: Industry guidelines from laser cleaning systems like those from IPG Photonics manuals.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for 1064 nm cleaning of organics like wood; avoids excessive heat-affected zone compared to longer pulses, while picosecond/femtosecond are overkill for industrial apps. 20 ns recommended for Beech to limit thermal diffusion. Source: Laser physics reviews on ablation of polymers/wood (e.g., Appl. Phys. A 2018, vol. 124, art. 456).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for pulsed cleaning; lower rates (10-30 kHz) reduce heat accumulation in low-conductivity Beech, higher (50-100 kHz) speed up process but risk damage. 30 kHz balances speed and safety. Source: Academic literature on pulse train effects in wood laser processing (e.g., Opt. Lasers Eng. 2020, vol. 132, 106089).

  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant ablation without substrate harm; Beech damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to absorption and low reflectivity (~10-20%). 1.5 J/cm² recommended for safe cleaning.
