  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for 1064 nm cleaning of wood to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm); picosecond (ps) reduces HAZ further but increases cost. Avoid fs for industrial scale. Recommended 20 ns for redwood's reflectivity (~20-30% at 1064 nm). Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on pulsed laser ablation of lignocellulosic materials.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for cleaning applications; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation on low-conductivity redwood, leading to carbonization. Recommended 30 kHz for balance. Source: Academic literature in Lasers Med. Sci. (2017) on pulse repetition effects in wood laser processing.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal; redwood damage threshold ~3-5 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate absorption. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning below threshold. Conservative estimate for variability in wood moisture. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Wood Sci. Technol. (2021) and J. Appl. Phys. (2015).
