scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contaminant removal; slower for heavy soiling (100-500 mm/s), faster for light (1000-2000 mm/s). Recommended 500 mm/s balances efficiency and heat control on redwood. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and Opt. Lasers Eng. (2020) case studies on wood preparation.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for 1064 nm cleaning of wood to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm); picosecond (ps) reduces HAZ further but increases cost. Avoid fs for industrial scale. Recommended 20 ns for redwood's reflectivity (~20-30% at 1064 nm). Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on pulsed laser ablation of lignocellulosic materials.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for cleaning applications; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation on low-conductivity redwood, leading to carbonization. Recommended 30 kHz for balance. Source: Academic literature in Lasers Med. Sci. (2017) on pulse repetition effects in wood laser processing.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal; redwood damage threshold ~3-5 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate absorption. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning below threshold. Conservative estimate for variability in wood moisture. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Wood Sci. Technol. (2021) and J. Appl. Phys. (2015).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for cleaning; smaller for fine details (100-200 μm), larger for broad areas (300-500 μm). Recommended 200 μm for uniform redwood surface preparation without excessive overlap heating. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaners for industrial wood applications.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy; recommended 2 passes for complete removal on redwood while minimizing exposure time and damage. Source: Industry practice in restoration case studies, e.g., J. Cult. Herit. (2019) on laser cleaning of wooden artifacts.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  50 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Typical for uniform coverage; 30-50% for speed, 50-80% for thoroughness. Recommended 50% to ensure even cleaning on redwood's textured surface without gaps or over-ablation. Source: Process optimization studies in Proc. SPIE (2022) on scan strategies for laser surface treatment.
