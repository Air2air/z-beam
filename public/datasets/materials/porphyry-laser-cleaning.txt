scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective for contamination removal on hard stones; 500 mm/s recommended for optimal balance of cleaning efficiency and heat dissipation; slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (1000-2000 mm/s) for light; based on industry standards from Adapt Laser Systems manuals.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning of porphyry; 20 ns minimizes heat-affected zone (HAZ) due to porphyry's high melting point (~1300°C) and reflectivity; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but less common industrially (Laser Phys. 2015 study on stone ablation).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for 1064 nm cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for porphyry to achieve fast processing without excessive heat accumulation; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up but risk thermal buildup on low-conductivity stones (Opt. Lasers Eng. 2019).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant ablation on porphyry; 1.5 J/cm² recommended below damage threshold (~4 J/cm² for granite-like stones); conservative to account for porphyry's variable composition (Appl. Surf. Sci. 2017 ablation threshold study).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for industrial cleaning; 200 μm recommended for porphyry surface preparation, balancing fine detail and large-area coverage; smaller for precision, larger for broad contamination (Laserax equipment specifications).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Typically 1-2 passes for light industrial contamination on durable stones like porphyry; multi-pass for heavy buildup to ensure complete removal without exceeding safe fluence; from case studies in stone restoration (J. Laser Appl. 2020).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage on irregular porphyry surfaces; 70% recommended for even cleaning without gaps or over-ablation; typical 50-80% in process optimization (Opt. Express 2016 study on scan strategies).
