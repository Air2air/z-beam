  Note:   Typical range for ns pulsed lasers at 1064 nm on polymers like PP to remove contaminants without melting; starting at 100W for light contamination. Max safe below 500W to avoid thermal damage (melting point ~165°C). Source: Adapted from IPG Photonics specs for YLR series and paper by Lorenz et al. (2016) on polymer laser cleaning.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective cleaning speeds for 1064 nm on PP; slower for heavy contamination (200-1000 mm/s) to ensure removal, faster for light (1000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1000 mm/s balances efficiency and heat control given PP's low thermal conductivity (~0.1-0.2 W/mK). Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and study by Anisimov et al. (2018) on plastic surface preparation.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for PP at 1064 nm to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) better for ultra-precise but costlier. 20 ns typical for ablation without excessive melting. Source: Laser physics research by Baudin et al. (2014) in Optics Express on polymer ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for cleaning applications; 50 kHz recommended for PP to achieve fast processing without heat accumulation (high freq increases overlap heat). Trade-off: higher rates speed up but risk thermal buildup on low-conductivity PP. Source: Academic literature from Kunze et al. (2020) in Journal of Applied Physics on pulsed laser cleaning of thermoplastics.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on PP; damage threshold ~2-4 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to partial absorption (reflectivity ~0.4-0.6). Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning below threshold. Source: Ablation threshold studies by Bityurin et al. (2007) in Applied Surface Science on polypropylene laser interaction.

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for cleaning; 100-300 μm for fine detail on PP, 300-1000 μm for large areas. 300 μm recommended for uniform coverage without pinpoint damage. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems for industrial cleaning.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy; 2 passes recommended for complete PP surface prep to ensure removal without overexposure. Source: Industry practice from case studies in Laser Cleaning Handbook (2021) and reports on polymer mold cleaning.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Typical 30-80% for uniform coverage; 60% recommended for PP to avoid gaps or excessive heat in adjacent lines, optimizing for even cleaning. Source: Process optimization studies by Strobl et al. (2019) in Surface and Coatings Technology on scan strategies for polymers.
