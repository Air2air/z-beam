  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range balances cleaning efficiency and heat input; recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal CFRP surface prep. Slower (500 mm/s) for heavy contamination, faster (3000 mm/s) for light. Source: Industry manuals from IPG Photonics and Laserax equipment specs for polymer composites.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for CFRP to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond for ultra-precise but costlier. 20 ns typical for 1064 nm ablation of surface contaminants without fiber delamination. Source: Laser physics studies in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2020).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for industrial cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for CFRP to achieve fast processing while limiting heat accumulation in low-conductivity matrix. Higher rates increase speed but risk overheating. Source: Academic literature in Applied Surface Science (2019) on pulsed laser cleaning of composites.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal (e.g., oils, paints); CFRP damage threshold ~2-5 J/cm² depending on matrix. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for safe cleaning, below ablation threshold for carbon fibers (~10 J/cm²). Conservative to account for reflectivity (~30-50% at 1064 nm). Source: Ablation threshold studies in Composites Part B (2017).
