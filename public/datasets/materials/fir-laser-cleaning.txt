scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Range for effective contaminant removal without substrate ablation; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s) vs light (2000-5000 mm/s). Recommended 1500 mm/s balances speed and control for Fir surface preparation. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Association guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems); case studies on wood restoration.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for Fir to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precision but costlier. 20 ns optimal for 1064 nm ablation of organics without excessive plasma formation. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (e.g., Anisimov et al., 2010) on wood ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for cleaning applications; 30 kHz recommended for Fir to achieve fast coverage while limiting heat accumulation (trade-off: higher rates speed up process but risk thermal damage on low-conductivity wood). Source: Academic literature in Journal of Applied Physics (e.g., studies on pulse overlap in laser cleaning, Semaltianos, 2013).

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe cleaning of contaminants; Fir damage threshold ~6-10 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate reflectivity (~40% for wood). Recommended 2 J/cm² for removal without charring. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Optics Express (e.g., research on softwood laser interaction, Kononenko et al., 2006); conservative for industrial use.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for cleaning; smaller (100-200 μm) for fine detail on Fir grain, larger (300-500 μm) for broad areas. 200 μm recommended for uniform surface preparation. Source: Equipment specifications from Trumpf and CleanLASER systems; process optimization in industrial applications.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers; 2 passes recommended for complete Fir cleaning without overexposure. Source: Industry practice from case studies in wood heritage restoration (e.g., European Laser Cleaning projects) and manufacturer guidelines.

overlap_ratio:
