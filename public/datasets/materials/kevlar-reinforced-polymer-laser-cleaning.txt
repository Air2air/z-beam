power:
  Value:  50 W
  Min:    10 W
  Max:    200 W
  Note:   Typical range for pulsed fiber lasers in polymer composite cleaning to avoid thermal damage; start at 50 W for initial tests on Kevlar-reinforced polymer due to its low thermal conductivity (~0.3 W/m·K). Max safe ~150-200 W to prevent matrix degradation. Source: IPG Photonics specs for YLR series; studies in J. Laser Appl. (2018) on composite surface prep.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance cleaning efficiency and heat input; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (2000-3000 mm/s). Recommended 1000 mm/s for uniform removal on Kevlar without fiber disruption. Source: Industry standards from Laserax manuals; Opt. Lasers Eng. (2020) case studies on polymer ablation.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning with minimal HAZ; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces thermal effects but increases cost. 20 ns optimal for Kevlar to limit heat diffusion in low-conductivity matrix. Avoid >100 ns to prevent melting (~450°C decomposition). Source: Laser physics in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on aramid composites.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Lower rates reduce heat accumulation in heat-sensitive Kevlar; high rates (>50 kHz) speed cleaning but risk substrate damage. 30 kHz balances speed and safety for contamination removal. Trade-off: higher freq increases throughput but elevates surface temp. Source: Academic lit. in J. Clean. Prod. (2021) on pulsed laser parameters for polymers.

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below damage threshold (~2-4 J/cm² for Kevlar epoxy composites at 1064 nm) for safe cleaning; 0.2-1 J/cm² for light contaminants, up to 3 J/cm² for heavy. Recommended 1 J/cm² to ablate surface without fiber/matrix harm. Source: Ablation studies in Compos. Part B (2017); damage thresholds from SPIE Proc. (2022).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots for precision on textured Kevlar surfaces, larger for broad areas; 100-300 μm for fine cleaning, 300-500 μm for large-scale prep. 200 μm recommended to match Kevlar weave (~100-200 μm fibers) and ensure even fluence. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent Inc.; process opt. in Lasers Manuf. Mater. Process. (2020).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for stubborn residues; 2 passes typical for Kevlar to achieve complete removal without cumulative heat damage. Multi-pass allows lower fluence per pass. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Adapt Laser Systems; J. Mater. Process. Technol. (2019).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage without gaps or over-ablation; 30-50% for fast scans, 60-80% for thorough cleaning on irregular Kevlar surfaces. 60% recommended for even contaminant removal while minimizing total energy input. Source: Process optimization in Opt. Laser Technol. (2021); standards from scanning system manuals like Scanlab.
