  Value:  1000 mm/s

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning with minimal HAZ; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces thermal effects but increases cost. 20 ns optimal for Kevlar to limit heat diffusion in low-conductivity matrix. Avoid >100 ns to prevent melting (~450°C decomposition). Source: Laser physics in Appl. Phys. A (2019) on aramid composites.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Lower rates reduce heat accumulation in heat-sensitive Kevlar; high rates (>50 kHz) speed cleaning but risk substrate damage. 30 kHz balances speed and safety for contamination removal. Trade-off: higher freq increases throughput but elevates surface temp. Source: Academic lit. in J. Clean. Prod. (2021) on pulsed laser parameters for polymers.

  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below damage threshold (~2-4 J/cm² for Kevlar epoxy composites at 1064 nm) for safe cleaning; 0.2-1 J/cm² for light contaminants, up to 3 J/cm² for heavy. Recommended 1 J/cm² to ablate surface without fiber/matrix harm. Source: Ablation studies in Compos. Part B (2017); damage thresholds from SPIE Proc. (2022).

Multi-pass allows lower fluence per pass. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Adapt Laser Systems; J. Mater. Process. Technol. (2019).

Source: Process optimization in Opt. Laser Technol. (2021); standards from scanning system manuals like Scanlab.
