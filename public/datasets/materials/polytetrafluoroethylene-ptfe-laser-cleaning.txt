scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds balance energy delivery and heat dissipation; 500 mm/s for heavy contamination (e.g., oils), up to 3000 mm/s for light dust; recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal PTFE cleaning without residue. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and Journal of Cleaner Production (2020) case studies on industrial polymer prep.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for cost-effective cleaning of PTFE; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm) but increases cost; 20 ns minimizes thermal damage due to PTFE's poor conductivity. Avoid fs for industrial scale. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on polymer ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for 1064 nm cleaning to achieve fluence without excessive heat buildup; 30 kHz recommended for PTFE to balance speed and avoid accumulation (high freq risks melting); lower for heavy soiling. Trade-off: higher freq speeds process but increases HAZ. Source: Academic literature in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021).

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal without exceeding PTFE ablation threshold (~1.5-2 J/cm² at 1064 nm); recommended 1.0 J/cm² for safe cleaning of surfaces like rust or oils, considering PTFE's high reflectivity (~90% at 1064 nm). Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) and Optics Express (2022) on fluoropolymers.
