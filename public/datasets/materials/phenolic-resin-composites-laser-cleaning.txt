Max safe ~300W to prevent charring (melting point ~200-300°C). Source: IPG Photonics specs and studies in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018) on polymer composites.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for 1064 nm cleaning of contaminants; slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy soiling, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light; 1500 mm/s optimal for uniform removal without substrate heating on low-reflectivity phenolics (~10-20% at 1064 nm). Source: Industry standards from Laserax manuals and J. Cleaner Production (2020) case studies.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred over ps/fs for cost-effective cleaning with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm); 20 ns balances ablation efficiency and reduced thermal diffusion in phenolics. Ps (10-100 ps) for ultra-precise work but higher cost. Source: Laser physics in Applied Surface Science (2019) on polymer ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for industrial cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for phenolics to achieve fast processing while limiting heat accumulation (high freq >50 kHz risks cumulative heating due to low conductivity). Trade-off: higher rates speed up cleaning but increase HAZ. Source: Academic literature in Journal of Laser Applications (2021).

energy_density:
  Value:  1 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal; damage threshold for phenolics ~1.5-2.5 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate absorption; 1.0 J/cm² safe for cleaning without ablation of substrate. Conservative for thermal sensitivity. Source: Ablation studies in Composites Part B (2017) and Optics Express (2022).

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for precision vs. area coverage; 300 μm recommended for phenolic composites in surface prep to focus energy and minimize overshoot on irregular surfaces. Smaller for fine details, larger for broad areas. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent Inc. and process optimization in Industrial Laser Solutions (2020).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-3) for heavy; 2 passes typical for complete removal on phenolics without excessive heat buildup. Source: Industry practice in aerospace case studies (e.g., AIAA Journal, 2019) and manufacturer guidelines.

  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage; 50% recommended for phenolic composites to avoid gaps or overexposure, balancing efficiency and even fluence distribution. Higher (60-80%) for heavy contamination. Source: Process optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2021).
