  Note:   Typical range for industrial laser cleaning of hard stones like quartzite to remove contaminants without substrate damage; starting at 200W balances efficiency and safety. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs for stone cleaning; academic review in 'Laser Cleaning of Stone Sculptures' (Optics & Lasers in Engineering, 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for 1064 nm cleaning on quartzite; slower for heavy contamination (e.g., rust/oil) to ensure removal, faster for light dust. Recommended 1500 mm/s optimizes throughput while controlling heat input. Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals; case studies in Journal of Cultural Heritage (2020) on stone surface preparation.

pulse_width:
  Value:  30 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for quartzite cleaning to ablate contaminants via photothermal effects while minimizing heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) reduce HAZ further but increase cost. Avoid fs for industrial scale. Recommended 30 ns for balance. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on SiO2-based materials; ablation studies showing ns optimal for reflectivity >80% at 1064 nm.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to achieve uniform energy delivery; higher rates speed up process but risk heat accumulation on low-conductivity quartzite (melting point ~1700°C). Recommended 50 kHz avoids cumulative heating. Trade-off: >50 kHz faster but monitor for microcracks. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2017) on repetition rate effects in stone laser cleaning.

energy_density:
  Value:  2 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal (e.g., oxides, organics) below quartzite damage threshold (~10-15 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to high reflectivity ~90%). Recommended 2 J/cm² ensures safe cleaning without ablation of substrate. Conservative for heavy contamination. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2015) on quartz; industrial data from Adapt Laser Systems.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for industrial applications; smaller for fine detail cleaning (e.g., engravings on quartzite), larger for broad surface prep. Recommended 200 μm provides good resolution and fluence control on reflective surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaners; optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2021).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination; multi-pass for heavy layers to build fluence gradually and prevent thermal stress/cracking in brittle quartzite. Recommended 2 passes for most industrial removal tasks. Source: Industry practice in laser cleaning case studies (e.g., restoration of natural stone, Heritage Science, 2019); manuals from Lasertec.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage without gaps or overexposure; 60% recommended for quartzite to achieve even cleaning on irregular surfaces while minimizing total energy input and HAZ. Higher for heavy contamination. Source: Process optimization studies in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2020); typical ranges from scanning software in IPG systems.
