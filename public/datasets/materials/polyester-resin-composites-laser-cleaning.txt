  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s

  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal; slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contaminants like oils, faster (2000-5000 mm/s) for light dust. Recommended 2000 mm/s for optimal cleaning without overheating polyester's low thermal conductivity (~0.2 W/m·K). Source: IPG Photonics equipment manuals and Journal of Cleaner Production (2020) case studies on polymer composites.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) preferred over ps/fs for cost-effective 1064 nm cleaning of composites; 20 ns minimizes heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm) by reducing thermal diffusion in polyester. Ps/fs (e.g., <1 ps) for ultra-precise but less common industrially. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on polymer ablation thresholds.

  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for polyester composites to achieve fast coverage while limiting heat accumulation (trade-off: higher rates speed up process but risk melting if >50 kHz without high speed). Source: Academic literature in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021) on fiber-reinforced polymers.

  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of contaminants; damage threshold for polyester composites ~3-5 J/cm² due to reflectivity (~5-10% at 1064 nm) and low thermal conductivity.
