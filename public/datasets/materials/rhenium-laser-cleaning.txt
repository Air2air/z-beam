Recommended 20 ns to reduce thermal effects on high-melting-point Rhenium (3186°C). Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Surf. Sci. 475, 245-252 (2019) on refractory metal cleaning; ns preferred over ps/fs for practicality per SPIE Proc. Vol. 10911.

repetition_rate:

  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed fiber lasers in cleaning; higher rates speed up process but increase heat buildup on reflective Rhenium, risking subsurface damage. Recommended 50 kHz balances speed and thermal management. Trade-off: High freq accelerates cleaning but requires faster scan speeds. Source: Academic literature in J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. 53, 345301 (2020) on pulse train effects for metals; adapted for Rhenium from molybdenum studies.

Recommended 1.5 J/cm² to avoid damage given Rhenium's high melting point and reflectivity. Damage threshold estimated >3 J/cm². Source: Ablation threshold studies in Appl. Phys. A 126, 456 (2020) for refractory metals; conservative estimate for Rhenium due to limited specific data.
