  Min:    50 W
  Max:    300 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial cleaning of refractory metals like Rhenium to balance efficiency and avoid thermal damage; recommended starting at 150 W for 1064 nm Nd:YAG lasers. Rhenium's high reflectivity (~60% at 1064 nm) requires higher power than less reflective metals. Source: Extrapolated from studies on laser cleaning of tungsten (similar refractory properties) in J. Laser Appl. 32, 022064 (2020); manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics YLR series.

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Range for effective contaminant removal without substrate ablation; slower for heavy contamination (e.g., oxides), faster for light surface prep. Recommended 1500 mm/s for optimal balance on Rhenium to minimize heat accumulation given its high thermal conductivity (48 W/m·K). Source: Industry standards from Laser CleanTech manuals and process optimization in Opt. Lasers Eng. 128, 106010 (2020); conservative for Rhenium's properties.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime typical for industrial cleaning to ablate contaminants while sparing Rhenium substrate; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but less common industrially due to cost. Recommended 20 ns to reduce thermal effects on high-melting-point Rhenium (3186°C). Source: Laser physics research in Appl. Surf. Sci. 475, 245-252 (2019) on refractory metal cleaning; ns preferred over ps/fs for practicality per SPIE Proc. Vol. 10911.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed fiber lasers in cleaning; higher rates speed up process but increase heat buildup on reflective Rhenium, risking subsurface damage. Recommended 50 kHz balances speed and thermal management. Trade-off: High freq accelerates cleaning but requires faster scan speeds. Source: Academic literature in J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. 53, 345301 (2020) on pulse train effects for metals; adapted for Rhenium from molybdenum studies.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range below Rhenium's ablation threshold (~4-5 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe contaminant removal; lower for light cleaning, higher for heavy. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² to avoid damage given Rhenium's high melting point and reflectivity. Damage threshold estimated >3 J/cm². Source: Ablation threshold studies in Appl. Phys. A 126, 456 (2020) for refractory metals; conservative estimate for Rhenium due to limited specific data.

spot_size:
  Value:  300 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for precision vs. area coverage; smaller for fine surface prep on Rhenium components, larger for broad industrial cleaning. Recommended 300 μm for uniform energy distribution on reflective surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent Armonia series and Trumpf lasers; based on optimization in Surf. Coat. Technol. 384, 125347 (2020) for metal cleaning applications.

  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for heavy or uneven layers; recommended 2 passes for complete Rhenium cleaning to ensure uniformity without excessive heat input. Source: Industry practice from case studies in aerospace superalloy cleaning (Rhenium-containing) per J. Manuf. Processes 52, 1-12 (2020); multi-pass reduces risk of incomplete removal.

overlap_ratio:

Laser Technol. 131, 106446 (2020) on scan strategies for metals; typical 50-80% range adapted for refractory materials like Rhenium.
