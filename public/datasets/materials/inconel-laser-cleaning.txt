power:
  Value:  100 W
  Min:    20 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for industrial pulsed fiber lasers cleaning metals like Inconel; starting at 100 W balances efficiency and safety to avoid melting (Inconel melting point ~1300-1400°C); max safe ~300-500 W with proper scanning to prevent substrate damage. Source: IPG Photonics YLR series specs and study in Optics and Lasers in Engineering (2018) on Ni-based alloys.

scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective cleaning at 1000-3000 mm/s for light contamination (e.g., oils), slower (500-1500 mm/s) for heavy oxides; 2000 mm/s recommended for optimal balance on Inconel to minimize heat input given its high thermal conductivity (~15 W/m·K). Source: Industry standards from Laserax equipment manuals and Journal of Cleaner Production (2020) case study on superalloys.

pulse_width:
  Value:  50 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (10-200 ns) standard for 1064 nm cleaning of reflective metals like Inconel to enable ablation without excessive plasma shielding; 50 ns recommended for minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ <10 μm), as picosecond/femtosecond better for ultra-precision but costlier. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2019) on pulse duration effects for Ni alloys.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  100 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   20-100 kHz typical for controlled energy delivery in cleaning; 100 kHz recommended for Inconel to achieve fast processing while limiting heat accumulation (trade-off: higher rates speed up cleaning but risk thermal buildup on low-reflectivity contaminants). Source: Academic literature in Journal of Laser Applications (2021) reviewing rep rates for metal surface preparation.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal without damaging Inconel (ablation threshold ~2-4 J/cm² due to high reflectivity ~60% at 1064 nm); 1.5 J/cm² safe for cleaning, below damage threshold of ~5 J/cm². Source: Ablation threshold studies in Surface and Coatings Technology (2017) on Inconel 718.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   50-300 μm for fine detail cleaning, up to 500 μm for large areas; 200 μm recommended for Inconel to ensure uniform fluence distribution and avoid hotspots on irregular surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser cleaning systems.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, 2-3 for moderate/heavy; 2 passes recommended for complete removal on Inconel without excessive wear, allowing residue assessment between passes. Source: Industry practice in Aerospace Manufacturing and case studies from Laser Cleaning International (2022).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-50% for speed on large areas, 60-80% for uniform coverage; 60% recommended to ensure no gaps in cleaning Inconel surfaces while optimizing process time. Source: Process optimization studies in Optics and Laser Technology (2020) on scan strategies for alloys.
