scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Slower speeds (100-500 mm/s) for heavy contamination like rust or coatings on basalt, faster (1000-2000 mm/s) for light dirt; optimal balances cleaning efficiency and heat accumulation. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Europe guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems); case studies in J. Pou et al. (Appl. Surf. Sci., 2011) for stone ablation.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) preferred for basalt to achieve ablation with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond reduces HAZ further but increases cost. Recommended ns for industrial scalability and basalt's properties. Source: Laser physics research in A. Nevosad et al. (J. Appl. Phys., 2008) on pulsed laser cleaning of geological materials; avoids thermal cracking in low-conductivity basalt.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 20-80 kHz for effective contaminant removal; higher rates speed up cleaning but risk heat buildup on basalt's poor thermal dissipation. Trade-off favors moderate rates for uniform results. Source: Academic literature including R. N. Raman et al. (Opt. Express, 2010) on repetition rate effects in stone cleaning; manufacturer data from Trumpf lasers.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of contaminants (e.g., oxides) on basalt; damage threshold ~4-5 J/cm² to prevent substrate cracking or melting. Conservative for basalt's high absorption at 1064 nm. Source: Ablation threshold studies in M. Autric et al. (Proc. SPIE, 1998) on rock materials; confirmed in industrial reports for 1064 nm cleaning.
