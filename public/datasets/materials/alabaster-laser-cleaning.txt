pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for alabaster to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond for ultra-precision but less common industrially. 20 ns optimal for 1064 nm on low-conductivity materials like gypsum. Source: Laser physics research in Opt. Lasers Eng. (2015) on stone ablation.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Lower rates reduce heat accumulation in thermally sensitive alabaster; higher rates speed up cleaning but risk damage. 30 kHz recommended for effective contamination removal. Trade-off: high freq increases efficiency but elevates surface temp. Source: Academic literature, Appl. Surf. Sci. (2020) on pulsed laser cleaning of minerals.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.8 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below alabaster's damage threshold (~2 J/cm² for gypsum) for safe cleaning; lower for light contamination. 0.8 J/cm² recommended to ablate contaminants without melting. Conservative due to alabaster's poor thermal conductivity. Source: Ablation threshold studies in J. Appl. Phys. (2017) on calcium sulfate materials.
