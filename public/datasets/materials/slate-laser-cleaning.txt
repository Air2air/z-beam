power:

  Min:    50 W
  Max:    500 W
  Note:   Typical range for Nd:YAG laser cleaning of stone materials like slate to remove contaminants without thermal damage; starting at 200W for controlled ablation. Max safe ~500W to avoid cracking. Source: Manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics (e.g., YLR series for stone restoration) and academic paper by S. J. H. Lee et al. (Opt. Lasers Eng., 2018).

scan_speed:
  Value:  1500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for 1064 nm cleaning of slate; slower (500-1000 mm/s) for heavy contamination like rust/moss, faster (2000-3000 mm/s) for light dirt. Recommended 1500 mm/s for balanced efficiency and minimal heat input. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Europe guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) preferred for slate to ablate contaminants via photothermal effects with low heat-affected zone; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier applications. 20 ns recommended to minimize thermal diffusion in low-conductivity slate. Source: Laser physics research by P. Psyllaki et al. (J. Cult. Herit., 2009).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; higher rates (50-100 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation on reflective slate surfaces. 50 kHz balances speed and safety for contamination removal. Trade-off: High freq increases throughput but requires faster scan speeds. Source: Academic literature, e.g., R. N. Raman et al. (Appl. Surf. Sci., 2017).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe cleaning of slate at 1064 nm; damage threshold ~2-3 J/cm² due to slate's moderate absorption and low thermal conductivity (~1-2 W/m·K). Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for contaminant ablation without substrate etching. Source: Ablation threshold studies by A. Pouli et al. (Appl. Phys. A, 2010).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter 100-500 μm; smaller (100-200 μm) for fine surface preparation on slate, larger (300-500 μm) for broad area cleaning. 200 μm recommended for uniform contaminant removal on textured slate surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent Inc. (Arma series) and industry case studies.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers; 2 passes recommended for complete removal on slate without over-ablation, allowing cooling between passes. Source: Industry practice from stone restoration case studies (e.g., European Laser Cleaning Association reports, 2021).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-80% overlap ensures uniform coverage; 60% recommended for slate to avoid striping while preventing excessive heat buildup on low-reflectivity contaminants. Source: Process optimization studies by M. Autric et al. (Proc. SPIE, 2015).
