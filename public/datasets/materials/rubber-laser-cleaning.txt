Source: Laser physics in Applied Surface Science (2019) on polymer cleaning thresholds; ns pulses avoid melting unlike longer durations.

  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Moderate rates for effective contaminant removal without excessive heat buildup in rubber; higher rates (>50 kHz) speed up process but risk accumulation. Trade-off noted in literature. Source: Academic literature in Lasers in Manufacturing and Materials Processing (2021); typical for 1064 nm fiber lasers from Coherent specs.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Low fluence to stay below rubber damage threshold (~0.5-1 J/cm² for ablation onset); 0.1-0.5 J/cm² for light contaminants, up to 1 J/cm² for heavy. Conservative for safe cleaning. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017) on elastomers; uncertainty noted for specific rubber types due to variability in composition.

Source: Industry practice in case studies from Rubber & Plastics News (2020); multi-pass approaches in laser cleaning guidelines from SPIE proceedings.

Source: Process optimization studies in Optics and Laser Technology (2019); typical range from manufacturer guidelines like those from CleanLaser.
