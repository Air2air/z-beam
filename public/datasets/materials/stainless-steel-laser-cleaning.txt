  Note:   Nanosecond regime typical for cost-effective industrial cleaning of SS; picosecond (0.001-1 ns equivalent) or femtosecond for ultra-low HAZ but less common. 50 ns recommended to reduce thermal effects while ablating contaminants. repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Standard for pulsed cleaning; higher rates (>100 kHz) speed up process but risk heat accumulation on reflective SS. 50 kHz balances speed and thermal management. energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of contaminants; SS damage threshold ~4 J/cm² for ns pulses at 1064 nm due to high thermal conductivity. 1.5 J/cm² recommended for effective cleaning without substrate pitting. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017).

  Max:    500 μm

  Max:    10 passes
