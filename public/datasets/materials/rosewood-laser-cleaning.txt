  Value:  15 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for industrial cleaning to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ) on organic materials like rosewood; picosecond/femtosecond for ultra-precision but costlier. 15 ns balances ablation and thermal control.   Value:  20 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Lower rates reduce heat accumulation in low-conductivity rosewood; higher rates speed up cleaning but increase burn risk. 20 kHz recommended for effective pulsed cleaning. Source: Academic literature on wood laser ablation (Applied Surface Science, 2020).

  Value:  1 J/cm²

  Note:   Fluence below rosewood damage threshold (~3 J/cm² at 1064 nm) for safe contaminant removal; conservative for reflectivity (~10-20% at NIR). Source: Ablation threshold studies on hardwoods (Journal of Applied Physics, 2017).
