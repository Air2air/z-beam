scan_speed:
  Value:  2000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contaminant removal without excessive heat buildup; slower for heavy contamination (500-1000 mm/s), faster for light (3000-5000 mm/s). Recommended 2000 mm/s for optimal balance on alumina. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Association guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) preferred for cost-effective cleaning; picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-minimal HAZ but less common industrially. 20 ns recommended to limit thermal diffusion in low-conductivity alumina. Source: Laser physics research in Opt. Express by Colombier et al. (2012) on ceramic ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical 10-100 kHz for pulsed cleaning; higher rates increase speed but risk heat accumulation on alumina (trade-off: >50 kHz may cause microcracks). 30 kHz recommended for safe operation. Source: Academic literature in Appl. Surf. Sci. by Lorenz et al. (2009) on repetition rate effects in oxide cleaning.

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant ablation without substrate damage; alumina damage threshold at 1064 nm ~2-4 J/cm². 1.5 J/cm² recommended for safe cleaning of light contaminants. Source: Ablation threshold studies in J. Phys. D: Appl. Phys. by Autrique et al. (2013) for ceramics at IR wavelengths.

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for precision (50-200 μm for fine details) vs. area coverage (200-500 μm); 200 μm recommended for uniform cleaning on alumina surfaces. Source: Equipment specifications from Trumpf and SPI Lasers for scanning heads in industrial cleaning.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers; 2 passes recommended for complete removal on alumina without risking brittleness-induced damage. Source: Industry practice in case studies from Laser Institute of America reports on ceramic surface preparation.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   30-80% ensures uniform coverage; 60% recommended to avoid gaps or overexposure on reflective alumina (reflectivity ~20-40% at 1064 nm). Source: Process optimization studies in Surf. Coat. Technol. by Kumar et al. (2018) on scan overlap for laser cleaning.
