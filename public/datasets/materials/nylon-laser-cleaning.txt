power:
  Value:  30 W
  Min:    10 W
  Max:    100 W
  Note:   Low to moderate power range for heat-sensitive polymers like Nylon to prevent melting (melting point ~220°C); typical for 1064 nm fiber lasers in industrial cleaning. Recommended starting value balances efficiency and safety. Source: Manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics and Coherent for polymer surface preparation; academic review in 'Journal of Laser Applications' (2020).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Slower speeds ensure controlled energy delivery on low thermal conductivity Nylon (~0.25 W/mK) to avoid heat accumulation; higher speeds (up to 2000 mm/s) for light contamination, lower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy. Recommended for optimal contaminant removal without substrate damage. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Association guidelines and equipment manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred over picosecond/femtosecond for cost-effective 1064 nm cleaning of Nylon, minimizing heat-affected zone (HAZ) via rapid ablation of contaminants; shorter pulses (<20 ns) reduce thermal diffusion in polymers. Picosecond (10-100 ps) for ultra-precise applications but less common at this wavelength. Source: Laser physics research in 'Applied Surface Science' (2018) on polymer ablation thresholds.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  50 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Moderate rates for Nylon cleaning to achieve fast processing while limiting heat buildup; higher rates (>50 kHz) increase speed but risk thermal accumulation on low-conductivity substrates, lower for heavy contamination. Trade-off: efficiency vs. HAZ control. Source: Academic literature in 'Optics and Lasers in Engineering' (2019) on pulsed laser cleaning of polymers.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence below Nylon's damage threshold (~1.2-1.5 J/cm² at 1064 nm due to moderate IR absorption) for safe contaminant removal; lower for light soils (0.1-0.3 J/cm²), higher for heavy (0.5-1.0 J/cm²). Recommended value ensures photothermal cleaning without ablation. Source: Ablation threshold studies in 'Polymer Degradation and Stability' (2017) and 'Lasers in Manufacturing' conference proceedings (2021).

spot_size:
  Value:  100 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Smaller spots (50-100 μm) for fine detail cleaning on Nylon surfaces, larger (100-300 μm) for broad area preparation; balances resolution and coverage while controlling fluence on reflective polymers. Recommended for industrial contamination removal. Source: Equipment specifications from SPI Lasers and research in 'Journal of Applied Physics' (2022) on beam profiling for polymers.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Multi-pass approach preferred for Nylon to gradually remove contaminants without exceeding thermal limits; single pass for light cleaning, up to 5 for heavy or uneven surfaces. Recommended minimizes risk of melting. Source: Industry practice from case studies in 'Surface and Coatings Technology' (2020) and manufacturer application notes (e.g., CleanLaser).

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  70 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Higher overlap ensures uniform coverage on irregular Nylon surfaces, preventing gaps in cleaning; 30-50% for large areas, 70-80% for precision. Recommended for even energy distribution and minimal HAZ. Source: Process optimization studies in 'Procedia Manufacturing' (2019) on scan strategies for laser cleaning of polymers.
