scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for 1064 nm cleaning of oak; slower for heavy contamination (e.g., mold or grease) to ensure removal, faster for light dust; 500 mm/s optimal for balancing efficiency and heat control. Source: Industry manuals from IPG Photonics and Adapt Laser Systems.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for industrial cleaning of oak to minimize heat-affected zone (HAZ <50 μm); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) for ultra-precise but costlier applications; 20 ns balances ablation of contaminants with oak's sensitivity to thermal damage. Source: Laser physics research in Optics & Lasers in Engineering (2018).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for 1064 nm cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for oak to achieve fast coverage without excessive heat accumulation, as higher rates (>50 kHz) risk charring due to oak's low reflectivity (~10-20% at 1064 nm). Trade-off: higher freq speeds process but increases thermal load. Source: Academic literature, Applied Surface Science (2020).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for contaminant removal on wood; oak damage threshold ~3-5 J/cm² (ablation starts at ~1 J/cm² for organics); 1.5 J/cm² safe for cleaning without degrading cellulose structure. Conservative due to oak's melting/decomposition point ~200-300°C. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2017).

spot_size:
  Value:  200 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter for precise industrial cleaning; smaller for fine details on oak surfaces (e.g., engravings), larger for broad areas; 200 μm recommended for uniform contaminant removal without excessive overlap heating. Source: Equipment specs from Coherent and Trumpf lasers.

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass for heavy layers; 2 passes typical for oak to ensure complete removal while monitoring for discoloration. Source: Industry case studies on wood restoration (e.g., Laser Cleaning Conference proceedings, 2019).

overlap_ratio:

  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Ensures uniform coverage in scan patterns; 50% recommended for oak to avoid gaps in cleaning while preventing over-exposure and thermal buildup. Source: Process optimization in Surface and Coatings Technology (2021).
