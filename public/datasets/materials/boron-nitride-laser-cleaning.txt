  Value:  1000 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective range for contamination removal; recommended 1000 mm/s for optimal balance of cleaning efficiency and heat control on BN. Light contamination: 1500-3000 mm/s; heavy: 100-500 mm/s. Source: Industry standards from Laserax and Adapt Laser manuals.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime preferred for industrial cleaning to ablate contaminants with minimal HAZ; 20 ns recommended for BN to leverage its thermal conductivity and reduce heat accumulation. Picosecond (ps) or femtosecond for ultra-precision but less common industrially. Source: Laser physics reviews in Optics & Lasers in Engineering (e.g., studies on ceramic ablation).

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning; 30 kHz recommended for BN to balance speed and prevent heat buildup, as higher rates increase average power and risk substrate damage. Trade-off: High freq accelerates cleaning but accumulates heat in low-absorptivity materials like BN at 1064 nm. Source: Academic literature in Journal of Applied Physics on pulse train effects.

energy_density:
  Value:  0.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe contaminant ablation; damage threshold for BN estimated ~2-5 J/cm² based on ceramic studies, so 0.5 J/cm² recommended to stay below while effective for surface prep. Conservative due to BN's reflectivity at 1064 nm. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Applied Surface Science (e.g., on nitrides and ceramics).

spot_size:
