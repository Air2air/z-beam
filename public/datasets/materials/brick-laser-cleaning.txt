  Max:    1000 W
  Note:   Typical range for 1064 nm Nd:YAG lasers in stone/brick cleaning to remove contaminants without thermal damage; starting at 200W balances efficiency and safety. Source: Manufacturer specs from IPG Photonics (e.g., YLR series for restoration); academic review in 'Laser Cleaning of Stone Surfaces' (Optics & Lasers in Engineering, 2015).

scan_speed:
  Value:  500 mm/s
  Min:    10 mm/s
  Max:    5000 mm/s
  Note:   Effective speeds for contaminant removal on porous brick; slower (100-500 mm/s) for heavy soiling like soot, faster (500-2000 mm/s) for light dust. Recommended 500 mm/s optimizes cleaning rate while minimizing heat buildup. Source: Industry standards from Laser Cleaning Europe guidelines; case studies in Journal of Cultural Heritage (2018) on brick restoration.

pulse_width:
  Value:  20 ns
  Min:    0.1 ns
  Max:    1000 ns
  Note:   Nanosecond regime (5-100 ns) preferred for brick to ablate contaminants via photothermal effects with minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ); picosecond (ps) or femtosecond (fs) reduces HAZ further but increases cost. Recommended 20 ns for balance. Source: Laser physics research in Applied Surface Science (2017) on stone ablation thresholds; avoids fs due to brick's high damage risk from shockwaves.

repetition_rate:
  Value:  30 kHz
  Min:    1 kHz
  Max:    200 kHz
  Note:   Typical for pulsed cleaning to control energy delivery; 10-50 kHz for brick prevents heat accumulation in low-conductivity material, while higher rates speed up process but risk thermal stress. Recommended 30 kHz for optimal throughput. Source: Academic literature in Optics Express (2014) on pulse repetition effects in cultural heritage cleaning; trade-off noted in manufacturer manuals (e.g., Adapt Laser Systems).

energy_density:
  Value:  1.5 J/cm²
  Min:    0.1 J/cm²
  Max:    20 J/cm²
  Note:   Fluence range for safe ablation of organic/inorganic contaminants on brick; damage threshold ~3-5 J/cm² to avoid surface cracking or vitrification. Recommended 1.5 J/cm² for effective cleaning below threshold. Source: Ablation threshold studies in Journal of Applied Physics (2016) on fired clay materials; conservative for brick's porosity and reflectivity (~20-30% at 1064 nm).

spot_size:
  Value:  2000 μm
  Min:    0.1 μm
  Max:    500 μm
  Note:   Beam diameter range for industrial cleaning; smaller (0.5-1 mm) for detailed work, larger (2-5 mm) for broad brick surfaces. Recommended 2 mm for uniform coverage on rough brick texture. Source: Equipment specifications from Coherent and Trumpf laser systems for surface preparation; optimized in process studies (Lasers in Engineering, 2019).

pass_count:
  Value:  2 passes
  Min:    1 passes
  Max:    10 passes
  Note:   Single pass for light contamination, multi-pass (2-5) for heavy layers to ensure complete removal without excessive energy. Recommended 2 passes for brick to achieve uniform results while staying below damage threshold. Source: Industry practice in restoration case studies (e.g., European Commission reports on laser cleaning of masonry, 2020); avoids overexposure on porous surfaces.

overlap_ratio:
  Value:  60 %
  Min:    10 %
  Max:    90 %
  Note:   Overlap ensures uniform coverage on uneven brick; 30-50% for speed, 60-80% for thorough cleaning of pores. Recommended 60% for optimal uniformity without redundancy. Source: Process optimization studies in Applied Optics (2015) on scan strategies for stone; standard in manufacturer guidelines (e.g., Laserax equipment manuals).
