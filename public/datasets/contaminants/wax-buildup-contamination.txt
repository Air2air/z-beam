DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Wax-buildup contamination arises from paraffin, carnauba, and
synthetic deposits in protective coatings and polishes, and it
accumulates layer by layer on surfaces. This buildup,
hydrophobic and soft in texture, distinguishes itself from
greasy oils or dust by even spreading and resistance to
solvents. On metals such as steel and aluminum, it clings
tightly yet vaporizes readily under laser pulse, thus challenges
like incomplete removal diminish, and laser cleaning succeeds
through precise ablation without substrate harm.
