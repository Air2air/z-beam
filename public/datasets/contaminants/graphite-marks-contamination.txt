───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Graphite marks arise from carbon residues left by pencils,
lubricants, or electrical contacts rubbing onto surfaces. These
dark, powdery deposits stand out from rust or oils due to their
conductive, non-adhesive nature that smears easily under
pressure. On metals like steel or aluminum, they cling tightly
yet vaporize cleanly with laser pulses, cutting removal
challenges like residue buildup while preserving the base
material.
