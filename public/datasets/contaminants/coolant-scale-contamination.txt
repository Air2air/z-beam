DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Coolant-scale contamination arises from silicate and phosphate
deposits in engine cooling systems, where coolants degrade
during operation and so minerals precipitate onto surfaces. This
scale exhibits layered hardness and poor adhesion compared to
rust or oil residues, because it forms crystalline structures
that bond tightly yet crack unevenly under stress. On aluminum
and cast iron, scale clings stubbornly and so resists scraping
without surface damage, while on brass it flakes more readily
but leaves residues; removal challenges include avoiding
substrate erosion during mechanical methods, yet laser cleaning
succeeds by vaporizing layers precisely through thermal
expansion, so surfaces restore uniformity without harm.
