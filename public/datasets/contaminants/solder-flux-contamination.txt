DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Solder-flux contamination forms during electronics soldering
operations, as rosin-based and no-clean fluxes deposit resinous
residues on substrates. This layer, it adheres tenaciously to
copper and brass, which distinguishes it from water-soluble
types by its non-volatile, sticky persistence that manifests
under exposure. On PCB surfaces, the contamination exhibits
strong bonding dependent from humidity, posing removal
challenges like chemical resistance, yet laser cleaning
effectively ablates it precisely, without damaging the
underlying materials.
