DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Hydraulic-fluid contamination, it arises from leaks in heavy
machinery, where petroleum-based oils seep gradually under
vibration and pressure, forming sticky deposits that harden into
tenacious oily films. These contaminants, they distinguish
themselves by persistent adhesion that penetrates surfaces,
unlike dry dust or watery residues; on steel, layers build
slowly and tenaciously, aluminum shows quicker pore-deep spread,
while concrete absorbs profoundly, yielding darkened stains.
Removal proves challenging, as solvents fail against embedded
oils and scraping risks damage, yet laser cleaning excels, its
pulses vaporizing films effectively without harming substrates.
