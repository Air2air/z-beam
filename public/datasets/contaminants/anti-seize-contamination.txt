───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

During assembly, anti-seize contamination forms as sticky
metallic and graphite-based deposits so tenacious adhesion
builds up over time from heat and friction on surfaces. This
contamination exhibits heat-resistant properties and strong
bonding on steel, stainless steel, and aluminum, which
distinguishes it from rust or oils because mechanical removal
damages substrates during process. Key challenges occur in
removal due to that bonding, so laser cleaning ablates layers
precisely and base materials remain unharmed after treatment.
