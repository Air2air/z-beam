DESCRIPTION:
Adhesive-residue-contamination, it sticks firmly to surfaces during removal processes. This organic residue forms uneven patches because adhesives break down and leave behind sticky films. In observations, formation happens quickly after peeling, so layers build up irregularly on metals and plastics. During exposure to handling, contamination spreads and adheres tightly, creating patterns that vary by material.

Treatment with laser cleaning faces distinct challenges here. Residue resists ablation because it chars easily and smokes, so complete removal requires careful pulses to avoid damaging the base. On metals, this contamination, it bonds strongly and needs multiple passes for clearance, while on plastics, it softens first and then lifts away. After application of laser, surfaces show cleaner states, but traces still remain in crevices from the data. Process achieves better results on smooth areas, so preparation helps in tackling these patterns.
