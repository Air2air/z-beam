DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Annealing-scale builds up as a thick oxide layer during slow
heating in controlled atmospheres, ramping up deeper than
surface rust and clinging tight to shift conductivity far beyond
lighter contaminants. On steel, it grips firm and alters
properties sharply, stainless steel holds it even tighter, and
titanium shows looser attachment overall, posing significant
challenges since mild acids drag slowly without etching
underneath while blasting scatters debris widely. Turns out,
laser cleaning lines up best, vaporizing the buildup precisely
to achieve a clean finish and keep the base material intact.
