DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Plastic-residue contamination, it manifests as melted, degraded,
or oxidized layers from heat exposure or UV degradation, which
distinguishes itself from inorganic rusts by its viscoelastic,
non-brittle nature that clings irregularly. On metals such as
steel and aluminum, the residue adheres tenaciously, influenced
from surface porosity, while on glass it spreads thinly yet
persistently, leading to challenges in removal due to its
thermal sensitivity and potential for re-melting during
cleaning. Laser cleaning proves effective, as the process
vaporizes the residue selectively, that demonstrates
preservation of the substrate integrity without mechanical
abrasion.
