DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Efflorescence appears as white, crystalline salt deposits that
form when moisture draws soluble salts through porous materials
like concrete or brick, crystallizing on the surface as water
evaporates. This contamination sets it apart from dirt or algae
by its powdery, water-soluble nature that flakes easily yet
recurs without addressing underlying moisture issues, behaving
more aggressively on absorbent stones such as limestone or
sandstone where it embeds deeply. Removal poses a solid
challenge due to the risk of substrate damage from abrasive
methods, but laser cleaning addresses this effectively by
vaporizing the salts layer by layer while preserving the
material's integrity.
