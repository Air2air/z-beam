───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Adhesive residue contamination forms from sticky organic films
left on surfaces after tape or label removal from manufactured
items and shipped goods. During handling, it re-adheres easily,
so this differs from oily residues because strong bonding occurs
on metals like steel and aluminum, while blending happens into
plastics such as ABS. Mechanical removal causes re-sticking
challenges, but laser cleaning vaporizes organics selectively
and leaves substrates undamaged.
