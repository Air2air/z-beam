DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rubber-residue contamination arises from vulcanized rubber
deposits of tire marks, seals, and gaskets, so it forms during
friction and compression on surfaces. This residue shows unique
elasticity and strong adhesion that distinguish it from oily or
powdery contaminants because of its polymeric nature. On
materials like steel or concrete, residue clings tightly and
resists scraping, so removal challenges include incomplete
cleaning without damaging substrate, yet laser cleaning succeeds
by vaporizing layers precisely during pulsed exposure.
