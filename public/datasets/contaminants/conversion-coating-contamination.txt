DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Conversion coatings, they form through chemical reactions on metal surfaces, creating thin protective layers from phosphates or chromates. This contamination, it manifests tenaciously, distinguished by its crystalline structure that embeds deeply, unlike looser rust or scale. On steel and aluminum, it adheres strongly, while on zinc, it seems that flaking occurs more readily; removal challenges arise from this bonding, yet laser cleaning effectively vaporizes it without substrate damage, influenced from precise energy control.
