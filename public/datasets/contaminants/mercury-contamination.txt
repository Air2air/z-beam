DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Mercury contamination forms from elemental spills or amalgam
residues in industrial settings. Before cleaning, liquid films
spread quickly on concrete and tile, so vapors release easily
during handling and this volatility distinguishes it from solid
pollutants. On metals such as copper, steel, and aluminum,
mercury alloys and penetrates deeply, so adhesion strengthens
and removal challenges include toxicity and residue scattering;
laser cleaning vaporizes contamination precisely without
substrate harm, so after treatment surfaces exhibit effective
cleanliness.
