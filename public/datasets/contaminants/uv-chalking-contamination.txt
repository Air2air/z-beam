DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

UV-chalking contamination, it arises from photodegradation under
ultraviolet exposure, where polymer chains fragment on the
surface and thus form a white chalky residue that scatters and
adheres as a powdery film. This residue distinguishes itself
from dust or oxidation marks through its brittle texture that
flakes easily under touch and pure white matte color,
contrasting with sticky grime or yellowish patina, while on
materials like ABS and polycarbonate it builds thickly and
cracks during flexing, yet on nylon and polypropylene it
develops sparse spots that roughen texture over time, so removal
challenges include embedding into pores that worsens coverage
with mechanical wiping or chemical dissolution damaging the
base. Laser cleaning addresses these issues effectively, as
pulses target the residue selectively and vaporize it without
heat spread to the substrate, thus restoring surface uniformity
already on delicate layers like acrylic or redwood.
