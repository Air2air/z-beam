───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Thermal paste contamination arises from silicone or metal-based
materials used in CPU cooling systems and it forms when paste
spreads beyond interfaces during assembly or operation. This
contaminant shows unique greasy texture and strong adhesion so
it differs from dry dust or oxide layers by resisting simple
wiping. On copper and aluminum surfaces, it hardens over time
and removal challenges include residue buildup because paste
bonds tightly. Laser cleaning works effectively since pulses
vaporize paste without harming underlying nickel-plated areas.
