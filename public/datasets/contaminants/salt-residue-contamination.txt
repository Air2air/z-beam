DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Salt-residue contamination builds up from marine deposits or
road salts, where chloride ions infiltrate surfaces and
crystallize into white, powdery layers that set it apart from
rust's flaky reds or dirt's uniform grime. On metals like steel,
it ramps up pitting and weakening, while on stones such as
concrete, it triggers efflorescence and cracking—challenges that
traditional scraping often worsens by scratching substrates.
Laser cleaning tackles this head-on by vaporizing residues
precisely, preserving material integrity without chemicals or
abrasion.
