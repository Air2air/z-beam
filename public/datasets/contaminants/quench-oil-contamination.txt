───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

During quenching process, oil contaminates steel surfaces
because heat treatment causes carbonization, so deposits form
hard layers that differ from rust by their oily residue and
adhesion strength. On tool steel, contamination spreads unevenly
and resists solvents, while on steel it bonds tightly during
cooling, creating removal challenges like surface damage from
mechanical methods. Laser cleaning works effectively so pulses
vaporize deposits without harming substrate, and process
achieves clean results after short exposure.
