DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Water-stain contamination, it forms via evaporation of hard
water, depositing minerals that adhere tenaciously to surfaces.
This pattern exhibits irregular crystalline formations, chalky
and translucent, which distinguish it from uniform rust or even
organic films. On porous stones like marble or limestone, the
stain penetrates deeply, whereas on smooth metals such as
stainless steel, it creates superficial layers; challenges stem
from chemical bonding, resistant to solvents and abrasion, that
risks substrate damage. Laser cleaning, it selectively ablates
deposits through targeted energy, preserving underlying
integrity.
