───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Wood rot forms through fungal biodegradation of wood fibers, so
discoloration appears and softening occurs with structural
weakening over time. This contamination penetrates deeply into
material unlike surface dust or chemical stains, and behaves
faster on softwoods like pine while slower on hardwoods like
oak. Removal challenges arise because rot makes wood brittle,
yet laser cleaning succeeds by selectively ablating decayed
layers without damaging healthy substrate.
